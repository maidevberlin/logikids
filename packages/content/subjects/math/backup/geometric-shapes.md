# Geometric Shapes and Properties Tasks

Create mathematics problems that explore properties of 2D shapes including triangles, quadrilaterals, and circles. Problems should help students identify and classify shapes, apply angle relationships, and calculate perimeter and area.

**Vary the problem structure:**
- **Triangle classification**: Given side lengths or angles, determine triangle type (equilateral, isosceles, scalene, right, acute, obtuse)
- **Angle sum in triangles**: Calculate missing angles using the fact that interior angles sum to 180°
- **Exterior angles**: Apply the exterior angle theorem (exterior angle equals sum of two non-adjacent interior angles)
- **Quadrilateral properties**: Identify quadrilaterals (parallelogram, rectangle, rhombus, square, trapezoid, kite) based on given properties (parallel sides, equal sides, right angles, diagonals)
- **Angle sum in polygons**: Calculate sum of interior angles using $(n-2) \times 180°$ for n-sided polygons
- **Perimeter calculations**: Calculate perimeter of triangles, rectangles, squares, or composite figures by adding all side lengths
- **Area calculations**: Calculate area of rectangles $(l \times w)$, squares $(s^2)$, triangles $(\frac{1}{2} \times b \times h)$, parallelograms $(b \times h)$, trapezoids $(\frac{1}{2}(a+b) \times h)$
- **Circle properties**: Calculate circumference $(C = 2\pi r)$ and area $(A = \pi r^2)$ given radius or diameter
- **Composite figures**: Break down complex shapes into simpler ones and calculate total area or perimeter

**Vary the content/context:**
- **Architecture**: Floor plans, room dimensions, window shapes, roof designs
- **Land measurement**: Garden plots, farm fields, property boundaries, park layouts
- **Art and design**: Logo design, mosaic patterns, quilt designs, geometric art
- **Construction**: Building materials, tile layouts, fence planning, paving stones
- **Nature**: Honeycomb patterns, leaf shapes, flower petals, snowflake symmetry
- **Sports**: Playing field dimensions, court markings, goal areas

**Vary the complexity based on age:**
- **For younger ages** ({{age}} < 13): Basic shape identification, simple perimeter and area with whole numbers, triangles and common quadrilaterals only, clear diagrams with all measurements labeled
- **For middle ages** ({{age}} 13-14): Classify shapes based on properties, calculate missing angles, work with composite figures (2-3 simple shapes), use decimal measurements, apply multiple formulas in one problem
- **For older ages** ({{age}} >= 15): Complex composite figures, work backwards from area to find dimensions, apply properties to prove shape types, combine angle relationships with algebraic expressions

**Adjust difficulty with {{difficulty}}:**
- **Easy**: Single shape, all measurements given, one formula application, whole numbers, basic shapes (triangle, rectangle, square, circle)
- **Medium**: Multiple shapes or steps, calculate one missing measurement first, decimal values, special quadrilaterals (parallelogram, trapezoid), composite figures with 2 shapes
- **Hard**: Complex composite figures (3+ shapes), work backwards from area to dimensions, prove shape properties using angle relationships, algebraic expressions for side lengths

**Include variety in numerical values:**
- Different side lengths: 3 cm, 7.5 m, 12 mm, 4.2 km
- Various angles: 30°, 45°, 60°, 72°, 108°
- Different radii: 5 cm, 8.5 m, 3.2 dm
- Number of polygon sides: 5, 6, 8, 10, 12
- Composite figure dimensions: ensure different combinations each time
- Use realistic values appropriate to context (rooms in meters, gardens in meters or kilometers, logos in centimeters)
