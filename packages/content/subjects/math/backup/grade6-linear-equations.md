# Linear Equations

Create diverse problems for solving linear equations using equivalent transformations, including setting up equations from word problems and checking solutions.

   $${{coefficient}}x + {{constant1}} - {{constant1}} = {{constant2}} - {{constant1}}$$

### 2. Two-Step Equations - Multiplication/Division First

**Process:**

3. Verify the solution

### 7. Number Problem

### 8. Perimeter Problem
A rectangle has length ${{length}}$ cm and width $w$ cm. The perimeter is ${{perimeter}}$ cm.

a) Write an equation using the perimeter formula: $P = 2(l + w)$
b) Substitute known values: ${{perimeter}} = 2({{length}} + w)$
c) Solve for $w$
d) Verify by calculating the perimeter with your answer

**Perimeter formula:**
$$P = 2(l + w) = 2l + 2w$$

### 9. Distribution and Collection
Solve: ${{a}}(x - {{b}}) + {{c}} = {{d}}(x + {{e}})$

**Detailed solution:**
1. Distribute on both sides
   - Left side: ${{a}}(x - {{b}}) = {{a}}x - ?$

2. Rewrite equation: ${{a}}x - ? + {{c}} = {{d}}x + ?$
3. Simplify both sides
4. Collect $x$ terms on one side, constants on the other
5. Solve for $x$

### 10. Mixed Operations with Fractions
Solve: $\frac{{{num}}x + {{const1}}}{{{denom}}} = {{const2}}$

**Strategy:**
1. Multiply both sides by {{denom}} to eliminate the fraction

3. Check: substitute your answer back

### 12. Consecutive Numbers Problem
The sum of three consecutive numbers is {{sum}}.

a) Let $x$ be the first number. Write expressions for the second and third numbers
b) Write an equation for their sum
c) Solve for $x$
d) What are the three consecutive numbers?
e) Verify that their sum equals {{sum}}

**Equation setup:**
If first number is $x$, then:
- Second number: $x + 1$
- Third number: $x + 2$
- Sum: $x + (x + 1) + (x + 2) = {{sum}}$

## Task Requirements
- Include a variety of equation types (two-step, variables on both sides, with parentheses)
- Provide at least 2-3 word problems requiring students to set up equations
- Always require students to check their solutions
- Show equivalent transformations clearly at each step
- Use varied coefficients and constants (including negative numbers)
- Include at least one real-world context (age, money, distance, perimeter)
- Emphasize the "balance" concept: what you do to one side, do to the other
- Ensure significantly different numerical values across task generations
