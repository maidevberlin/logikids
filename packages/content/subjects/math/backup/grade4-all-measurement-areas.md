# All Measurement Areas

Generate tasks that integrate length, mass, volume, and time measurements, focusing on conversions between units and applying measurements in real-world contexts.

## Task Variations

### 1. Length Conversions
Convert between metric length units:
- **Basic Conversions**: "{{value}} {{unit1}} = ___ {{unit2}}"
- **Multi-Step**: "Convert {{value}} km to meters, then to centimeters"
- **Mixed Units**: "{{larger}} {{unit1}} {{smaller}} {{unit2}} = ___ {{unit2}} total"

Conversion facts:
- $1 \text{ km} = 1000 \text{ m}$
- $1 \text{ m} = 100 \text{ cm}$
- $1 \text{ cm} = 10 \text{ mm}$

Examples:
- $3.5 \text{ km} = 3500 \text{ m} = 350000 \text{ cm}$
- $2 \text{ m } 45 \text{ cm} = 245 \text{ cm}$
- $1500 \text{ mm} = 150 \text{ cm} = 1.5 \text{ m}$

### 2. Mass Conversions
Work with metric mass units:
- **Kilograms to Grams**: "{{kg}} kg = ___ g"
- **Mixed Masses**: "{{kg}} kg {{g}} g = ___ g total"
- **Grams to Kilograms**: "{{grams}} g = ___ kg ___ g"
- **Tonnes**: "{{tonnes}} tonnes = ___ kg" (1 tonne = 1000 kg)

Examples:
- $3 \text{ kg } 250 \text{ g} = 3250 \text{ g}$
- $4500 \text{ g} = 4 \text{ kg } 500 \text{ g}$
- $2.5 \text{ tonnes} = 2500 \text{ kg}$

### 3. Volume Conversions
Convert between liquid volume units:
- **Liters to Milliliters**: "{{liters}} l {{ml}} ml = ___ ml"
- **Milliliters to Liters**: "{{ml}} ml = ___ l ___ ml"
- **Decimal Forms**: "{{decimal}} liters = ___ ml"

Examples:
- $2.75 \text{ l} = 2750 \text{ ml}$
- $3 \text{ l } 400 \text{ ml} = 3400 \text{ ml}$
- $5600 \text{ ml} = 5 \text{ l } 600 \text{ ml} = 5.6 \text{ l}$

### 4. Mixed Measurement Problems
Combine different measurement types:
- **Multiple Measurements**: "Convert: {{length}}, {{mass}}, and {{volume}}"
- **Comparison Across Types**: "Is {{measurement1}} larger relative to its unit than {{measurement2}}?"
- **Real Scenarios**: Problems involving length AND mass AND volume

Example:
"Package dimensions: 1.5 m × 80 cm × 50 cm, Mass: 3 kg 750 g, Capacity: 2.5 l. Convert all to smallest units."

### 5. Addition and Subtraction with Conversions
Calculate with measurements requiring conversion:
- **Same Type, Different Units**: "{{val1}} {{unit1}} + {{val2}} {{unit2}} = ___"
- **Subtraction**: "{{larger}} - {{smaller}} = ___" (different units)
- **Multi-Step**: Add/subtract three or more measurements

Examples:
- $2 \text{ km } 500 \text{ m} + 1800 \text{ m} = 2500 \text{ m} + 1800 \text{ m} = 4300 \text{ m} = 4.3 \text{ km}$
- $5 \text{ kg} - 1750 \text{ g} = 5000 \text{ g} - 1750 \text{ g} = 3250 \text{ g} = 3 \text{ kg } 250 \text{ g}$

### 6. Estimation and Reasonableness
Determine appropriate measurements:
- **Best Estimate**: "Which is the best estimate for {{object}}: {{opt1}}, {{opt2}}, or {{opt3}}?"
- **Reasonable Check**: "Is {{measurement}} a reasonable {{type}} for a {{object}}?"
- **Order of Magnitude**: "About how many {{small_unit}} in a {{large_unit}}?"

Common benchmarks:
- Door height: ~2 m
- Person mass: 30-80 kg
- Car gas tank: 40-60 l
- Book mass: 200-500 g
- Pencil length: 15-20 cm

### 7. Word Problems with Multiple Units
Apply conversions to real scenarios:
- **Shopping**: "Fabric costs {{price}} per meter. You need {{length}} cm. How many meters do you buy? Total cost?"
- **Cooking**: "Recipe needs {{amount1}} {{unit}}. You have {{amount2}} {{different_unit}}. Enough?"
- **Travel**: "Trip is {{distance}} km. You've driven {{partial}} m. Distance remaining?"

Example:
"Ribbon costs €2 per meter. You need 350 cm. Buy 4 m (minimum). Cost: €8. Leftover: 4 m - 3.5 m = 0.5 m = 50 cm"

### 8. Conversion Tables and Patterns
Work with systematic conversions:
- **Complete Table**: "Fill in missing values in this conversion table"
- **Pattern Recognition**: "What pattern do you see when converting {{unit1}} to {{unit2}}?"
- **Using Tables**: "Use this table to convert {{value}} {{unit}}"

| Kilograms | Grams |
|-----------|-------|
| 1         | 1000  |
| 2.5       | 2500  |
| 3.75      | 3750  |
| ?         | 4200  |
| 5.5       | ?     |

## Answer Requirements

Provide solutions that:
- Show conversion factors explicitly: "$(1 \text{ km} = 1000 \text{ m})$"
- Demonstrate step-by-step conversions for multi-step problems
- Express answers in appropriate units (simplify or convert as needed)
- Include estimation reasoning for real-world contexts
- Use tables to organize complex problems with multiple conversions:

| Measurement | Original | Conversion | Result |
|-------------|----------|------------|--------|
| Length      | 2.5 km   | ×1000      | 2500 m |
| Mass        | 3 kg 250 g | +3000+250 | 3250 g |
| Volume      | 1.5 l    | ×1000      | 1500 ml|

- Provide both decimal and mixed unit forms when appropriate
- Explain when to use which units (km for long distances, cm for small objects, etc.)
