---
id: grade2-numbers-to-100
name: Numbers to 100
description: Understanding place value with tens and ones, reading, writing, comparing numbers to 100, using hundred chart and number line
grade: 2
focus: Place value and number sense
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Understand place value concept with tens and ones for numbers to 100
  - Read and write numbers to 100 in digits and words
  - Compare and order numbers to 100 using greater than, less than, and equal to
  - Identify patterns and relationships on a hundred chart
  - Locate and compare numbers on a number line to 100
  - Count forward and backward by ones, twos, fives, and tens within 100
  - Recognize and continue number sequences with different step sizes
problem_types:
  - Place value decomposition
  - Number comparison
  - Hundred chart patterns
  - Number line positioning
  - Skip counting
  - Number sequencing
  - Missing number
  - Ordering numbers
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Numbers up to 50, simple comparisons
    - Skip counting by tens or fives with clear starting points
    - Hundred chart with highlighted patterns or visual support
  medium:
    - Numbers up to 100, multiple comparisons
    - Skip counting by twos or mixed patterns
    - Number line tasks requiring estimation or mental calculation
  hard:
    - Three or more numbers to order
    - Complex sequences with varying steps
    - Hundred chart patterns requiring pattern recognition across rows/columns
prerequisites:
  - grade1-addition-subtraction-to-20
real_world_context:
  - Counting large collections like coins in a piggy bank or stickers in an album
  - Reading house numbers, bus numbers, or page numbers in books
  - Understanding scores in games that go beyond 20
  - Organizing items by size or quantity in groups of tens
  - Reading price tags and comparing costs at a store
  - A knight counting soldiers lined up in rows of ten for a castle defense
  - Tracking daily steps on a pedometer during a school fitness challenge
  - Numbering seats in a theater or classroom for assigned seating
  - Counting beads on an abacus to understand tens and ones
  - A train conductor counting passengers in different carriages
  - Reading jersey numbers at a soccer match or sports event
  - Organizing a stamp or trading card collection by number
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid numbers larger than 100
  - Don't use negative numbers
  - Avoid fractions or decimals - not appropriate for Grade 2
  - Don't require written addition or subtraction - focus on number sense
  - Avoid skip counting by steps larger than 10 or unusual increments
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
