focus: Area formulas and surface area calculations
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Derive the area formula for parallelograms through transformation to rectangles
  - Derive the area formula for triangles by relating them to parallelograms or rectangles
  - Derive the area formula for trapezoids using decomposition into known shapes
  - Identify and measure the relevant base and height dimensions for each shape
  - Apply area formulas flexibly to solve problems with different orientations
  - Calculate surface areas of cubes and cuboids by summing face areas
  - Use area formulas to compare and contrast different geometric figures
problem_types:
  - Parallelogram area
  - Triangle area
  - Trapezoid area
  - Identifying dimensions
  - Formula derivation
  - Surface area of cuboids
  - Shape comparison
  - Composite shapes
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Direct formula application with clearly labeled base and height
    - Simple whole number dimensions (under 20)
    - Standard orientations with horizontal base
  medium:
    - Identifying which measurements to use when multiple are shown
    - Shapes in non-standard orientations
    - Surface area with dimensions requiring calculation of opposite faces
  hard:
    - Decomposing irregular shapes into parallelograms, triangles, and trapezoids
    - Problems requiring multiple formula applications
    - Argumentative comparisons of areas (proving which is larger without calculating)
