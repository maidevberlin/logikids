---
id: grade3-money-advanced
name: Advanced Money Skills
description: Working with amounts up to 1000 Euro, decimal notation, larger bills, multi-step shopping problems, and budget planning
grade: 3
focus: Money and financial calculations
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Use decimal notation correctly for money amounts (€ with comma or period)
  - Work with bills up to 500€ (100€, 200€, 500€ notes)
  - Calculate total costs for 3 or more items in shopping scenarios
  - Determine correct change from amounts up to 1000€
  - Compare prices and make budget-conscious shopping decisions
  - Convert fluently between euros (€) and cents (ct) for amounts up to 1000€
problem_types:
  - Multi-item shopping
  - Change calculation
  - Budget planning
  - Price comparison
  - Decimal notation conversion
  - Bill and coin combinations
  - Shopping with constraints
  - Cost estimation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Amounts up to 100€ with 2-3 items
    - Simple decimal notation (whole euros or .50 amounts)
    - Change from 50€ or 100€ bills
  medium:
    - Amounts up to 500€ with 3-4 items
    - Mixed decimal amounts (e.g., 12.75€, 234.90€)
    - Change calculation requiring multiple bills
  hard:
    - Amounts up to 1000€ with 4-5 items
    - Budget constraints with multiple shopping decisions
    - Optimization problems (best value, staying within budget)
prerequisites:
  - grade2-money-and-currency
  - grade3-written-addition-and-subtraction
  - grade3-numbers-to-1000
real_world_context:
  - Planning a shopping trip at the supermarket with a family budget
  - Saving money to buy a bicycle or video game by comparing prices
  - Helping parents calculate costs when buying multiple items at the store
  - Planning a class trip budget for transportation and entrance fees
  - Comparing product prices to find the best deal or value
  - A young entrepreneur calculating profits from a lemonade stand
  - Budgeting pocket money for a week of school lunches and snacks
  - Comparing prices of different brands at a toy store
  - Planning expenses for a birthday party with decorations and cake
  - Calculating total cost of school supplies from a shopping list
  - A pet owner budgeting for food, treats, and toys
  - Figuring out change when paying with large bills at a market
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid amounts above 1000€ (beyond grade 3 scope)
  - Don't use coins smaller than 1 cent or fractional cents
  - Avoid scenarios requiring multiplication or division
  - Don't introduce percentages or discounts (grade 4+ topic)
  - Avoid unrealistic prices that don't match actual store items
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
