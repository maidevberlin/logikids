# Relative Frequency

Create diverse problems involving absolute and relative frequency, converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages, and representing data in diagrams.

## Problem Variations

### 1. Basic Relative Frequency - Class Survey
In a class of {{total_students}} students, {{count}} students {{characteristic}}.

a) What is the absolute frequency of students who {{characteristic}}?
b) Calculate the relative frequency as a fraction (simplify if possible)
c) Express the relative frequency as a decimal (round to 2 decimal places)
d) Express the relative frequency as a percentage

**Formula:**
$$\text{Relative Frequency} = \frac{\text{Absolute Frequency}}{\text{Total Number}}$$

### 4. Sports Statistics
A basketball player attempted {{total_shots}} shots during a game:
- Made: {{made}} shots
- Missed: {{missed}} shots

a) Calculate the relative frequency of made shots (shooting percentage)
b) Express as a fraction, decimal (3 decimal places), and percentage
c) If the player attempts {{future_shots}} shots in the next game with the same success rate, how many shots would you expect them to make?

**Shooting percentage formula:**
$$\text{Shooting \%} = \frac{\text{Made Shots}}{\text{Total Attempts}} \times 100\%$$

## Task Requirements
- Include conversions between fractions, decimals, and percentages
- Provide at least 2 real-world contexts (surveys, sports, weather, traffic, etc.)
- Emphasize the formula: Relative Frequency = Absolute Frequency รท Total
- Use tables to organize data clearly
- Include questions comparing theoretical probability vs. experimental relative frequency
- Show rounding conventions (typically 2-3 decimal places)
- Verify that all relative frequencies sum to 1 (or 100%)
- Use varied total counts and frequencies across different task generations
- Include at least one visualization request (bar chart, pie chart concept)
