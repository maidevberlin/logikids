---
id: grade4-money-decimals
name: Money and Decimal Notation
description: Understanding euro and cent notation with decimals; adding, subtracting, and multiplying money amounts; making change and calculating totals; comparing prices and converting between euros and cents
grade: 4
focus: Money arithmetic with decimal notation
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Read and write money amounts using decimal notation (€12.50 = 12 Euro 50 Cent)
  - Add and subtract money amounts including regrouping across the decimal point
  - Multiply money amounts by whole numbers in shopping contexts
  - Calculate change and verify totals in purchase scenarios
  - Compare prices and determine best value in multi-item comparisons
  - Convert between euros and cents using place value understanding
  - Solve multi-step word problems involving budgeting and shopping
problem_types:
  - Decimal notation writing
  - Money addition
  - Money subtraction
  - Price multiplication
  - Change calculation
  - Price comparison
  - Euro-cent conversion
  - Shopping budget problems
  - Best value determination
  - Payment verification
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Amounts under €20 with at most two items
    - Addition and subtraction without regrouping across the decimal point
    - Simple conversions (whole euros to cents or vice versa)
  medium:
    - Amounts up to €100 with 3-4 items
    - Operations requiring regrouping across the decimal point
    - Two-step problems (calculate total, then find change)
  hard:
    - Amounts over €100 or multi-step budgeting scenarios
    - Three or more operations in sequence
    - Comparing multiple options to find best value or checking for errors
prerequisites:
  - grade1-money
  - grade4-numbers-to-million
real_world_context:
  - Shopping for groceries, school supplies, or toys with a budget
  - Calculating the total cost of multiple items at a store
  - Determining if you have enough money for a purchase and how much change you'll receive
  - Comparing prices at different stores to find the best deal
  - Saving money over time and tracking progress toward a savings goal
  - Planning a class party budget with decorations and snacks
  - A young entrepreneur pricing items at a garage sale
  - Comparing meal deals at different restaurants
  - Tracking weekly allowance and spending habits
  - Budgeting for holiday gifts for family members
  - Calculating entry fees and snack costs for a day at the zoo
  - Splitting restaurant bills among family members
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid amounts with more than two decimal places
  - Don't use contexts unfamiliar to 9-10 year olds (investments, interest)
  - Avoid ambiguous "best value" without clear comparison criteria
  - Don't mix currencies or use exchange rates
  - Avoid division of money amounts (not in Grade 4 curriculum)
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
