---
id: grade1-money
name: Money
description: Recognizing coins and bills, counting money, and solving simple purchase problems
grade: 1
focus: Money and practical arithmetic
difficulty: easy
learning_objectives:
  - Identify and name Euro coins (1c, 2c, 5c, 10c, 20c, 50c, 1€, 2€)
  - Identify and name Euro bills (5€, 10€)
  - Count and compare amounts of money using coins and bills
  - Determine the total value of a collection of coins or bills
  - Solve simple purchase problems with totals under 20€
  - Make equivalent amounts using different coin combinations
  - Compare which amount is more or less expensive
problem_types:
  - Coin identification
  - Counting coins
  - Counting bills
  - Mixed counting
  - Simple purchase
  - Comparing prices
  - Equivalent amounts
  - Making change
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single coin or bill identification
    - Counting identical coins (e.g., five 1€ coins)
    - Totals under 10€ with maximum 5 coins
  medium:
    - Mixed coins with totals under 20€
    - Simple purchases with exact payment
    - Comparing two prices under 10€
  hard:
    - Multiple coins and bills combined, totals approaching 20€
    - Finding different ways to make the same amount
    - Simple change calculation (from 5€, 10€, or 20€)
real_world_context:
  - Buying snacks, toys, or school supplies at a store
  - Saving money in a piggy bank and counting savings
  - Comparing prices of different items to choose the cheaper option
  - Receiving allowance and understanding coin and bill values
  - Paying for items at a bakery or kiosk
  - A young merchant trading goods at a medieval market fair
  - Counting coins earned from a lemonade stand on a summer day
  - Buying tickets for a carousel ride at an amusement park
  - Collecting coins found in a sandbox or playground treasure hunt
  - Shopping for birthday presents within a budget for a friend
  - Paying for bus or tram fare for a school trip
  - Buying postcards or souvenirs at a museum gift shop
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid totals exceeding 20€
  - Don't use bills larger than 10€ in calculations
  - Avoid complex change scenarios requiring subtraction across tens
  - Don't introduce decimal notation (use cent notation 50c not 0.50€)
  - Avoid scenarios requiring more than 10 individual coins or bills
prerequisites:
  - grade1-addition-subtraction-to-20
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
---
