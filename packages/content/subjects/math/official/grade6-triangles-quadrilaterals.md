ages: [11, 12]
focus: Plane geometry - properties, classification, angle relationships, congruence, and construction

learning_objectives:
  - Classify triangles and quadrilaterals by their properties (sides, angles, symmetry)
  - Apply the angle sum property of triangles (180°) and quadrilaterals (360°)
  - Recognize and construct congruent figures using various geometric criteria
  - Use geometric tools (compass, ruler, protractor, set square) for precise constructions
  - Understand hierarchical relationships between shape families and special cases
  - Investigate symmetry properties (line and rotational symmetry) in polygons
  - Calculate unknown angles using properties of geometric figures
  - Apply triangle and quadrilateral concepts to real-world problem-solving
prerequisites:
  - geometric-shapes-2d
  - area-perimeter
real_world_context: Architectural elements (roof trusses, floor tiles, building frameworks), engineering (bridge supports, triangulated structures), art and design (tessellations, quilting patterns), navigation (triangulation for positioning), construction (setting square angles, creating foundations), athletics (goal posts, court markings), and nature (bee honeycomb structure, crystal formations)
problem_types:
  - Classification tasks
  - Angle calculations
  - Congruence determination
  - Construction tasks
  - Symmetry exploration
  - Property reasoning
  - Comparison tasks
  - Problem-solving applications
  - Error analysis
  - Verification tasks
age_guidelines:
  11:
    - Focus on basic classification of triangles and quadrilaterals by visible properties
    - Simple angle calculations using triangle and quadrilateral angle sum properties
    - Recognition of congruent figures through direct comparison
    - Basic construction with compass and ruler following step-by-step instructions
    - Identification of line symmetry in standard orientations
    - Understanding relationships between simple shape families (square as special rectangle)
    - Calculating one unknown angle when other angles are given directly
    - Real-world contexts with familiar shapes and clear geometric relationships
  12:
    - Advanced classification combining multiple criteria simultaneously
    - Multi-step angle calculations involving parallel lines and transversals
    - Application of congruence criteria (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS) to verify congruence
    - Independent construction design choosing appropriate methods
    - Analysis of both line symmetry and rotational symmetry in non-standard orientations
    - Understanding hierarchical relationships and special cases across quadrilateral families
    - Solving problems requiring intermediate steps and property combinations
    - Real-world problem-solving requiring geometric reasoning and justification
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Direct classification of clearly drawn shapes in standard orientations
    - Finding one missing angle in a triangle or quadrilateral when three angles are given
    - Identifying obvious congruent figures shown in same orientation
    - Basic construction following explicit step-by-step instructions with all measurements provided
    - Counting lines of symmetry in equilateral triangles, squares, or rectangles
    - Recognizing that a square is a rectangle or that opposite sides of parallelograms are equal
    - Simple angle calculations with angles that are multiples of 10
    - Single-step problems with one clear property application
  medium:
    - Classification requiring analysis of multiple properties or non-standard orientations
    - Angle calculations requiring 2-3 steps or application of multiple properties
    - Determining congruence using specific criteria or comparing multiple figures
    - Construction tasks requiring selection of appropriate method from several options
    - Analyzing symmetry in complex or rotated figures
    - Understanding hierarchical relationships between shape families
    - Multi-step problems combining properties from different areas
    - Real-world applications requiring interpretation and geometric reasoning
  hard:
    - Complex classification involving edge cases and hierarchical reasoning
    - Multi-step angle problems with parallel lines, transversals, and composite figures
    - Proving congruence with minimal information or determining what additional information is needed
    - Designing custom constructions to meet specific geometric constraints
    - Analyzing both line and rotational symmetry in irregular or composite shapes
    - Explaining why certain shape combinations are impossible or must have certain properties
    - Problems requiring synthesis of multiple concepts and justification of reasoning
    - Complex real-world scenarios requiring problem decomposition and geometric modeling
