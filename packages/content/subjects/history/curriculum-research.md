# History Curriculum Research

Research on KMK competency framework and Bavarian LehrplanPLUS for Geschichte.

## Key Finding

**No official KMK Bildungsstandards exist for History** in Sekundarstufe I. The KMK only provides:

- EPA (Einheitliche Prüfungsanforderungen) for Abitur
- Kerncurriculum framework with competency model

Individual states define their own curricula. This research uses Bavaria (LehrplanPLUS) as reference.

## KMK Competency Framework

Source: [KMK Kerncurriculum Geschichte](https://www.kmk.org/fileadmin/Dateien/veroeffentlichungen_beschluesse/2010/2010_04_29-Kerncurriculum-Geschichte.pdf)

### Core Competencies (Inner Ring)

| Kompetenz             | Description                                                                                                                                   |
| --------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Sachkompetenz**     | Knowledge of historical terms, concepts, events, persons, processes, structures. Understanding time periods and dating systems.               |
| **Methodenkompetenz** | Interpreting sources (written, visual, material), analyzing historical representations, developing historical arguments, presenting findings. |
| **Urteilskompetenz**  | Forming evidence-based judgments, evaluating historical events and decisions, understanding multiple perspectives.                            |

### Extended Competencies (Outer Ring)

| Kompetenz                  | Description                                                |
| -------------------------- | ---------------------------------------------------------- |
| **Narrative Kompetenz**    | Ability to construct and deconstruct historical narratives |
| **Orientierungskompetenz** | Using historical knowledge for present-day orientation     |

## Grade 6: Von den ersten Menschen bis zu Karl dem Grossen

History begins as a separate subject in grade 6. Focus: Prehistory through Early Middle Ages.

### Lernbereich 1: Der Mensch und seine Geschichte (6 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Time as historical dimension
- Types of historical sources (written, oral, pictorial, material)
- Archaeology and its methods
- Hunters and gatherers (Old Stone Age)
- Farmers and settlers (New Stone Age)

**Key Terms:** Quelle (source), Altsteinzeit, Jungsteinzeit

**Concept Ideas:**

- Understanding historical sources
- Comparing Stone Age periods
- Archaeological methods

---

### Lernbereich 2: Agypten - eine fruhe Hochkultur (6 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Characteristics of early civilizations
- Geographic significance of the Nile
- Pyramids and Egyptian art
- Pharaonic rule and society
- Religion in ancient Egypt

**Key Dates:** ab 3000 v. Chr. - Hochkultur in Agypten

**Key Terms:** Pharao, Monarchie, Pyramide, Hochkultur

**Concept Ideas:**

- Nile and Egyptian civilization
- Pharaonic rule structure
- Egyptian religion and afterlife beliefs

---

### Lernbereich 3: Die griechische Antike (10 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Greek landscape and polis development
- Greek colonization
- Greek culture: myths, epics, Olympics, theater
- Athenian democracy (Solon to Pericles)
- Persian Wars and their influence
- Athens' Golden Age (5th century BC)

**Key Dates:** 5. Jh. v. Chr. - Blutezeit Athens

**Key Terms:** Antike, Kolonisation, Polis, Aristokratie, Demokratie, Verfassung

**Historical Figures:** Solon, Perikles, Themistokles

**Concept Ideas:**

- The Greek polis
- Athenian democracy
- Greek cultural achievements
- Persian Wars

---

### Lernbereich 4: Menschen machen Geschichte (6 hrs) - Langsschnitt

**Topics:**

- Individual impact on history
- Biographical approach to history
- Possibilities and limits of power

**Historical Figures:**

- Female rulers (e.g., Hatshepsut)
- Political strategists (e.g., Themistocles)
- Military commanders (e.g., Alexander the Great)

**Concept Ideas:**

- Alexander the Great's empire
- Hatshepsut as female pharaoh

---

### Lernbereich 5: Das Imperium Romanum (12 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Rome's mythical founding and early history
- Roman Republic: society and power structures
- Transition from Republic to Principate
- Augustus and the Pax Romana
- Life in Roman provinces
- Limes and Romanization
- Jewish diaspora and temple destruction
- Christianity: persecution to state religion

**Key Dates:**

- 753 v. Chr. - mythische Grundung Roms
- 1. Jh. v. Chr. - Ubergang Republik zur Kaiserzeit
- um Christi Geburt - Zeitalter des Augustus

**Key Terms:** Senat, Republik, Diktator, Limes, Judentum, Christentum, Caesar, Romanisierung, Provinz

**Concept Ideas:**

- Roman Republic structure
- Rise of the Roman Empire
- Life in Roman provinces
- Spread of Christianity

---

### Lernbereich 6: Von der Antike zum Mittelalter (8 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Decline and fall of the Roman Empire
- Migration Period (Volkerwanderung)
- Mediterranean tripartition
- Rise of Islam
- Frankish kingdom formation
- Charlemagne's coronation and empire
- Synthesis: Germanic, Christian, Roman traditions

**Key Dates:**

- um 500 - Reichsbildung der Franken
- 800 - Kaiserkronung Karls des Grossen

**Key Terms:** Islam, Mittelalter, Karl der Grosse, Konig, Kaiser

**Concept Ideas:**

- Fall of the Roman Empire
- Charlemagne and the Frankish Empire
- Transition to the Middle Ages

---

### Lernbereich 7: Leben in der Familie (6 hrs) - Langsschnitt

**Topics:**

- Gender roles in ancient societies
- Athenian family structure
- Roman familia
- Comparison with modern families
- Social history perspective

**Concept Ideas:**

- Family life in ancient Rome
- Women's roles in ancient societies

---

## Grade 7: Das Mittelalter und die fruhe Neuzeit

Focus: Medieval period through Absolutism.

### Lernbereich 1: Konig und Reich (7 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Medieval rulership structures
- Otto I and the Ottonian Empire
- Church-state relations
- Investiture Controversy
- Electoral system (Kurfursten)

**Key Dates:** 962 - Otto I / Ottonian Empire

**Key Terms:** Adel, Lehnswesen, Kurfursten, Wahlkonigtum, Reisekonigtum

**Concept Ideas:**

- Medieval kingship
- Feudal system (Lehnswesen)
- Church and state conflict

---

### Lernbereich 2: Leben und Kultur im Mittelalter (10 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Medieval social structure (Stande)
- Grundherrschaft (manorial system)
- Monastic life and institutions
- Staufer period cultural achievements (~1200)
- Medieval cities and urban development
- Romanesque and Gothic architecture
- Challenges: plagues, persecution of Jews

**Key Dates:** um 1200 - Stauferzeit

**Key Terms:** Stande, Grundherrschaft, Kloster, Handel, Stadtrecht, Burger, Reichsstadte, Romanik, Gotik, Ghetto

**Concept Ideas:**

- Medieval social estates
- Life in a medieval monastery
- Medieval cities and trade
- Gothic cathedrals

---

### Lernbereich 3: Neue raumliche und geistige Horizonte (11 hrs)

**Topics:**

- Crusades: religious and military confrontation
- Fall of Constantinople (1453)
- Renaissance and humanism
- Invention of printing press
- Age of Discovery
- Columbus and the "discovery" of America

**Key Dates:**

- 1453 - Eroberung Konstantinopels
- 1492 - Entdeckung Amerikas

**Key Terms:** Kreuzzuge, Renaissance, Humanismus, Buchdruck

**Historical Figures:** Kolumbus

**Concept Ideas:**

- The Crusades
- Renaissance humanism
- Gutenberg and the printing press
- Age of Exploration

---

### Lernbereich 4: Wirtschaft und Handel (6 hrs) - Langsschnitt

**Topics:**

- Roman trade networks
- Medieval commerce (Hanseatic League)
- Early capitalism (Fugger family)
- Comparison with modern global trade

**Key Terms:** Hanse, Fugger

**Concept Ideas:**

- The Hanseatic League
- The Fugger banking dynasty

---

### Lernbereich 5: Das konfessionelle Zeitalter (8 hrs)

**Topics:**

- 16th-century upheaval
- Witch persecution
- Luther and the Reformation (1517)
- Peasants' War (Bauernkrieg)
- Thirty Years' War (1618-1648)
- Peace of Westphalia

**Key Dates:**

- 1517 - Beginn der Reformation
- 1618-1648 - Dreissigjähriger Krieg

**Key Terms:** Reformation, Luther, Bauernkrieg, Westfalischer Friede, Flugblatter

**Concept Ideas:**

- Martin Luther and the Reformation
- The Thirty Years' War
- Peace of Westphalia

---

### Lernbereich 6: Absolutismus und Barock (8 hrs)

**Topics:**

- French absolutism as model
- Government structures under absolutism
- Versailles as symbol of power
- Absolutism in German territories
- Balance of power politics
- Baroque architecture and culture

**Key Terms:** Absolutismus, Versailles, Barock

**Historical Figures:** Ludwig XIV

**Concept Ideas:**

- Absolutism and Louis XIV
- Versailles as power symbol
- Baroque art and architecture

---

### Lernbereich 7: Bauwerke als Ausdruck politischen Denkens (6 hrs) - Langsschnitt

**Topics:**

- Architecture reflecting political ideology
- Greek: Dionysostheater Athens
- Medieval: Kaiserpfalz Aachen
- Baroque: Schloss Schleissheim, Wurzburger Residenz
- Modern: Reichstag, Bundeskanzleramt

**Concept Ideas:**

- Architecture as political expression

---
