---
id: grade6-athenian-democracy
name: Athenian Democracy
description: Explores the development of Athenian democracy from Solon's reforms through Pericles' Golden Age and the constitutional foundations of ancient democratic governance
grade: 6
focus: Constitutional development and democratic governance
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Explain how Solon's reforms laid the foundation for Athenian democracy
  - Describe the key institutions of Athenian democracy (Assembly, Council of 500, courts)
  - Analyze Pericles' contributions to Athenian democracy and its Golden Age
  - Compare Athenian democracy with modern democratic systems
  - Understand the limitations of Athenian democracy (women, slaves, foreigners)
  - Evaluate the role of citizenship in ancient Athens
  - Trace the evolution of Athenian constitution from aristocracy to democracy
problem_types:
  - Constitutional comparison
  - Timeline sequencing
  - Reform analysis
  - Citizenship criteria
  - Institution function
  - Source interpretation
  - Leadership evaluation
  - Democratic limitation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Basic facts about Solon and Pericles as key figures
    - Simple identification of democratic institutions (Assembly, courts)
    - Recognizing who could and could not vote in Athens
  medium:
    - Explaining the purpose of specific reforms (debt cancellation, Council of 400)
    - Comparing Athenian democracy with aristocratic rule
    - Understanding the connection between citizenship and political participation
  hard:
    - Analyzing the tensions between democratic ideals and social exclusion
    - Evaluating long-term impacts of constitutional changes
    - Comparing ancient Athenian democracy with modern representative democracy
prerequisites:
  - grade6-greek-polis
real_world_context:
  - Students simulate an Athenian Assembly meeting to vote on a city proposal
  - A museum exhibit displays pottery shards used as voting ballots in ancient Athens
  - Historians debate whether Athens was truly democratic given its exclusions
  - A theater group reenacts Pericles delivering his famous funeral oration
  - Students compare the rights of an Athenian citizen with their own civic rights
  - Archaeologists uncover inscriptions of Athenian laws from the 5th century BC
  - A documentary explores how Solon's debt reforms helped ordinary farmers
  - Mock trial demonstrates how Athenian jury courts functioned with 500+ jurors
  - Students create a timeline showing Athens's transformation from tyranny to democracy
  - Researchers examine how the lottery system selected Council of 500 members
  - A debate considers why women and slaves were excluded from Athenian democracy
  - History club visits ruins of the Pnyx where the Athenian Assembly met
  - Students compare direct democracy (Athens) with representative democracy (today)
  - A podcast discusses Pericles' building program and the Parthenon's construction
  - Role-play activity shows differences between aristocratic and democratic decision-making
anti_patterns:
  - Don't romanticize Athenian democracy without mentioning its exclusions
  - Avoid anachronistic terms like "political parties" or "elections" for Athens
  - Don't confuse Athenian direct democracy with modern representative systems
  - Avoid presenting Solon and Pericles as the only important democratic reformers
  - Don't assume students understand terms like "constitution" or "assembly"
version: 1
