---
id: grade7-medieval-kingship
name: Medieval Kingship and Feudalism
description: Understand medieval rulership structures, the feudal system, and the development of the Holy Roman Empire under Otto I
grade: 7
focus: Medieval political systems and power structures
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Explain how medieval kings exercised power through the feudal system (Lehnswesen)
  - Describe the relationship between kings, nobles, and vassals in the feudal hierarchy
  - Analyze the significance of Otto I and the foundation of the Ottonian Empire in 962
  - Understand the electoral system (Kurfürsten) and its role in choosing the German king
  - Compare medieval kingship with other forms of rulership studied previously
  - Evaluate the limitations and challenges of medieval royal power
problem_types:
  - Feudal hierarchy diagram
  - Vassal oath interpretation
  - Electoral process analysis
  - Power structure comparison
  - Otto I timeline
  - Rights and obligations matching
  - Royal itinerary explanation
  - Medieval document analysis
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify key figures and basic feudal relationships (lord, vassal, fief)
    - Recognize the date 962 and Otto I's coronation
    - List the seven Kurfürsten
  medium:
    - Explain mutual obligations in feudal relationships
    - Analyze why kings had to travel constantly (Reisekönigstum)
    - Compare elected vs. hereditary kingship
  hard:
    - Evaluate strengths and weaknesses of the feudal system
    - Analyze complex power struggles between king, nobles, and church
    - Assess how the electoral system affected royal authority
real_world_context:
  - A king distributing lands to loyal nobles after winning a battle
  - A vassal knight receiving a fief from his lord in a formal ceremony
  - The seven Kurfürsten gathering to elect a new German king
  - Otto I being crowned Holy Roman Emperor in Rome in 962
  - A traveling king moving his court from palace to palace to govern his realm
  - A noble swearing an oath of loyalty to his lord in exchange for land
  - A dispute between a king and a powerful duke over territorial rights
  - Nobles refusing military service because the king violated feudal obligations
  - A young prince learning he will become king only if the electors choose him
  - A castle serving as both military fortress and administrative center
  - Peasants working the land that their lord received as a fief from the king
  - A medieval document recording the rights and duties between lord and vassal
  - The contrast between a powerful local duke and a weak elected king
  - A king calling his vassals to provide knights for a military campaign
  - The transformation from Charlemagne's inherited empire to an elected kingdom
anti_patterns:
  - Don't oversimplify feudalism as a strict pyramid (it was complex and varied)
  - Avoid presenting all medieval kings as weak or powerless
  - Don't confuse the feudal system with manorial economy (Grundherrschaft)
  - Avoid anachronistic terms like "Germany" for the medieval Holy Roman Empire
  - Don't ignore regional variations in feudal practices across medieval Europe
