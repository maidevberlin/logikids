real_world_context:
  - A peasant family wakes at dawn to work the lord's fields three days per week before tending their own small plot
  - A monastery scribe spends years copying religious manuscripts by candlelight while other monks tend gardens and care for the sick
  - A noble lady manages her husband's estate while he is away at war, overseeing servants and resolving disputes
  - A village blacksmith holds a privileged position among peasants because his skills are essential to the manor
  - A serf dreams of saving enough to buy freedom for his children, knowing he will never escape his bound status
  - During harvest season, all peasants must bring a portion of their grain to the lord's granary as required payment
  - A knight trains from childhood in combat and courtly manners, expected to protect the weak and serve his lord faithfully
  - A merchant traveling between towns must navigate the rigid social hierarchy despite his growing wealth
  - The village church teaches that God ordained the three estates and everyone must accept their divinely assigned position
  - A bishop wields both spiritual authority and political power, living in luxury while preaching humility to peasants
  - Peasants gather in the village common to share tools and support each other through the harsh demands of manorial labor
  - A rare peasant girl enters a convent, finding education and escape from rural poverty through religious life
  - During a famine, nobles feast in their castles while peasants face starvation, revealing the harsh inequality of the system
  - The lord holds court to settle disputes among his peasants, demonstrating his judicial power over their lives
  - A freeman farmer negotiates with the lord to rent additional land, enjoying more rights than bound serfs
