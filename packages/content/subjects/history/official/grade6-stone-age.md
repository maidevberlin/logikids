---
id: grade6-stone-age
name: Life in the Stone Age
description: Compare and contrast life in the Old Stone Age (Paleolithic) and New Stone Age (Neolithic), understanding the transition from hunters and gatherers to farmers and settlers
grade: 6
focus: Prehistoric human societies and the Neolithic Revolution
difficulty: easy
learning_objectives:
  - Describe daily life and survival strategies of hunters and gatherers in the Old Stone Age
  - Explain how farming and settlement changed human societies in the New Stone Age
  - Compare tools, housing, and social organization between Old and New Stone Age periods
  - Understand the causes and effects of the transition from nomadic to settled lifestyles
  - Identify key innovations of the Neolithic Revolution including agriculture and animal domestication
  - Analyze how environmental changes influenced Stone Age human development
  - Recognize archaeological evidence used to understand prehistoric life
problem_types:
  - Comparison questions
  - Timeline ordering
  - Tool identification
  - Cause and effect
  - Daily life scenarios
  - Innovation impact
  - Archaeological interpretation
  - Settlement patterns
  - Survival strategies
  - Cultural development
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Direct comparisons between two clearly different aspects (tools, housing, food sources)
    - Single-step cause-effect relationships (farming leads to permanent settlements)
    - Identification of basic Stone Age innovations visible in images or descriptions
  medium:
    - Multi-factor comparisons requiring consideration of several aspects simultaneously
    - Chains of cause-effect relationships (climate change to agriculture to settlements)
    - Analysis of how specific innovations transformed multiple aspects of daily life
  hard:
    - Complex analysis of interconnected changes across social, economic, and cultural dimensions
    - Evaluation of archaeological evidence requiring inference about incomplete information
    - Synthesis of multiple factors explaining long-term historical transformations
prerequisites:
  - grade6-historical-sources
real_world_context:
  - Students visit a Stone Age archaeological site where they examine reconstructed dwellings and ancient tool fragments
  - A documentary shows how climate change at the end of the Ice Age created conditions for the first agriculture
  - In a museum exhibit, students compare stone hand axes from different time periods to understand technological development
  - An illustrated timeline helps students visualize the 2.5 million years of Stone Age human evolution
  - Students examine how modern hunter-gatherer communities in remote regions live similarly to Paleolithic people
  - A simulation game challenges students to survive as Stone Age hunters gathering food and avoiding predators
  - Students read about the discovery of Otzi the Iceman and what his preserved tools reveal about Neolithic life
  - An archaeological dig activity teaches students how researchers reconstruct past societies from artifacts and remains
  - Students compare cave paintings from France and Spain to understand Old Stone Age artistic expression
  - A lesson on the origins of farming explains how wild wheat and barley were first cultivated in the Fertile Crescent
  - Students explore how the domestication of dogs, sheep, and cattle changed human relationships with animals
  - A project asks students to design a Stone Age settlement explaining location, housing, and food sources
  - Students debate whether the shift to farming improved or worsened quality of life for early humans
  - An exhibit shows how pottery invention in the Neolithic period revolutionized food storage and cooking
  - Students examine flint-knapping demonstrations to understand the skill required to create Stone Age tools
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid referencing specific dates or precise chronology beyond general periods
  - Do not include terminology from later historical periods or anachronistic concepts
  - Avoid oversimplifying the transition as purely positive progress
  - Do not present Stone Age people as unintelligent or primitive in capabilities
  - Avoid scenarios requiring knowledge beyond the Old and New Stone Age periods
version: 1
version_notes: Initial concept covering Lernbereich 1 of Grade 6 Bavarian curriculum - hunters/gatherers vs farmers/settlers
