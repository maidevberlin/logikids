# Speaking - Greetings and Introductions

Generate tasks that teach {{age}}-year-old students how to greet others and introduce themselves in English.

## Task Variations

### 1. Greeting Matching
Match greetings with appropriate responses.

**Draw a line to connect each greeting with the correct response:**

| Greeting | Response |
|----------|----------|
| Hello! | A. Goodbye! |
| How are you? | B. My name is Tom. |
| What's your name? | C. I'm fine, thank you. |
| Bye! | D. Hi! Nice to meet you. |

### 2. Dialogue Completion
Complete a conversation between two children meeting.

**Fill in the blanks:**

Anna: "{{blank}}, I'm Anna!"
Ben: "Hi Anna! I'm {{blank}}."
Anna: "{{blank}} are you?"
Ben: "I'm fine, {{blank}} you. How are you?"
Anna: "I'm {{blank}} too!"

**Word Bank:** Hello • Ben • How • thank • great

### 3. Introduction Sentences
Build complete introduction sentences.

**Put the words in the correct order:**

1. **is / name / Tom / My**
   → ________________________________

2. **old / I'm / eight / years**
   → ________________________________

3. **to / you / Nice / meet**
   → ________________________________

### 4. Time-Appropriate Greetings
Choose the correct greeting for different times of day.

**What do you say? Circle the correct answer:**

1. It's 9:00 in the morning. You say:
   - a) Good night!
   - b) Good morning!
   - c) Good evening!

2. It's 3:00 in the afternoon. You say:
   - a) Good afternoon!
   - b) Good morning!
   - c) Good night!

3. It's 8:00 at night and you're going to bed. You say:
   - a) Good morning!
   - b) Good afternoon!
   - c) Good night!

### 5. Courtesy Phrases Practice
Complete sentences with polite phrases.

**Fill in the missing courtesy words:**

1. When someone helps you, you say: "{{blank}} you!"
2. When you want something, you say: "{{blank}}, can I have a pencil?"
3. When someone says "Thank you," you say: "You're {{blank}}!"
4. When you accidentally bump into someone, you say: "{{blank}} me!"

**Word Bank:** Excuse • Thank • Please • welcome

### 6. Role-Play Dialogue Order
Put conversation lines in the correct order.

**Number these sentences from 1-6 to make a conversation:**

___ Nice to meet you too!
___ Hi! My name is Sarah. What's your name?
___ I'm nine years old. How old are you?
___ Hello! I'm Alex.
___ I'm eight.
___ Nice to meet you, Alex!

### 7. Introduction Information Table
Practice sharing personal information.

**Complete the table about yourself:**

| Question | Your Answer |
|----------|-------------|
| What's your name? | My name is {{blank}}. |
| How old are you? | I'm {{blank}} years old. |
| How are you? | I'm {{blank}}. |

**Now practice saying your answers out loud!**

### 8. Multiple Choice Conversations
Choose the best response in different situations.

**What would you say? Circle the correct answer:**

1. A new student joins your class. You say:
   - a) Goodbye!
   - b) Hello! What's your name?
   - c) Thank you!

2. Your teacher says "Good morning!" You say:
   - a) Good night!
   - b) Good morning!
   - c) See you later!

3. Your friend asks "How are you?" You say:
   - a) My name is Sam.
   - b) I'm fine, thank you.
   - c) Nice to meet you.

### 9. Formal vs. Informal Greetings
Identify appropriate greetings for different situations.

**Match the situation with the greeting:**

| Situation | Greeting |
|-----------|----------|
| Meeting a friend | A. Good morning, Mrs. Smith. |
| Meeting your teacher in the morning | B. Hi! How are you? |
| Saying goodbye to a friend | C. Hello, nice to meet you. |
| Meeting someone new | D. Bye! See you later! |

### 10. Complete Mini-Dialogues
Fill in appropriate responses.

**Read and complete each conversation:**

**Conversation 1:**
Tom: "Hello! I'm Tom."
You: "Hi Tom! {{blank}}"
Tom: "Nice to meet you!"
You: "{{blank}}"

**Conversation 2:**
Lisa: "How are you today?"
You: "{{blank}}"
Lisa: "That's great!"

**Helpful phrases:** I'm [your name] • Nice to meet you too • I'm fine, thank you
