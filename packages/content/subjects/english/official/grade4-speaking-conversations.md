# Speaking - Conversations

Create tasks that help students practice conversational English in everyday situations.

**Task Requirements:**
- Focus on authentic, useful dialogues
- Practice both asking AND answering questions
- Cover everyday topics (family, school, hobbies, food, free time)
- Include role-play scenarios
- Provide conversation structures/frames
- Encourage natural interaction
- Make conversations meaningful and age-appropriate
- Age-appropriate for 9-10 year olds

**Generate varied task types:**

## Variation 1: Getting to Know You Conversation
Practice introductions and personal information exchange.

**Example:**
**Partner A asks:** | **Partner B answers:**
- What's your name? | My name is...
- How old are you? | I'm... years old.
- Where are you from? | I'm from...
- Do you have any brothers or sisters? | Yes, I have... / No, I don't.
- What's your favorite subject? | My favorite subject is...

Then switch roles! Partner B asks, Partner A answers.

**Extension:** Add your own questions about hobbies, pets, favorite foods.

## Variation 2: Shopping Role Play
Practice a conversation in a shop.

**Example:**
**At the ice cream shop:**

**Customer:** Hello! What flavors do you have?
**Shop assistant:** We have chocolate, vanilla, strawberry, and lemon.
**Customer:** Can I have {{flavor}}, please?
**Shop assistant:** Of course! One scoop or two?
**Customer:** {{number}} scoop(s), please. How much is it?
**Shop assistant:** That's £{{price}}.
**Customer:** Here you are. Thank you!
**Shop assistant:** You're welcome! Enjoy!

Practice with different items: bookshop, toy shop, clothes shop.

## Variation 3: Making Plans Conversation
Talk about future plans and activities.

**Example:**
**Friend A:** What are you doing this weekend?
**Friend B:** I'm going to {{activity}}. What about you?
**Friend A:** I'm {{activity}}. Do you want to {{suggestion}}?
**Friend B:** Yes, that sounds great! / Sorry, I can't. I have to {{reason}}.
**Friend A:** Okay! Maybe next time. / Great! When shall we meet?
**Friend B:** How about {{day}} at {{time}}?
**Friend A:** Perfect! See you then!

Activities: visit the park, play football, go to the cinema, have a picnic

## Variation 4: Talking About Hobbies
Exchange information about interests and hobbies.

**Example:**
**Conversation starters:**
- What do you like doing in your free time?
- What's your favorite hobby?
- Do you play any sports?
- Can you play a musical instrument?
- What do you do after school?

**Sample dialogue:**
**A:** What's your favorite hobby?
**B:** I love {{hobby}}. What about you?
**A:** I like {{hobby}}. How often do you {{hobby}}?
**B:** I {{hobby}} {{frequency}}. Do you want to try it?
**A:** Yes, please! / No, thank you. I prefer {{different hobby}}.

## Variation 5: School Life Conversation
Talk about school subjects, schedule, and activities.

**Example:**
**Discussion prompts:**
- What's your favorite subject? Why?
- What subjects do you have today?
- When do you have {{subject}}?
- Do you like {{subject}}?
- What do you do at break time?

**Sample exchange:**
**A:** What's your favorite subject?
**B:** My favorite subject is {{subject}} because it's {{adjective}}.
**A:** Really? I like {{subject}}. When do you have it?
**B:** I have it on {{days}}.
**A:** Me too! / I have it on {{different days}}.

## Variation 6: Food and Preferences
Discuss food likes, dislikes, and eating habits.

**Example:**
**Conversation:**
**A:** What's your favorite food?
**B:** I love {{food}}! Do you like it?
**A:** Yes, I do! / No, I don't. I prefer {{different food}}.
**B:** What do you usually eat for breakfast/lunch/dinner?
**A:** I usually eat {{food}} for {{meal}}.
**B:** That sounds {{adjective}}!

**Follow-up questions:**
- Can you cook?
- What can you cook?
- What don't you like eating?
- What's your favorite restaurant?

## Variation 7: Describing Family
Talk about family members and what they do.

**Example:**
**Guided conversation:**
**A:** Do you have any brothers or sisters?
**B:** Yes, I have {{number}} {{brother(s)/sister(s)}}. / No, I don't.
**A:** How old is/are {{he/she/they}}?
**B:** {{He's/She's/They're}} {{age}} years old.
**A:** What does {{he/she}} like doing?
**B:** {{He/She}} likes {{activity}}.

**About parents:**
- What does your mum/dad do? (job)
- What do you do with your family?
- Do you have any pets?

## Variation 8: Weather and Seasons Chat
Talk about weather and seasonal activities.

**Example:**
**A:** What's the weather like today?
**B:** It's {{weather adjective}}. / It's {{verb}}.
**A:** What's your favorite season?
**B:** My favorite season is {{season}} because {{reason}}.
**A:** What do you like doing in {{season}}?
**B:** I like {{activity}}. What about you?

Weather words: *sunny, rainy, cloudy, windy, hot, cold, warm, snowing*
Seasons: *spring, summer, autumn, winter*

## Variation 9: Phone Conversation Practice
Practice a simple phone conversation.

**Example:**
**Person A calls Person B:**

**A:** Hello, this is {{name}}. Can I speak to {{friend's name}}, please?
**B:** Hi! This is {{friend's name}}. How are you?
**A:** I'm fine, thanks! And you?
**B:** I'm great! Why are you calling?
**A:** I wanted to ask... {{question/invitation}}
**B:** {{response}}. What time?
**A:** At {{time}}. Is that okay?
**B:** Yes, perfect! See you then!
**A:** Great! Bye!
**B:** Bye!

## Variation 10: At a Restaurant Role Play
Order food and drinks in a restaurant.

**Example:**
**Waiter:** Good afternoon! Welcome to {{restaurant name}}. Here's the menu.
**Customer:** Thank you. Can I have {{drink}}, please?
**Waiter:** Of course. Are you ready to order food?
**Customer:** Yes. I'd like {{food}}, please.
**Waiter:** Good choice! Anything else?
**Customer:** No, thank you. That's all.
**Waiter:** Okay! Your food will be ready in 10 minutes.
*(Later)*
**Waiter:** Here you are. Enjoy your meal!
**Customer:** Thank you! Can I have the bill, please?
**Waiter:** Yes, that's £{{amount}}.
**Customer:** Here you are. Thank you!
