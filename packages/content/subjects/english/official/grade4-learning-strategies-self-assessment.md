# Learning Strategies - Self-assessment

Create tasks that help students develop effective learning strategies and self-assessment skills.

**Task Requirements:**
- Teach practical learning techniques (flashcards, word lists, practice)
- Encourage portfolio keeping and documentation
- Provide simple self-assessment tools (checklists, "can-do" statements)
- Introduce simple reference materials (picture dictionaries, word lists)
- Make learning strategies age-appropriate and fun
- Foster learner autonomy and reflection
- Age-appropriate for 9-10 year olds

**Generate varied task types:**

## Variation 1: Vocabulary Card Creation
Guide students to create effective vocabulary learning cards.

**Example:**
Make a vocabulary card for the word *{{word}}*:

**Front of card:**
```
Word: _____
Picture: [draw or paste]
```

**Back of card:**
```
Meaning in German: _____
Example sentence: _____
Category: _____
```

Example for "bicycle":
- Picture: drawing of a bike
- Meaning: Fahrrad
- Example: "I ride my bicycle to school."
- Category: Transport

Create {{number}} cards for new words you learned this week.

## Variation 2: "I Can" Self-Assessment Checklist
Provide a checklist for students to assess their own abilities.

**Example:**
What can you do in English? Check (✓) the boxes:

| I can... | Yes ✓ | Not yet |
|----------|-------|---------|
| say hello and goodbye | ☐ | ☐ |
| introduce myself | ☐ | ☐ |
| talk about my family | ☐ | ☐ |
| say what I like and don't like | ☐ | ☐ |
| count to 100 | ☐ | ☐ |
| ask for the time | ☐ | ☐ |
| describe my hobbies | ☐ | ☐ |

Look at your "Not yet" boxes. Choose {{number}} things to practice this week!

## Variation 3: Learning Technique Matching
Match different learning techniques to situations.

**Example:**
Which learning strategy helps you best? Match:

1. I want to remember new words → _____
2. I want to practice speaking → _____
3. I don't understand a word → _____
4. I want to improve my pronunciation → _____

**Strategies:**
A. Use a dictionary
B. Make flashcards
C. Listen to songs and repeat
D. Talk with a partner

Then: Which strategy do you use most often? Which one do you want to try?

## Variation 5: Dictionary Skills Practice
Practice using a simple picture dictionary or word list.

**Example:**
Use your picture dictionary to find words:

1. Find {{number}} words about animals.
2. Find {{number}} words about food.
3. Find a word that starts with the letter {{letter}}.
4. Find the English word for "{{german_word}}".

For each word you find, write:
- The word: _____
- A sentence: _____
- A picture: [draw]

## Variation 6: Learning Goal Setting
Help students set and track learning goals.

**Example:**
**My English Learning Goals**

**This Week I Want To:**
- ☐ Learn {{number}} new words about {{topic}}
- ☐ Practice saying {{phrase}}
- ☐ Read {{number}} pages in my English book
- ☐ Listen to {{number}} English songs

**Next Week I Will:**
- Look back: Did I reach my goals? Yes/No
- What worked well? _____
- What was difficult? _____
- New goal: _____

## Variation 7: Error Learning Activity
Teach students to learn from their mistakes.

**Example:**
**My Mistake Book**

Find your mistakes and learn from them:

| I wrote/said | The correct form is | Why? | Example |
|--------------|---------------------|------|---------|
| He like apples | He like**s** apples | add -s | She likes bananas |
| I can to swim | I can swim | no "to" | I can play football |

Keep a page in your notebook for mistakes. They help you learn!

**Example:**

Words: *Monday, cat, red, Tuesday, dog, blue, Wednesday, bird, green, Thursday, fish, yellow*

**Strategy 1: By Topic**
| Days | Animals | Colors |
|------|---------|--------|
| _____ | _____ | _____ |

**Strategy 2: Alphabetically**

G-M: _____, _____
N-Z: _____, _____

**Strategy 3: By How I Use Them**
- At school: _____
- At home: _____
- Outside: _____

Which strategy works best for you?

## Variation 10: Peer Learning Strategy
Practice teaching what you know to reinforce learning.

**Example:**
**Teach a Friend**

Choose something you learned in English. Teach it to a friend or family member:

**What I taught:** _____________________

**Who I taught:** _____________________

**How I explained it:**
1. First I _____
2. Then I _____
3. Finally I _____

**How it went:**
- ☐ They understood everything
- ☐ Some parts were difficult
- ☐ I need to practice more myself

**What I learned:** When you teach something, you learn it better!
