# Expanded Vocabulary Themes

Create tasks that expand students' vocabulary across 8-10 thematic areas, building toward 300-400 active words.

**Task Requirements:**
- Cover multiple themes (family, school, hobbies, food, weather, animals, daily routines, time, body parts, sports)
- Include both nouns and verbs
- Teach functional phrases for everyday situations
- Use authentic contexts
- Provide visual support when possible
- Make vocabulary memorable through engaging activities
- Age-appropriate for 9-10 year olds

**Generate varied task types:**

## Variation 1: Thematic Word Sorting
Present mixed vocabulary from different themes and ask students to categorize them.

**Example:**
Sort these words into the correct categories:

Words: *cloudy, swimming, breakfast, sister, dinner, sunny, football, brother, lunch, rainy, tennis, mother*

| Family | Meals | Weather | Sports |
|--------|-------|---------|--------|
| _____ | _____ | _____ | _____ |
| _____ | _____ | _____ | _____ |
| _____ | _____ | _____ | _____ |

## Variation 2: Daily Routine Story Completion
Provide a text about someone's daily routine with gaps for students to fill using appropriate vocabulary.

**Example:**
> My Day
>
> I _____ (wake up) at 7 o'clock. Then I _____ (brush) my teeth and _____ (get dressed). For breakfast, I usually eat _____ (cereal/toast). At 8:30, I _____ (go) to school. After school, I _____ (do) my homework and _____ (play) with my friends. In the evening, I _____ (watch) TV and _____ (read) a book. I _____ (go) to bed at 9 o'clock.

Word bank: *wake up, brush, get dressed, eat, go, do, play, watch, read*

## Variation 3: Picture Matching with Action Verbs
Match action verbs to descriptions or pictures of daily activities.

**Example:**
Match the verb to the activity:

1. *brush* → A. your homework
2. *do* → B. to music
3. *listen* → C. your teeth
4. *ride* → D. dinner
5. *cook* → E. a bike

Then use each verb in a sentence: "I _____ my teeth every morning."

## Variation 4: Weather and Clothing Connections
Connect weather vocabulary with appropriate clothing choices.

**Example:**
What do you wear when it's...?

1. *sunny and hot* → _____
   (Options: t-shirt and shorts, coat and scarf, raincoat and boots)

2. *rainy* → _____
   (Options: swimsuit, raincoat and umbrella, winter jacket)

3. *cold and snowy* → _____
   (Options: sandals, warm jacket and gloves, light dress)

Vocabulary to teach: *sunny, cloudy, rainy, windy, hot, cold, warm, cool, snowy*

## Variation 5: Hobby and Free Time Survey
Create a survey-style task using vocabulary about hobbies and free time activities.

**Example:**
Complete the sentences about your hobbies:

1. I like _____ (playing/watching) football.
2. My favorite hobby is _____ (reading/drawing/swimming).
3. On weekends, I often _____ (visit/meet) my friends.
4. I'm interested in _____ (music/sports/art).
5. After school, I usually _____ (play/do/watch) _____.

Hobbies vocabulary: *reading, drawing, painting, swimming, dancing, playing music, collecting, cycling, playing computer games*

## Variation 6: Food Categories and Preferences
Practice food vocabulary through categorization and preference expression.

**Example:**
Put these foods in the correct category:

Foods: *apple, carrot, chicken, banana, broccoli, fish, strawberry, potato, bread, cheese*

| Fruit | Vegetables | Protein | Other |
|-------|------------|---------|-------|
| _____ | _____ | _____ | _____ |

Then complete: "I like _____ but I don't like _____."

Teach phrases: *I like..., I love..., I don't like..., My favorite food is..., I prefer...*

## Variation 7: Time Expressions in Context
Use time vocabulary in realistic contexts.

**Example:**
Fill in the correct time expression:

1. I have English lessons _____ Monday and Wednesday. (*on*)
2. My birthday is _____ May. (*in*)
3. School starts _____ 8 o'clock. (*at*)
4. We have holidays _____ summer. (*in*)
5. I play football _____ the afternoon. (*in*)

Time vocabulary: *morning, afternoon, evening, night, today, tomorrow, yesterday, Monday-Sunday, January-December, at, on, in*

## Variation 8: Body Parts and Health
Introduce body parts vocabulary with functional health expressions.

**Example:**
Complete the sentences:

1. I have a headache. My _____ hurts. (*head*)
2. I can't walk. My _____ hurts. (*leg/foot*)
3. I need glasses. My _____ are bad. (*eyes*)
4. I can't write. My _____ hurts. (*hand*)

Body parts: *head, eyes, ears, nose, mouth, teeth, arms, hands, legs, feet, stomach, back*

Functional phrases: *I feel..., I have a..., My ... hurts, I need...*

## Variation 9: Sports and Activities Word Web
Create connections between sports vocabulary and related words.

**Example:**
Complete the word web for "football":

```
        play football
             |
    _________|_________
    |        |        |
 players   field    ball
    |        |        |
  team    goal    kick
```

Sports vocabulary: *football, basketball, tennis, swimming, cycling, running, jumping*
Action verbs: *play, kick, throw, catch, run, jump, swim, ride*

## Variation 10: Functional Phrases in Situations
Practice functional phrases for common situations.

**Example:**
What do you say when...?

1. You want to ask the time:
   → "What _____ is it?" (*time*)

2. You want to ask about the weather:
   → "What's the _____ like?" (*weather*)

3. You want to know someone's age:
   → "How _____ are you?" (*old*)

4. You want to ask about someone's favorite subject:
   → "What's your _____ subject?" (*favorite*)

Functional phrases to teach: *What time is it? How are you? How old are you? What's your favorite...? Can I have...? May I...?*
