# Learning Strategies - Memorization

Generate tasks that teach {{age}}-year-old students effective memorization strategies for English vocabulary.

## Task Variations

### 1. Rhyming Words Memory
Students identify and match rhyming words that help them remember vocabulary.

**Find the rhyming pairs. Draw lines to connect them:**

Left column:
- *cat*
- *bee*
- *dog*
- *sun*

Right column:
- *tree*
- *hat*
- *run*
- *frog*

**Now make a rhyme:** The cat wears a _____.

### 4. Memory Chant Practice
Use rhythm and repetition to memorize vocabulary.

**Learn this chant. Fill in the missing words:**

*Days of the Week Chant:*
- Monday, Tuesday, {{blank}} day,
- Thursday, Friday, it's okay!
- Saturday, {{blank}}, we can play!
- Seven days make one whole week!

**Practice tip:** Clap your hands while saying each day!

### 5. Picture Dictionary Match
Students match words to pictures and organize them like a dictionary.

**Look at the pictures. Write the correct word under each one:**

**Word Bank:** book • pencil • bag • ruler

1. [Picture of book] → ___________
2. [Picture of pencil] → ___________
3. [Picture of bag] → ___________
4. [Picture of ruler] → ___________

**Now put them in ABC order:** ___, ___, ___, ___

### 6. Action Words Memory
Use movements to remember verbs.

**Match each action to the movement:**

| Word | What do you do? |
|------|-----------------|
| *jump* | A. Move your hands like you're writing |
| *write* | B. Move your legs up and down |
| *clap* | C. Put your hands together and make noise |
| *run* | D. Move your legs very fast |

**Memory trick:** Do the action while saying the word!
