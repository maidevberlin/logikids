# Reading - Various Text Types

Create a reading comprehension task using different text types appropriate for Grade 5 students.

## Task Variations

### 1. Personal Correspondence (Postcards, Messages, Emails)
Provide a **simple message** from {{sender}} about {{topic}} and ask students to:
- Identify **who** wrote it and **why**
- Extract **specific information** (dates, places, activities)
- Understand the **sender's feelings** or intentions

Example scenarios: vacation postcard, birthday thank-you, party invitation, friendly email

> *Example format*:
> "Dear Sarah,
> I'm having such a great time in London! Yesterday we visited the Tower of London and saw the Crown Jewels. Today we're going to the Natural History Museum..."

### 2. Instructions and How-To Texts
Present **step-by-step instructions** for {{activity}} and have students:
- Identify what **materials** are needed
- Understand the **sequence** of steps
- Answer questions about **specific details**

| Text Type | Example Content |
|-----------|-----------------|
| Recipe | Making a fruit salad, baking cookies |
| Craft | Origami instructions, making a bookmark |
| Game Rules | Board game, playground game |
| Tutorial | Using a website, care instructions |

### 3. Factual Information Texts
Provide a **short informational text** about {{topic}} and ask students to:
- Identify the **main idea** or purpose
- Find **specific facts** (numbers, names, dates)
- Answer **comprehension questions**

Example topics: animal facts, country information, historical event, invention, natural phenomenon

### 4. Simple Literary Texts (Stories, Fables)
Present a **short story** or **fable** about {{character}} and have students:
- Identify the **main characters**
- Understand the **plot** (beginning, middle, end)
- Recognize the **central theme** or message

Example story types: adventure, friendship, problem-solving, moral lesson

### 5. Advertisements and Announcements
Show a **poster** or **advertisement** for {{event_or_product}} and ask students to:
- Understand **what** is being advertised
- Find **important details** (price, date, location, contact)
- Identify **who** the target audience is

Example content: summer camp, book fair, school play, new product, community event

### 6. Schedules and Timetables
Provide a **schedule** for {{context}} and have students:
- Locate **specific information** (times, places, activities)
- Answer questions requiring reading across rows/columns
- Make simple conclusions based on the information

Example contexts: bus schedule, TV program guide, school timetable, weekly activities

### 7. Informational Signs and Notices
Present **signs or notices** from {{location}} and ask students to:
- Understand the **purpose** of the sign
- Identify what action is **required or prohibited**
- Find specific information (opening hours, rules, directions)

Example locations: museum, park, library, zoo, swimming pool, store

### 8. Short News Articles or Blog Posts
Provide a **simple article** about {{topic}} and have students:
- Identify the **main news** or point
- Extract **key information** (who, what, when, where)
- Distinguish between **facts** and **opinions** (if age-appropriate)

Example topics: school event, sports result, new playground, community news, seasonal activity

**Reading Strategies to Practice**:
- **Skimming**: Getting the general idea quickly
- **Scanning**: Finding specific information
- **Predicting**: Using titles and pictures to guess content
- **Context clues**: Understanding new words from surrounding text

**Important**: All texts should use **simple, clear language**, be **short enough** to maintain engagement (100-200 words), include **familiar vocabulary** with some new words inferable from context, and be **age-appropriate** in content and presentation.
