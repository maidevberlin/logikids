# Intercultural Competence - Countries and Cultures

Create tasks that help students learn about and appreciate cultures in English-speaking countries.

**Task Requirements:**
- Explore multiple English-speaking countries (UK, USA, Canada, Australia, Ireland, New Zealand)
- Cover festivals, traditions, daily life, school systems
- Encourage comparison without judgment
- Use authentic materials (photos, texts, videos)
- Develop cultural awareness and respect
- Make connections to students' own culture
- Foster curiosity and openness
- Age-appropriate for 10-11 year olds

**Generate varied task types:**

## Variation 1: Festival Comparison Chart
Compare festivals across English-speaking countries.

**Example:**

**Major Festivals**

| Festival | Country | When | What people do |
|----------|---------|------|----------------|
| Halloween | USA, UK, Ireland | October 31st | Dress up in costumes, trick-or-treat, carve pumpkins |
| Thanksgiving | USA | 4th Thursday in November | Family dinner, turkey, give thanks |
| Guy Fawkes Night | UK | November 5th | Fireworks, bonfires |
| Australia Day | Australia | January 26th | BBQs, beach, celebrate being Australian |
| Canada Day | Canada | July 1st | Parades, fireworks, wear red and white |

**Tasks:**
1. Which festival happens in October? _____
2. Which countries celebrate with fireworks? _____
3. Which festival is about giving thanks? _____
4. Compare: Which festival in your country is similar to Thanksgiving? _____

## Variation 2: School System Comparison
Compare schools in different English-speaking countries.

**Example:**

Read about schools in {{country}}:

> **School in England:**
> - Children start primary school at age 5
> - They wear school uniforms
> - School day: 9:00 AM - 3:30 PM
> - Lunch: Many children have school dinners in the canteen
> - Sports: Football, cricket, rugby are popular
> - Subjects: English, Maths, Science, PE, Art, Music
> - Exams: SATs at age 11

**Compare with your school:**

| Topic | England | My School |
|-------|---------|-----------|
| Start age | 5 | _____ |
| Uniform | Yes | _____ |
| School hours | 9:00-3:30 | _____ |
| Lunch | School canteen | _____ |
| Popular sports | Football, cricket | _____ |

**Discussion:** What do you think about school uniforms? Would you like to try them?

## Variation 3: Country Profile Matching
Match information to the correct English-speaking country.

**Example:**

Match the facts to the country:

**Countries:** USA, UK, Australia, Canada, Ireland

**Facts:**
1. This country is famous for kangaroos and koalas. → _____
2. People here celebrate St. Patrick's Day. → _____
3. This country has 50 states. → _____
4. People here speak English and French. → _____
5. London is the capital of this country. → _____
6. This country is "Down Under" (in the Southern Hemisphere). → _____

**Answers:** 1-Australia, 2-Ireland, 3-USA, 4-Canada, 5-UK, 6-Australia

## Variation 6: Housing and Living Comparison
Learn about typical homes in different countries.

**Example:**

Read and compare:

> **Typical American Home:**
> Many Americans live in single-family houses with a yard (garden). Houses often have wooden frames. Many people have a car and a garage. Air conditioning is common because summers are hot in many states.

> **Typical British Home:**
> Many British people live in terraced houses or semi-detached houses. Houses are usually made of brick. Gardens are smaller than in the USA. Not many homes have air conditioning because the weather is cooler.

**True or False:**
1. American houses often have yards. (True)
2. British houses are usually made of wood. (False - brick)
3. Air conditioning is more common in the USA. (True)
4. British gardens are usually bigger than American yards. (False)

**Discuss:** How is your home similar or different?

## Variation 7: National Symbols and Icons
Identify and learn about national symbols.

**Example:**

**Match the symbol to the country:**

1. Bald Eagle → _____
2. Union Jack (flag) → _____
3. Kangaroo → _____
4. Maple Leaf → _____
5. Shamrock → _____

**Countries:** USA, UK, Australia, Canada, Ireland

**Then learn more:**
- Why is the bald eagle important to Americans? (Symbol of freedom)
- What colors are in the Union Jack? (Red, white, blue)
- Why is the maple leaf on Canada's flag? (Maple trees are important in Canada)

## Variation 8: Everyday Expressions and Slang
Learn how people speak differently in different countries.

**Example:**

**Different Words, Same Meaning:**

| American English | British English | Australian English |
|-----------------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Elevator | Lift | Lift |
| Apartment | Flat | Flat |
| Cookies | Biscuits | Biscuits |
| Trash | Rubbish | Rubbish |
| Vacation | Holiday | Holiday |
| Fries | Chips | Chips |
| Good/Great! | Brilliant! | Ripper! / Bonzer! |

**Tasks:**
1. How do British people say "elevator"? _____
2. What do Americans call "holidays"? _____
3. If an Australian says "Ripper!", what does it mean? _____
4. Practice: Rewrite this American sentence in British English:
   "I live in an apartment and I love cookies!"
   → "I live in a _____ and I love _____!"

## Variation 10: Cultural Dos and Don'ts
Learn about polite behavior in different cultures.

**Example:**

**Being Polite in English-Speaking Countries:**

**Things people usually do:**
- ✓ Say "please" and "thank you" often
- ✓ Queue (wait in line) patiently
- ✓ Shake hands when meeting someone
- ✓ Make eye contact when speaking
- ✓ Be on time

**Things to avoid:**
- ✗ Don't push in queues (cutting in line)
- ✗ Don't talk too loudly in public places
- ✗ Don't ask personal questions when you first meet someone

**Compare:**
How is this similar or different in your country?

**Scenario practice:**
What would you say in these situations in an English-speaking country?
1. Someone holds the door for you. → _____
2. You accidentally bump into someone. → _____
3. You want to ask someone the time. → _____

**Answers:** 1. Thank you! 2. Sorry! / Excuse me! 3. Excuse me, what time is it, please?
