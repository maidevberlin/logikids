# Vocabulary - Topic Fields

Create tasks that help students learn and practice vocabulary organized by topics relevant to their daily lives, along with functional phrases and connecting words.

## Task Variations

### 1. Topic Field Vocabulary Building
Present vocabulary for {{topic_field}} with exercises:
- **Matching** words to pictures or definitions
- **Categorizing** words within the topic
- **Using words** in context sentences
- **Gap-fill** exercises with topic vocabulary

| Topic Field | Example Vocabulary |
|-------------|-------------------|
| Food & Meals | breakfast, lunch, dinner, snack, ingredients, recipe, delicious |
| Hobbies | collect, practice, team, equipment, tournament, skill, club |
| School Life | subject, homework, break, grade, classroom, library, timetable |
| Weather & Seasons | temperature, forecast, cloudy, rainy, sunny, stormy, climate |
| Travel | journey, destination, passport, luggage, ticket, adventure, sightseeing |

### 2. Functional Phrases Practice
Teach and practice **functional phrases** for {{situation}}:
- **Identifying** appropriate phrases for contexts
- **Completing dialogues** with suitable expressions
- **Role-playing** using the phrases naturally

Example situations:
- **Asking for things**: *Could I have...?* / *May I borrow...?*
- **Making suggestions**: *How about...?* / *Why don't we...?*
- **Expressing preferences**: *I'd rather...* / *I prefer...*
- **Agreeing/Disagreeing**: *I agree* / *I'm not sure about that*

### 3. Connecting Words (Connectives)
Practice using **connecting words** to link ideas:

| Connector | Purpose | Example |
|-----------|---------|---------|
| **and** | Adding information | I like pizza *and* pasta. |
| **but** | Contrasting | I'm tired *but* happy. |
| **because** | Giving reasons | I'm inside *because* it's raining. |
| **so** | Showing results | It's sunny, *so* we'll go swimming. |
| **or** | Offering alternatives | Do you want tea *or* coffee? |
| **when** | Talking about time | I'm happy *when* I play soccer. |

### 4. Vocabulary in Context
Provide short texts about {{topic}} that use **topic-specific vocabulary**:
- Students identify **new words** from context
- Explain **meanings** based on context clues
- Use the words in **their own sentences**

Example topics: a day at the beach, preparing for a party, visiting a museum, playing a sport

### 5. Word Families and Collocations
Explore **related words** and **common combinations** for {{concept}}:
- **Nouns, verbs, adjectives** from the same word family
- **Common phrases** and **collocations**
- **Usage patterns** in sentences

> Example word family:
> *travel* (verb), *travel* (noun), *traveler* (noun), *traveling* (adjective)
> Common collocations: *travel abroad*, *travel by train*, *business travel*

### 6. Situational Vocabulary
Present a **situation** ({{scenario}}) and have students:
- List **relevant vocabulary**
- Practice **functional language** for the situation
- Create **dialogues** or **descriptions** using the words

Example scenarios: at the doctor's office, shopping for clothes, at a birthday party, on a camping trip

### 7. Topic Field Expansion
Build on basic vocabulary for {{topic}} by adding:
- **Descriptive adjectives** (*delicious food*, *exciting game*)
- **Action verbs** (*prepare a meal*, *score a goal*)
- **Specific terminology** (more precise words)

Example topics: sports (beginner â†’ advanced vocabulary), technology, nature, entertainment

### 8. Functional Language for Communication Needs
Practice phrases for **communication strategies**:
- **Asking for help**: *Can you help me?* / *I don't understand*
- **Clarifying**: *Do you mean...?* / *What does ... mean?*
- **Checking**: *Is this correct?* / *Did I do it right?*
- **Polite requests**: *Could you please...?* / *Would you mind...?*

**Vocabulary Learning Strategies**:

- **Grouping by topic**: Easier to remember related words together
- **Using context**: Understanding how words are actually used
- **Repetition and practice**: Using new words multiple times
- **Personal connection**: Relating vocabulary to own experiences

**Important**: Choose **age-appropriate topics** students encounter in their lives, present **15-20 new words** per topic field maximum, always show vocabulary **in context** (not just isolated lists), and practice with **varied, engaging exercises**.
