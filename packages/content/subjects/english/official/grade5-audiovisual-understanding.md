# Audiovisual Understanding

Create tasks that help students understand and connect audio and visual information in English media.

**Task Requirements:**
- Combine listening with visual elements (pictures, videos, charts, diagrams)
- Keep clips short (30 seconds - 2 minutes)
- Use age-appropriate content (cartoons, simple documentaries, instructional videos)
- Test understanding of BOTH audio and visual elements
- Practice predicting, inferring, and connecting information
- Use authentic or authentic-style materials
- Make tasks engaging and purposeful
- Age-appropriate for 10-11 year olds

**Generate varied task types:**

## Variation 1: Video Prediction and Viewing
Use visual clues to predict content before watching.

**Example:**
**Before Watching:**
Look at these pictures from the video. What do you think the video is about?

[Images showing: {{relevant scenes}}]

Predictions:
- I think it's about _____
- I think we will see _____
- I think we will learn about _____

**While Watching:**
Watch and check: Were your predictions correct?

**After Watching:**
Answer:
1. What was the video about? _____
2. What did you see? _____
3. What surprised you? _____

## Variation 2: Picture-Audio Matching
Match spoken descriptions to the correct images.

**Example:**
Listen to {{number}} descriptions. Match each description to the correct picture.

**Audio:**
1. "This animal has black and white stripes. It lives in Africa and looks like a horse."
2. "This animal is very tall. It has a long neck and eats leaves from trees."
3. "This animal is grey and very big. It has a long trunk and big ears."

**Pictures:**
A. [Elephant]
B. [Zebra]
C. [Giraffe]

Answers: 1→ ___, 2→ ___, 3→ ___

## Variation 3: Scene Sequencing from Audio
Listen and put pictures in the correct order.

**Example:**
Listen to the story. Put the pictures in the right order (1-5).

**Audio narration:**
"Tom woke up late. He quickly got dressed. He ran to the bus stop. He missed the bus. He walked to school."

**Pictures to order:**
☐ Tom walking to school
☐ Tom waking up
☐ Tom running to bus stop
☐ Tom getting dressed
☐ Missing the bus

## Variation 4: Video Comprehension with Visual Clues
Answer questions about a video using both audio and visual information.

**Example:**
Watch the video about {{topic}}. Answer these questions:

**Visual questions:**
1. What color is {{object}}? _____
2. How many {{items}} do you see? _____
3. What is {{person}} wearing? _____

**Audio questions:**
4. What does the narrator say about _____? _____
5. Why does {{event}} happen? _____

**Combined questions:**
6. What happens when {{audio event}} AND {{visual element}}? _____

## Variation 5: Weather Forecast Understanding
Watch/listen to a weather forecast and identify information.

**Example:**
Listen to the weather forecast. Look at the map and symbols.

**Audio:**
"Good morning! Today in London, it will be rainy with temperatures around 15 degrees. In Manchester, expect cloudy skies and 12 degrees. Scotland will see some sunshine with 10 degrees."

**Map shows:** UK with weather symbols in different cities

**Tasks:**
1. Draw the correct weather symbol for each city:
   - London: _____
   - Manchester: _____
   - Scotland: _____

2. What's the temperature in Manchester? _____
3. Which place will be sunny? _____

## Variation 6: Instructional Video Following
Watch a "how-to" video and answer questions.

**Example:**
Watch the video "How to Make a Paper Airplane"

**Tasks:**
1. Number the steps in the correct order (from what you see and hear):
   ☐ Fold the paper in half
   ☐ Fold the corners down
   ☐ Make the wings
   ☐ Test your plane

2. What does the video say NOT to do? _____
3. What shape do you make first? _____
4. Follow the instructions and make your own!

## Variation 7: Character Identification from Dialogue
Identify who is speaking based on audio and visual clues.

**Example:**
Watch the scene. Three children are talking: Tom, Emma, and Sarah.

**You see:** Tom wearing red, Emma wearing blue, Sarah wearing green

**You hear:**
- "I love playing football!"
- "My favorite subject is Art."
- "I have a dog called Max."

**Match:**
Who said what?
- "I love playing football!" → _____ (based on lip movement or context)
- "My favorite subject is Art." → _____
- "I have a dog called Max." → _____

## Variation 8: Chart/Graph with Audio Explanation
Listen to explanation of data while viewing charts.

**Example:**
Look at the bar chart showing favorite sports in Class 5.

[Chart showing: Football-15, Swimming-10, Basketball-8, Tennis-5]

**Listen to the teacher explain:**
"As you can see, football is the most popular sport in our class. Fifteen students chose it. Swimming came second with ten students. Basketball and tennis were less popular."

**Answer:**
1. How many students like football? _____
2. Which sport is least popular? _____
3. True or False: More students like basketball than swimming. _____
4. How many students are in the class total? _____

## Variation 9: Animated Story Comprehension
Watch a short cartoon and answer story questions.

**Example:**
Watch the cartoon "The Lost Kitten"

**Story elements:**
- **Characters you see:** _____
- **Setting (where):** _____
- **Problem (what you hear/see):** _____
- **Solution:** _____

**Detailed questions:**
1. What color is the kitten? (visual) _____
2. Why is the kitten sad? (audio) _____
3. Who helps the kitten? (visual + audio) _____
4. How does the story end? _____

## Variation 10: Subtitle Matching Task
Watch a short clip with incorrect subtitles. Identify mistakes.

**Example:**
Watch the clip. The subtitles have {{number}} mistakes. Find them!

**Video shows:** Girl eating ice cream in park

**Subtitles say:** "I love eating pizza in the garden. It's so cold today!"

**Mistakes:**
1. She's eating _____ not pizza. (ice cream)
2. She's in the _____ not garden. (park)
3. She says it's _____ not cold. (hot/warm)

**Correct version:**
"I love eating ice cream in the park. It's so warm today!"
