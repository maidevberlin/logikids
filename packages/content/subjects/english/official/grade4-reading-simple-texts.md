# Reading - Simple Texts

Create tasks that help students read and understand short, simple English texts in various formats.

**Task Requirements:**
- Use authentic or authentic-style texts (stories, emails, posters, signs, simple websites)
- Keep texts short (50-150 words)
- Use simple, high-frequency vocabulary
- Include some visual support
- Ask comprehension questions (literal and simple inference)
- Practice different text types (narratives, informational, functional)
- Make reading purposeful and engaging
- Age-appropriate for 9-10 year olds

**Generate varied task types:**

## Variation 1: Short Story Comprehension
Present a simple narrative and test comprehension.

**Example:**
Read the story:

> **Tom's School Trip**
>
> Last Tuesday, Tom went on a school trip to the zoo with his class. They saw lions, elephants, and monkeys. Tom's favorite animal was the penguin. The penguins were swimming and playing in the water. Tom took lots of photos. At lunchtime, everyone ate sandwiches in the park. It was a sunny day and Tom had a great time!

Answer these questions:
1. Where did Tom go? (*to the zoo*)
2. When did he go? (*last Tuesday*)
3. What was his favorite animal? (*the penguin*)
4. What was the weather like? (*sunny*)
5. What did they eat for lunch? (*sandwiches*)

## Variation 2: Email from a Pen Pal
Read an email and extract information.

**Example:**
You received this email from your British pen pal:

> Hi!
>
> My name is Emma and I'm 10 years old. I live in London with my family. I have one brother and a dog called Max.
>
> My favorite subject at school is Art. I love drawing and painting. After school, I usually play football with my friends. On weekends, I like reading books and riding my bike.
>
> What do you like to do? Please write back!
>
> Emma

**Tasks:**
1. Complete Emma's profile:
   - Age: _____
   - From: _____
   - Pets: _____
   - Favorite subject: _____
   - Hobbies: _____, _____, _____, _____

2. True or False:
   - Emma has a sister. (False)
   - She likes Art. (True)
   - She plays basketball. (False)

## Variation 3: Event Poster Information
Read a poster and find specific information.

**Example:**

> **SCHOOL SUMMER FAIR**
>
> Saturday, July 10th
> 2:00 PM - 5:00 PM
> School Playground
>
> Activities:
> - Face painting
> - Games
> - Cake sale
> - Music performance
>
> Entry: £2 (children), £5 (adults)
>
> Everyone welcome!

Answer:
- When is the fair? (day and time) _____
- Where is it? _____
- How much does it cost for children? _____
- Name {{number}} activities you can do there: _____

## Variation 4: Simple Recipe Reading
Read a simple recipe and understand the steps.

**Example:**

> **Chocolate Milkshake**
>
> You need:
> - 2 cups of milk
> - 3 spoons of chocolate powder
> - Ice cream (vanilla or chocolate)
> - Ice cubes
>
> Instructions:
> 1. Put the milk in a blender.
> 2. Add the chocolate powder.
> 3. Add 2 scoops of ice cream.
> 4. Add some ice cubes.
> 5. Blend for 30 seconds.
> 6. Pour into a glass and enjoy!

**Tasks:**
- What do you need? Make a shopping list: _____
- How many steps are there? _____
- What do you do first? _____
- How long do you blend it? _____
- Put the pictures in the correct order (if images provided)

## Variation 5: Profile or "About Me" Text
Read someone's profile and understand personal information.

**Example:**

> **Meet Sarah**
>
> Name: Sarah Johnson
> Age: 10
> From: New York, USA
> Languages: English and Spanish
> Family: Mum, Dad, and little sister (Amy, 6)
> Pets: A cat called Whiskers
> Hobbies: Swimming, reading, playing guitar
> Favorite food: Pizza
> Favorite color: Purple

**Tasks:**
1. Fill in the information:
   | Question | Answer |
   |----------|--------|
   | How old is Sarah? | _____ |
   | Where is she from? | _____ |
   | What languages does she speak? | _____ |
   | What's her pet's name? | _____ |

2. Write {{number}} things Sarah and you have in common.

## Variation 6: Simple Instructions or Rules
Read and understand practical instructions.

**Example:**

> **Library Rules**
>
> 1. Be quiet in the library.
> 2. You can borrow 3 books for 2 weeks.
> 3. Return books on time.
> 4. Handle books carefully.
> 5. No food or drinks allowed.
> 6. Ask the librarian if you need help.

**Tasks:**
- How many books can you borrow? _____
- How long can you keep them? _____
- Can you eat in the library? _____
- What should you do if you need help? _____
- Match the rule to the picture (if provided)

## Variation 7: Short Informational Text
Read factual information and answer questions.

**Example:**

> **Penguins**
>
> Penguins are birds, but they cannot fly. They are very good swimmers and can swim very fast. Penguins live in cold places, mostly in Antarctica. They eat fish and other sea animals.
>
> Penguins are black and white. Baby penguins are grey and fluffy. Penguins live in large groups called colonies. A group of penguins can have thousands of birds!

**Questions:**
1. Can penguins fly? _____
2. Where do they live? _____
3. What do they eat? _____
4. What color are baby penguins? _____
5. What is a group of penguins called? _____

## Variation 8: Dialogue or Conversation Reading
Read a simple dialogue and understand the exchange.

**Example:**

> **At the Shop**
>
> Tom: Hello! Do you have any chocolate ice cream?
> Shop assistant: Yes, we do. How many scoops would you like?
> Tom: Two scoops, please. How much is it?
> Shop assistant: That's £3, please.
> Tom: Here you are.
> Shop assistant: Thank you! Enjoy your ice cream!
> Tom: Thank you!

**Tasks:**
- What does Tom want? _____
- How many scoops does he order? _____
- How much does it cost? _____
- Role play: Practice the dialogue with a partner

## Variation 9: Schedule or Timetable Reading
Read and interpret a simple schedule.

**Example:**

> **Saturday Activities**
>
> | Time | Activity |
> |------|----------|
> | 9:00 - 10:00 | Football practice |
> | 10:30 - 11:30 | Music lesson |
> | 12:00 - 13:00 | Lunch with family |
> | 14:00 - 16:00 | Visit to the museum |
> | 18:00 | Dinner |

**Questions:**
- What time is football practice? _____
- How long is the music lesson? _____
- What happens at 2 PM (14:00)? _____
- What does he do before lunch? _____
- When is dinner? _____

## Variation 10: Birthday Invitation or Card
Read an invitation or greeting card.

**Example:**

> **You're Invited to My Birthday Party!**
>
> Name: Sophie
> When: Saturday, March 12th, 3:00 PM
> Where: My house, 45 Park Road
> Activities: Games, music, dancing, treasure hunt
> Food: Pizza, snacks, birthday cake
> Please bring: Nothing - just come and have fun!
> RSVP: Please tell me by March 5th if you can come
> Call: 555-1234

**Tasks:**
- When is the party? (day, date, time) _____
- Where is it? _____
- What will you do there? (Name {{number}} activities) _____
- What should you bring? _____
- By when should you reply? _____
- Write a short reply: "Thank you for the invitation. I can/can't come because..."
