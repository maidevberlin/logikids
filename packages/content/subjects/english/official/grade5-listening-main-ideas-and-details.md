# Listening - Main Ideas and Details

Create a listening comprehension task where students identify main ideas and extract important details from a short spoken text.

## Task Variations

### 1. School Announcement Comprehension
Present a **school announcement** (read aloud or described) about {{topic}} and ask students to identify:
- The **main purpose** of the announcement
- **Key details** (who, what, when, where)
- What action students should take

Example topics: field trip, schedule change, school event, lost and found

### 2. Conversation Understanding
Provide a **dialogue between two people** discussing {{situation}} and have students:
- Identify the **main topic** being discussed
- Note **specific details** mentioned
- Understand the **speakers' intentions** or plans

Example situations: weekend plans, hobby activities, restaurant order, shopping trip

### 3. Short Story with Details
Present a **simple narrative** about {{character}} and ask students to:
- Summarize the **main idea** in one sentence
- List **three important details** from the story
- Answer comprehension questions about the sequence of events

Example characters: a student's adventure, family vacation, sports competition

### 4. Instructions and Directions
Give **step-by-step instructions** for {{activity}} and have students:
- Identify the **goal** of the instructions
- Remember the **correct order** of steps
- Extract **specific details** (materials needed, timing)

Example activities: making a sandwich, playing a game, finding a location on a map

### 5. Weather Forecast Analysis
Present a **weather forecast** for {{location}} and ask students to:
- Determine the **overall weather pattern**
- Extract **specific information** (temperature, precipitation, wind)
- Make a **simple prediction** about what to wear or do

| Information Type | What to Listen For |
|------------------|-------------------|
| Main Idea | Overall topic or purpose |
| Details | Numbers, names, times, places |
| Gist | General meaning without every word |

### 6. Interview or News Report
Provide a **short interview** or **news segment** about {{topic}} and have students:
- Identify the **main subject** being reported
- Note **key facts** mentioned
- Distinguish between **main points** and **supporting details**

Example topics: new playground, school sports team, local event, student achievement

### 7. Making Predictions
Play **part of a story** or **situation** and have students:
- Understand what has happened so far (main idea)
- Note **clues** about what might happen next
- Make **reasonable predictions** based on details heard

Use context clues, character actions, and situational details to guide predictions.

### 8. Public Announcements
Present an **announcement** in a public setting ({{location}}) and ask students to:
- Identify the **purpose** of the announcement
- Extract **important information** for listeners
- Determine **who** should pay attention and **why**

Example locations: train station, airport, shopping mall, museum

**Important**: All listening texts should be **age-appropriate**, use **clear pronunciation**, contain **familiar vocabulary** with some new words inferable from context, and be **short enough** (30-60 seconds) to maintain attention while practicing main idea and detail extraction.
