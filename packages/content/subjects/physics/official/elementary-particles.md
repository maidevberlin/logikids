# Elementary Particles Tasks

Create physics problems that explore the fundamental building blocks of matter and the Standard Model of particle physics. Problems should help students understand the classification of particles, their properties, and how quarks combine to form composite particles.

**Vary the problem structure:**
- **Quark composition of hadrons**: Determine the quark content of baryons (protons, neutrons) and mesons, calculate total charge using quark charges ($u = +\frac{2}{3}e$, $d = -\frac{1}{3}e$, $s = -\frac{1}{3}e$)
- **Particle classification**: Given a list of particles (electrons, photons, protons, quarks, neutrinos, gluons), classify them as elementary particles, composite particles, fermions, or bosons
- **Charge and quantum number calculations**: Calculate total electric charge, baryon number, or lepton number for particle combinations or decay processes
- **Matter-antimatter pairs**: Identify antiparticles for given particles (electron/positron, proton/antiproton, quark/antiquark), explain conservation laws in pair production and annihilation
- **Standard Model organization**: Complete a table or diagram showing the three generations of quarks and leptons with their properties (mass, charge, spin)
- **Hadron identification**: Given quark combinations like $uud$, $udd$, $u\bar{u}$, or $d\bar{s}$, identify whether it's a baryon or meson and name the particle if possible
- **Conservation laws in particle interactions**: Check whether proposed particle reactions conserve charge, baryon number, and lepton number, determine if reactions are allowed
- **Fundamental vs composite**: Distinguish between elementary particles (quarks, leptons, gauge bosons) and composite particles (protons, neutrons, pions, kaons)

**Vary the content/context:**
- **Standard Model structure**: Three generations of matter, fermions vs bosons, matter particles and force carriers
- **Quarks**: Six flavors (up, down, charm, strange, top, bottom), fractional charges, color charge, quark confinement, never observed freely
- **Leptons**: Electron, muon, tau and their associated neutrinos, integer charges, no color charge
- **Hadrons**: Baryons (three quarks like protons, neutrons, lambda particles), mesons (quark-antiquark pairs like pions, kaons)
- **Force carriers**: Photon (electromagnetic), gluons (strong force), W and Z bosons (weak force), graviton (theoretical)
- **Antimatter**: Antiparticles with opposite charge, pair production and annihilation, matter-antimatter asymmetry in universe
- **Particle accelerators**: How particles are discovered and studied at facilities like LHC, creating particles from energy
- **Cosmic rays**: High-energy particles from space, particle showers in atmosphere, detecting rare particles

**Vary the complexity based on age:**
- **For age 17**: Focus on basic classification (elementary vs composite, fermions vs bosons), simple quark composition of proton and neutron, qualitative understanding of Standard Model structure, charge calculations for basic hadrons
- **For age 18**: Include all six quark flavors, three generations of leptons, detailed hadron composition, conservation law applications, understanding of confinement principle, force carrier identification
- **For age 19**: Complex particle reactions with multiple conservation laws, quantum number calculations, strangeness and other quantum numbers, particle decay chains, experimental detection methods, relationship to fundamental forces

**LaTeX for formulas and equations:**
- Quark charges: $Q_u = +\frac{2}{3}e$, $Q_d = -\frac{1}{3}e$, $Q_s = -\frac{1}{3}e$
- Particle compositions: $p = uud$, $n = udd$, $\pi^+ = u\bar{d}$, $\pi^- = d\bar{u}$
- Conservation laws: $Q_{\text{before}} = Q_{\text{after}}$, $B_{\text{before}} = B_{\text{after}}$, $L_{\text{before}} = L_{\text{after}}$
- Pair production: $E \geq 2m_ec^2$ for electron-positron pairs
- Annihilation: $e^+ + e^- \rightarrow \gamma + \gamma$

**Adjust difficulty with {{difficulty}}:**
- **Easy**: Basic quark composition (proton, neutron, pion), simple classification of particles, charge calculations for simple hadrons, identifying elementary vs composite particles
- **Medium**: All six quarks and leptons, hadron identification from quark content, applying single conservation laws, understanding three generations, force carrier identification
- **Hard**: Multiple conservation laws simultaneously, predicting allowed/forbidden reactions, calculating quantum numbers, particle decay chains, relating Standard Model to fundamental forces, experimental evidence and detection

**Include variety in numerical values and contexts:**
- Different quark combinations: $uud$, $udd$, $uds$, $uus$, $dds$, $sss$ for baryons
- Various mesons: $u\bar{d}$, $d\bar{u}$, $u\bar{u}$, $d\bar{d}$, $s\bar{s}$, $c\bar{s}$
- Particle masses ranging from 0.511 MeV (electron) to 173 GeV (top quark)
- Different quantum numbers for various particles
- Real particle names: proton, neutron, lambda, sigma, xi, pion, kaon, eta
- Conservation scenarios: allowed reactions, forbidden reactions, multiple conservation checks
- Ensure variety in which particles and combinations are featured
