# Legends and Myths

Create a task about legends and myths for {{age}}-year-old {{gender}} at difficulty level {{difficulty}}.

## Task Variations

Choose ONE of these variations:

1. **Legend vs. Fairy Tale Comparison**
   - Create a comparison table
   - Categories: **Setting**, **Characters**, **Time**, **Truth Element**, **Magic**
   - Examples: Legends have real places, fairy tales have "Once upon a time"
   - Sort 4-6 short texts into correct category

2. **Real vs. Fantastic Elements**
   - Present a German legend (e.g., Lorelei, Pied Piper)
   - Students create two lists: **Real Elements** | **Fantastic Elements**
   - Real: Rhine River, town of Hamelin
   - Fantastic: magical singing, enchanted rats
   - Explain the mix of reality and fantasy

3. **Historical Context**
   - Read a legend
   - Identify when and where it might have originated
   - Discuss: What was life like then? Why did people create this story?
   - Connect legend to historical events or places

4. **Local Legend Research**
   - Students write about a legend from their region
   - Include: place name, main characters, what happens, lesson/explanation
   - Structure in 4 paragraphs: **Introduction**, **Setting**, **Story**, **Meaning**

5. **Explaining Natural Phenomena**
   - Many legends explain natural events
   - Present 3 legends explaining thunder, mountains, rivers, etc.
   - Students identify what each legend tries to explain
   - Write their own legend explaining something natural (echo, rainbow, seasons)

6. **Character Types in Legends**
   - Identify typical legend characters
   - Types: heroes, mythical creatures, historical figures, spirits
   - Table: **Character Type** | **Example** | **Role in Story**
   - Compare to fairy tale characters (kings, princesses, witches)

7. **Legend Structure Analysis**
   - Break down a legend into parts
   - Parts: **Historical/Real Beginning** → **Mysterious Event** → **Explanation/Moral**
   - Show how legends blend fact and fiction
   - Identify each part in a sample legend

8. **Creating a Place Legend**
   - Choose a local place (building, mountain, lake)
   - Invent a legend explaining its name or a special feature
   - Include: real details about the place, magical event, why people remember it
   - Write in legend style (serious tone, mix of real and fantastic)
