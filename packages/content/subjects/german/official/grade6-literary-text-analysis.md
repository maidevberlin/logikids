---
id: grade6-literary-text-analysis
name: Literary Text Analysis
description: Analyzing narratives, myths, poems, and young adult literature through character analysis, theme identification, literary devices, and cultural exploration
grade: 6
focus: Analytical reading and interpretation of literary texts
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Understand fictional worlds and explore different cultures, particularly ancient civilizations
  - Empathize with literary characters to examine personal values and perspectives
  - Analyze form, theme, structure, character development, setting, and time period in narratives
  - Identify and interpret literary devices including metaphor, simile, and personification
  - Read and analyze complete children's or young adult novels critically
  - Use creative methods such as continuations and retellings to deepen understanding
  - Compare and contrast themes across different literary texts
problem_types:
  - Character trait analysis
  - Theme identification
  - Plot structure analysis
  - Setting and time period description
  - Literary device recognition
  - Narrative perspective analysis
  - Text-to-self connections
  - Cultural context exploration
  - Symbolic interpretation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Explicit character traits stated directly in text
    - Clear central themes with straightforward messages
    - Simple linear plot structures with obvious beginning, middle, and end
  medium:
    - Character traits requiring inference from actions and dialogue
    - Multiple themes or layers of meaning requiring interpretation
    - Complex plot structures with subplots, flashbacks, or parallel storylines
  hard:
    - Ambiguous characters with conflicting motivations or moral complexity
    - Symbolic meanings and implicit themes requiring deep analysis
    - Comparative analysis of multiple texts or cultural perspectives
prerequisites:
  - grade4-literary-response
  - grade4-reading-comprehension
real_world_context:
  - Reading myths and legends to understand ancient cultures and human values across time
  - Analyzing characters in novels to develop empathy and understand different perspectives
  - Recognizing literary devices in poetry, song lyrics, and everyday language
  - Discussing book club selections and defending interpretations with textual evidence
  - Creating fan fiction or alternative endings to explore narrative possibilities
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid university-level literary theory or overly complex terminology
  - Don't require memorization of author biographies or publication dates
  - Avoid texts with mature themes inappropriate for 11-12 year olds
  - Don't expect single correct interpretations that ignore valid alternative readings
  - Avoid cultural references that require extensive background knowledge
