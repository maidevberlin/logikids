---
id: grade10-formal-discourse
name: Formal Discourse
description: Participating in academic discussions, building on others' contributions, and using evidence
grade: 10
focus: Speaking and listening in academic contexts
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Contribute substantively to academic discussions with evidence-based arguments
  - Build on others' contributions by extending, challenging, or synthesizing ideas
  - Use textual evidence and examples to support spoken arguments
  - Moderate and facilitate academic discussions effectively
  - Navigate intellectual disagreement productively and respectfully
  - Ask probing questions that advance the discussion
  - Synthesize multiple viewpoints into coherent understanding
problem_types:
  - Discussion contribution
  - Moderator role
  - Response to peer
  - Question formulation
  - Synthesis statement
  - Challenge position
  - Fishbowl discussion
  - Seminar presentation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Contributions state personal opinions with one piece of evidence
    - Questions ask for clarification or basic information
    - Discussions focus on concrete, accessible topics
  medium:
    - Contributions connect multiple speakers' ideas with evidence
    - Questions probe assumptions or implications
    - Discussions require balancing competing perspectives
  hard:
    - Contributions synthesize complex arguments from multiple sources
    - Questions reveal gaps or contradictions in reasoning
    - Discussions address abstract concepts or multi-layered issues
real_world_context:
  - A literature seminar discusses whether Faust bears responsibility for Gretchen's fate
  - Philosophy students debate the ethics of civil disobedience in a democracy
  - History class examines multiple interpretations of a controversial historical event
  - Environmental forum weighs economic development against conservation priorities
  - Ethics seminar explores the tension between individual freedom and collective good
  - Students facilitate a fishbowl discussion on media representation and stereotypes
  - Science and society seminar debates genetic engineering regulations
  - Book club moderator guides discussion on narrative perspective and reliability
  - Mock academic conference where students present and discuss research findings
  - Interdisciplinary panel explores connections between art, politics, and social change
  - Poetry workshop participants discuss how form shapes meaning in modern verse
  - Cultural studies seminar examines globalization's impact on local traditions
  - Students moderate town hall on school policy changes affecting student life
  - Literature circle explores how authors from different epochs treat similar themes
  - Gender studies discussion analyzes representation in classic and contemporary texts
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid topics requiring specialized knowledge beyond grade 10 curriculum
  - Don't create discussions where only one position is defensible
  - Avoid scenarios where productive disagreement is impossible
  - Don't ask questions with single correct answers only
  - Avoid discussions inappropriate for 15-16 year olds
