Generate a task where {{childName}} practices recognizing nouns and capitalizing them, using articles (der, die, das) as clues.

**Focus areas to choose from:**
- Identifying nouns using articles
- Recognizing naming words (people, animals, things)
- Capitalizing nouns in sentences
- Distinguishing nouns from other words
- Using the article test (der/die/das)

## Task Variations

### Variation 1: Article Detective
Present {{num1}} sentences with articles. The student identifies the noun that follows each article and capitalizes it.

Examples:
- *der hund bellt* → *der Hund bellt*
- *die katze schläft* → *die Katze schläft*
- *das haus ist groß* → *das Haus ist groß*
- *ein ball rollt* → *ein Ball rollt*

### Variation 2: Noun Identification
Show {{num1}} sentences. The student circles or underlines all the nouns and checks if they're capitalized.

Example:
*ich sehe den baum. der hund läuft. mama kauft brot.*

Nouns to identify and capitalize:
- *Baum*
- *Hund*
- *Mama*
- *Brot*

### Variation 3: Article Test Practice
Provide {{num1}} words. The student uses the article test (can you put der, die, or das in front?) to determine if it's a noun.

| Word | Article Test | Is it a Noun? | Capitalize? |
|------|--------------|---------------|-------------|
| *hund* | der Hund ✓ | Yes | **Hund** |
| *laufen* | der laufen ✗ | No | laufen |
| *ball* | der Ball ✓ | Yes | **Ball** |
| *schnell* | der schnell ✗ | No | schnell |

### Variation 4: Rewrite with Capitals
Present {{num1}} sentences where nouns are not capitalized. The student rewrites them correctly.

Examples:
- *der hund spielt mit dem ball.* → *Der Hund spielt mit dem Ball.*
- *mama kauft einen apfel.* → *Mama kauft einen Apfel.*
- *das kind geht zur schule.* → *Das Kind geht zur Schule.*

### Variation 5: Noun or Not?
Show {{num1}} words from a sentence. The student sorts them into **Nomen** (nouns - capitalize) and **Keine Nomen** (not nouns - lowercase).

Example word list:
*der, Hund, ist, groß, und, braun*

Sorted:
- **Nomen**: Hund
- **Keine Nomen**: der, ist, groß, und, braun

### Variation 6: Find the Naming Words
Present {{num1}} sentences. The student identifies all "naming words" (people, animals, things, places) and capitalizes them.

Examples:
- *lisa hat eine katze.* → People: *Lisa*, Things: *Katze*
- *der park ist schön.* → Places: *Park*
- *opa liest ein buch.* → People: *Opa*, Things: *Buch*

### Variation 7: Mixed Capitalization Practice
Show a short text with {{num1}} sentences containing multiple capitalization needs (sentence starts AND nouns). The student corrects all capital letters.

Example text:
*ich gehe in den park. dort sehe ich einen hund und eine katze. mama ruft mich.*

Corrected:
*Ich gehe in den Park. Dort sehe ich einen Hund und eine Katze. Mama ruft mich.*

### Variation 8: Article + Noun Matching
Provide {{num1}} articles in one column and {{num1}} nouns in another. The student matches them and writes the pairs with correct capitalization.

| Articles | Nouns (lowercase) | Correct Match |
|----------|-------------------|---------------|
| der | ball | der Ball |
| die | katze | die Katze |
| das | haus | das Haus |
| ein | hund | ein Hund |

This reinforces both article-noun agreement and capitalization.
