---
id: grade10-media-production
name: Media Production
description: Creating multimedia content with purpose - podcasts, video essays, infographics
grade: 10
focus: Media literacy and multimedia communication
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Create multimedia content with clear purpose and target audience
  - Apply design principles to visual and audio communication
  - Write scripts and storyboards for audio and video content
  - Edit and revise multimedia projects for clarity and impact
  - Reflect critically on media choices and their effects on audience
  - Integrate multiple media formats into cohesive projects
  - Use digital tools appropriately for content creation
problem_types:
  - Podcast script
  - Video essay storyboard
  - Infographic design
  - Audio editing
  - Visual storytelling
  - Content planning
  - Platform adaptation
  - Multimodal composition
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single medium focus (audio OR visual, not combined)
    - Clear template or structure provided
    - Content for familiar audience and simple purpose
  medium:
    - Combining two media formats effectively
    - Adapting content for specific platform requirements
    - Creating content requiring research and synthesis
  hard:
    - Complex multimodal projects integrating 3+ elements
    - Adapting single content for multiple platforms/audiences
    - Projects requiring sophisticated editing and design choices
real_world_context:
  - A student podcast explaining climate science to younger students
  - Creating an infographic about healthy eating habits for school newsletter
  - Producing a video essay analyzing a novel for literature class
  - Designing Instagram stories teaching vocabulary to language learners
  - Recording audio guide for a local museum exhibit about regional history
  - Making a documentary-style video about student exchange experiences
  - Creating a tutorial video series on math concepts for peer tutoring
  - Producing a podcast episode interviewing local community activists
  - Designing an interactive timeline infographic about historical events
  - Making a video book review using clips, music, and narration
  - Creating a photo essay with captions about daily life in your city
  - Producing a public service announcement about recycling for social media
  - Making an audio drama adaptation of a short story
  - Designing a visual explainer about the school's solar panel project
  - Creating a YouTube video essay comparing two films or books
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid requiring expensive software or specialized equipment
  - Don't focus on technical skills over communication purpose
  - Avoid projects too complex to complete in reasonable timeframe
  - Don't neglect accessibility considerations (captions, descriptions)
  - Avoid topics requiring production values beyond student capability
version: 1
