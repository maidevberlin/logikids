---
id: grade7-discussion-debate
name: Discussion and Debate Skills
description: Engage in democratic dialogue and formal debate, resolve conflicts constructively, present and defend viewpoints with structured arguments, and address opposing positions with reasoned counter-arguments
grade: 7
focus: Democratic dialogue, formal debate, and evidence-based argumentation
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Navigate various conversation types using appropriate linguistic patterns and register
  - Demonstrate democratic dialogue practices including respect for diverse opinions and turn equity
  - Present personal viewpoints clearly with supporting arguments and relevant evidence
  - Summarize others' positions accurately before addressing their reasoning
  - Apply conflict resolution strategies that acknowledge multiple valid perspectives
  - Distinguish between facts and opinions when constructing or evaluating arguments
  - Lead or moderate structured discussions while maintaining neutrality and fairness
problem_types:
  - Democratic dialogue practice
  - Conflict resolution strategy
  - Viewpoint presentation
  - Position summarization
  - Counter-argument formulation
  - Fact-opinion distinction
  - Debate moderation
  - Register adaptation
  - Multi-perspective synthesis
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single-issue topics with clear pro/con positions and straightforward supporting arguments
    - Conflict scenarios with obvious resolution paths and 2-3 stakeholders
    - Distinguishing explicit facts from clearly marked opinions in discussion transcripts
  medium:
    - Multi-faceted topics requiring comparison of 3-4 competing viewpoints with tradeoffs
    - Leading small group discussions requiring neutral facilitation and fair time allocation
    - Summarizing complex positions before formulating counter-arguments with supporting evidence
  hard:
    - Nuanced conflicts requiring synthesis of multiple valid perspectives into compromise solutions
    - Adapting register and argumentation style across informal peer debates and formal structured debates
    - Analyzing discussion transcripts to identify logical fallacies, unsupported claims, and effective rhetoric
prerequisites:
  - grade6-discussion-argumentation
real_world_context:
  - Participating in mock town hall meetings about community issues, presenting citizen perspectives with supporting data
  - Moderating peer mediation sessions to facilitate conflict resolution between classmates with opposing viewpoints
  - Debating ethical dilemmas in literature class, such as character decisions in novels or historical moral choices
  - Leading student council discussions where members propose and debate school improvement initiatives
  - Engaging in science debates about environmental policies, weighing economic and ecological considerations
  - Participating in Model United Nations or Model European Parliament simulations at school
  - Leading class discussions about controversial topics in ethics, politics, or current events
  - Moderating student council meetings or club discussions with proper speaking protocols
  - Preparing for formal debate competitions or speech tournaments at regional level
  - Facilitating peer mediation sessions to resolve conflicts between classmates constructively
  - Conducting interviews with community members for school journalism or research projects
  - Organizing fishbowl discussions or panel debates for school assemblies on important topics
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid topics requiring specialized political or legal knowledge
  - Don't expect formal parliamentary debate rules or advanced rhetoric
  - Avoid conflicts with objectively correct answers instead of valid perspectives
  - Don't include more than 5 distinct positions or stakeholders
  - Avoid emotionally charged topics requiring mature emotional regulation
