id: grade11-enlightenment-literature
name: Enlightenment Literature
description: Analyzing Aufklärung texts and ideas through works like Lessing's Nathan der Weise and Emilia Galotti
grade: 11
focus: Enlightenment ideals and literary analysis
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Understand core Enlightenment ideals including reason, tolerance, progress, and humanism
  - Analyze key Enlightenment texts and authors in their historical and philosophical context
  - Connect Enlightenment literature to the broader intellectual movement of the 18th century
  - Identify how Enlightenment ideas influenced later literary movements and social change
  - Write analytical essays examining Enlightenment texts with attention to both form and ideas
  - Analyze the tension between reason and emotion in Enlightenment dramatic works
  - Evaluate the relevance of Enlightenment ideals to contemporary society
problem_types:
  - Ideal identification
  - Philosophical analysis
  - Historical contextualization
  - Character examination
  - Dialogue analysis
  - Theme tracing
  - Contemporary relevance
  - Movement comparison
  - Argument structure
  - Symbolic interpretation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify basic Enlightenment ideals like reason, tolerance, and education in texts
    - Recognize key Enlightenment authors and their major works
    - Understand the historical context of the Aufklärung movement
  medium:
    - Analyze how specific scenes embody Enlightenment philosophy
    - Compare Enlightenment ideals with earlier and later literary movements
    - Examine the relationship between dramatic form and philosophical content
  hard:
    - Develop thesis-driven interpretations connecting text to broader Enlightenment thought
    - Evaluate tensions and contradictions within Enlightenment philosophy as shown in literature
    - Analyze how Enlightenment authors use literary techniques to advance philosophical arguments
real_world_context:
  - A theater performs Nathan der Weise to promote interfaith dialogue in a diverse city
  - Students debate whether Lessing's ring parable offers solutions to modern religious conflicts
  - Analyzing Emilia Galotti as a critique of aristocratic power and its contemporary parallels
  - A philosophy seminar explores how Enlightenment ideals shaped modern democracy
  - Comparing Enlightenment tolerance with today's debates about free speech and pluralism
  - A museum exhibit connects Enlightenment literature to the American and French Revolutions
  - Students examine how Nathan's character embodies the ideal of the rational, tolerant individual
  - A book club discusses whether Emilia Galotti is a tragedy of virtue or a feminist critique
  - Analyzing how Lessing uses dramatic form to make philosophical ideas accessible
  - A documentary explores the influence of Enlightenment thinkers on German literature
  - Students trace the evolution from religious authority to rational inquiry in 18th-century texts
  - Comparing Lessing's portrayal of religious tolerance with contemporary interfaith initiatives
  - A seminar examines how Enlightenment education ideals influenced modern schooling
  - Analyzing the conflict between individual autonomy and social duty in Aufklärung drama
  - Students explore how Enlightenment authors challenged censorship and promoted free thought
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid reducing complex Enlightenment texts to simple moral lessons
  - Do not treat all Enlightenment authors as having identical views
  - Avoid anachronistic interpretations that ignore 18th-century context
  - Do not analyze philosophical content without attention to literary form
  - Avoid tasks requiring knowledge of texts not commonly taught at grade 11
