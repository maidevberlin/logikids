---
id: grade6-informational-texts
name: Informational Texts
description: Non-fiction texts, using reference materials, evaluating diagrams and graphics
grade: 6
focus: Research skills and non-fiction text analysis
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Work with non-fiction texts including reference books, encyclopedias, and dictionaries
  - Use diagrams and informational graphics effectively to extract information
  - Gather information for presentations and research projects from multiple sources
  - Navigate library classification systems for research purposes
  - Use age-appropriate search engines safely and effectively
  - Evaluate information source reliability through formal criteria
  - Distinguish primary from secondary sources
problem_types:
  - Information location
  - Graphic interpretation
  - Source evaluation
  - Dictionary use
  - Research planning
  - Fact verification
  - Encyclopedia navigation
  - Primary vs secondary distinction
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single clear source with explicit information
    - Simple graphics with clear labels
    - Basic dictionary or reference book use
  medium:
    - Multiple sources requiring comparison of information
    - Complex graphics requiring interpretation
    - Evaluating source reliability using provided criteria
  hard:
    - Evaluating conflicting sources and synthesizing information
    - Analyzing complex diagrams with implicit relationships
    - Distinguishing primary from secondary sources independently
prerequisites:
  - grade4-reading-comprehension
real_world_context:
  - Using encyclopedias and reference books to research school project topics
  - Interpreting diagrams and charts in science textbooks and magazines
  - Evaluating website reliability when searching for information online
  - Using dictionaries to understand unfamiliar words in reading materials
  - Comparing information from multiple sources to verify accuracy
  - Reading museum exhibit labels and informational plaques to learn about historical artifacts
  - Following assembly instructions with diagrams when building models or furniture
  - Using library catalog systems to locate books on specific topics for research assignments
  - Consulting field guides to identify plants, birds, or insects during nature exploration
  - Reading nutrition labels and ingredient lists to make informed food choices
  - Comparing product reviews from different websites before making purchase recommendations
  - Researching ancient civilizations using age-appropriate reference materials for history presentations
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid sources with content significantly above grade 6 reading level
  - Don't use graphics that require specialized scientific knowledge
  - Avoid research tasks requiring access to unavailable resources
  - Don't ask source evaluation without providing criteria
  - Avoid topics unfamiliar to 11-12 year olds
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
