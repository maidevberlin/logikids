---
id: grade10-speech-analysis
name: Speech Analysis
description: Analyzing political and public speeches for structure, rhetoric, and persuasive strategies
grade: 10
focus: Speech analysis and political discourse
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Analyze the structure and composition of political and public speeches
  - Identify and evaluate persuasive strategies used by speakers
  - Examine the relationship between speaker, audience, and context
  - Analyze delivery and performance elements in speeches
  - Compare written speech texts with oral performance
problem_types:
  - Structure analysis
  - Rhetorical strategy
  - Audience analysis
  - Performance evaluation
  - Text-performance comparison
  - Historical context
  - Persuasive effectiveness
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify basic speech structure (introduction, main points, conclusion)
    - Recognize obvious rhetorical devices and their immediate effects
    - Describe the speaker's intended audience and general purpose
  medium:
    - Analyze how structure and rhetoric work together to achieve persuasion
    - Evaluate the effectiveness of persuasive strategies for specific audiences
    - Compare written text with delivery elements (tone, pauses, emphasis)
  hard:
    - Analyze complex rhetorical strategies and their interaction across the speech
    - Evaluate how historical and political context shapes speech effectiveness
    - Critically assess ethical dimensions of persuasive techniques used
real_world_context:
  - A chancellor delivers a televised address on national policy during a crisis
  - A student activist speaks at a climate march calling for immediate action
  - Martin Luther King Jr.'s "I Have a Dream" speech is analyzed in history class
  - A mayor presents a speech about urban development to the city council
  - Barack Obama's 2008 election victory speech inspires a generation
  - Greta Thunberg addresses world leaders at a UN climate summit
  - A historic speech by Willy Brandt shapes German-Polish relations
  - A CEO presents a company vision to shareholders and employees
  - Nelson Mandela's inaugural address marks the end of apartheid
  - A local politician campaigns for environmental protection measures
  - Angela Merkel addresses the Bundestag on European unity
  - A TED speaker uses storytelling to advocate for educational reform
  - John F. Kennedy's "Ich bin ein Berliner" speech resonates in Berlin
  - A graduation speaker motivates students to pursue their dreams
  - Winston Churchill's wartime speeches rally a nation during WWII
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid speeches with highly specialized political or historical knowledge
  - Do not focus solely on device identification without analyzing effects
  - Avoid speeches in languages other than German or English
  - Do not conflate written and oral elements without acknowledging differences
  - Avoid speeches with culturally obscure references for 15-year-olds
prerequisites:
  - grade9-rhetorical-devices
