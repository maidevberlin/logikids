---
id: grade1-writing-composition
name: Writing Composition & Process
description: Learn to plan, draft, and revise simple texts including narratives, journal entries, and informational writing
grade: 1
focus: Writing process and text creation
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Write about personally meaningful topics following prompts
  - Collect narrative elements from familiar text forms
  - Compose brief narratives with logical sequencing
  - Create simple informational texts with clear structure
  - Maintain personal journal entries about daily experiences
  - Identify successful elements in own and others' texts
  - Implement revision suggestions to improve writing
problem_types:
  - Story beginning completion
  - Sequence ordering
  - Journal entry creation
  - Information text writing
  - Narrative element selection
  - Revision identification
  - Text continuation
  - Topic-based composition
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - 2-3 sentence texts about familiar personal experiences
    - Simple chronological narratives (first, then, finally)
    - Visual prompts supporting writing (picture sequences)
  medium:
    - 4-5 sentence texts combining description and action
    - Journal entries with temporal markers (today, yesterday)
    - Brief informational texts about known topics (pets, family)
  hard:
    - 6-8 sentence narratives with clear beginning, middle, and end
    - Creative variations on familiar story patterns
    - Revision tasks requiring text improvement decisions
prerequisites:
  - grade1-handwriting
  - grade1-phonological-awareness
  - grade1-spelling
real_world_context:
  - Writing personal journal entries about school days and weekend activities
  - Creating short stories for classroom books or display boards
  - Composing simple instructions for games or activities peers can follow
  - Writing informational texts about favorite animals, hobbies, or family traditions
  - Drafting short letters or cards to friends, family, or pen pals
  - Documenting science observations about plants, weather, or classroom pets
  - Writing adventure stories featuring brave explorers or magical creatures
  - Creating "All About Me" books with drawings and descriptive sentences
  - Composing thank-you notes after field trips or guest speaker visits
  - Writing recipes for imaginary potions or real cooking projects
  - Drafting narratives about historical figures studied in social studies
  - Creating informational posters about ocean animals or space discoveries
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid requiring complex plot structures beyond simple chronology
  - Don't expect perfect spelling; focus on ideas and structure first
  - Avoid abstract topics unfamiliar to 6-7 year olds
  - Don't use revision tasks requiring grammatical terminology not yet learned
  - Avoid multi-paragraph texts; keep compositions to single paragraphs
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
---
