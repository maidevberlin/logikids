name: Creative Writing (Advanced)
description: Producing creative texts with literary awareness - short prose, poetry, experimental forms
grade: 13
focus: Creative text production with literary technique application
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Produce creative short prose demonstrating narrative technique mastery
  - Write poetry applying knowledge of form, meter, and imagery
  - Experiment with genre conventions and literary forms
  - Apply literary devices consciously to achieve specific effects
  - Revise creative texts with critical self-awareness
  - Reflect on the creative writing process and aesthetic choices
problem_types:
  - Short prose composition
  - Poetry creation
  - Genre experimentation
  - Perspective shifting
  - Stream of consciousness
  - Dialogue-driven scenes
  - Symbolic narrative
  - Form poetry
  - Prose poetry
  - Stylistic imitation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Write creative texts using familiar narrative structures
    - Apply basic literary devices (metaphor, dialogue, description)
    - Demonstrate awareness of genre conventions
  medium:
    - Experiment with narrative perspective and time structure
    - Integrate multiple literary techniques for coherent effects
    - Revise drafts addressing structure, style, and impact
  hard:
    - Subvert or hybridize genre conventions purposefully
    - Create multilayered texts with symbolic and structural complexity
    - Demonstrate sophisticated control over style, voice, and form
prerequisites:
  - grade11-literary-interpretation
  - grade12-prose-interpretation
  - grade11-lyric-analysis
real_world_context:
  - Writing a short story that reimagines a classic fairy tale from the villain's perspective
  - Composing a poem cycle exploring urban alienation through seasonal imagery
  - Creating a stream-of-consciousness piece capturing a character's final moments
  - Writing dialogue-only flash fiction revealing a relationship's dissolution
  - Experimenting with second-person narration in a piece about memory and loss
  - Composing concrete poetry where form reflects content (visual poems)
  - Writing a dystopian micro-fiction inspired by contemporary surveillance culture
  - Creating a prose poem blending lyrical language with narrative momentum
  - Imitating Kafka's style to write an absurdist bureaucratic nightmare
  - Writing interconnected vignettes that function as both fragments and whole
  - Composing a sonnet sequence exploring philosophical questions about identity
  - Creating magical realist fiction where metaphors become literal events
  - Writing an epistolary story told through letters, texts, or social media posts
  - Experimenting with unreliable narration that gradually reveals its deceptions
  - Composing found poetry from newspaper headlines or everyday conversations
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid prescriptive formulas that limit creative experimentation
  - Don't prioritize correctness over creative risk-taking and originality
  - Never dismiss genre fiction or experimental forms as less valid
  - Avoid evaluating creative writing purely on technical execution
  - Don't require knowledge of obscure literary movements or terminology
