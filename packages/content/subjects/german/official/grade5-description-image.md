# Description - Image

Create {{count}} varied tasks about **systematic image description** for a {{age}}-year-old student. Focus on {{focus_area}}.

## Task Variations

### Variation 1: Three-Layer Description
Students practice describing foreground, middle ground, and background.

**Example:**
"Describe this nature scene systematically:

**Image:** *A lake scene with ducks in front, a small island with trees in the middle, and mountains in the distance.*

**Use this structure:**
1. **Overall impression:** What type of scene is this? What mood does it create?
   *Example: 'Das Bild zeigt eine friedliche Seelandschaft...'*

2. **Foreground (Vordergrund):** What is closest to the viewer?
   *Example: 'Im Vordergrund sieht man...'*

3. **Middle ground (Mittelgrund):** What is in the center area?
   *Example: 'Im Mittelgrund befindet sich...'*

4. **Background (Hintergrund):** What is farthest away?
   *Example: 'Im Hintergrund erkennt man...'*"

### Variation 2: Spatial Relationships
Students describe where things are in relation to each other.

**Example:**
"Describe the positions of objects in this room scene:

**Image:** *A classroom with a teacher's desk in front, student desks in rows, a blackboard on the wall, windows on the right, a bookshelf on the left.*

**Use spatial vocabulary:**
- **links/rechts** (left/right)
- **oben/unten** (above/below)
- **vorne/hinten** (front/back)
- **neben/zwischen** (beside/between)
- **vor/hinter** (in front of/behind)

*Example: 'Die Tafel hängt **an der Wand**, **hinter** dem Lehrerpult. **Rechts** von der Tafel sind **Fenster**...'*"

### Variation 3: Focus and Composition
Students identify the main subject and analyze composition.

**Example:**
"Analyze this portrait photograph:

**Questions:**
1. **What is the focus?** What draws your attention first?
2. **Where is the person positioned?** (center, left, right, rule of thirds?)
3. **What is in focus?** What is blurred?
4. **What leads the eye?** (lines, colors, lighting?)
5. **What is the mood?** How is it created?

*Use descriptive adjectives: hell (bright), dunkel (dark), klar (clear), verschwommen (blurred), scharf (sharp)*"

### Variation 4: Color and Light Description
Students describe colors, lighting, and atmosphere.

**Example:**
"Describe the lighting and colors in this sunset scene:

**Color vocabulary:**
- **Warm colors:** rot, orange, gelb, golden
- **Cool colors:** blau, grün, violett
- **Brightness:** hell, dunkel, leuchtend, matt, blass

**Lighting vocabulary:**
- **direktes Licht** (direct light)
- **Schatten** (shadows)
- **Gegenlicht** (backlight)
- **Dämmerung** (twilight)

*Example: 'Der Himmel leuchtet in **warmen Orangetönen**. Das **Gegenlicht** der Sonne wirft lange **Schatten**...'*"

### Variation 5: From General to Specific
Students practice moving from overview to detail.

**Example:**
"Describe this market scene in three levels of detail:

**Level 1 - General overview (2-3 sentences):**
*What type of scene? Where? When? Overall mood?*
'Das Bild zeigt einen belebten Marktplatz...'

**Level 2 - Main elements (3-4 sentences):**
*What are the main features? Who/what is there?*
'In der Mitte stehen mehrere Marktstände...'

**Level 3 - Specific details (3-4 sentences):**
*What interesting details can you see?*
'Auf einem Stand liegen frisches Obst und Gemüse. Man erkennt rote Äpfel, gelbe Bananen...'"

### Variation 6: Objective vs. Subjective Description
Students distinguish between factual description and interpretation.

**Example:**
"Separate **objective facts** from **subjective interpretation** in this image description:

*'Das Bild zeigt einen alten Mann auf einer Bank. Er sieht sehr einsam aus. Es ist Winter, denn Schnee liegt auf dem Boden. Der Mann wirkt traurig und verlassen. Er trägt einen grauen Mantel. Die kahlen Bäume verstärken die melancholische Stimmung.'*

**Objective (facts you can see):**
- _______________________
- _______________________

**Subjective (interpretation/feelings):**
- _______________________
- _______________________

*Rewrite as a purely objective description.*"

### Variation 7: Describing for Accessibility
Students write descriptions for visually impaired readers.

**Example:**
"Write an accessibility description of this family photo:

**Requirements for accessible descriptions:**
✓ Describe people's positions and appearances
✓ Explain important background details
✓ Mention relevant colors and lighting
✓ Describe facial expressions and gestures
✓ Keep it clear and concise
✓ Use logical order (left to right, top to bottom)

*Example start: 'Das Foto zeigt vier Personen vor einem roten Backsteinhaus. Von links nach rechts: Ein Junge mit blonden Haaren, etwa 10 Jahre alt, trägt...'*"

### Variation 8: Comparing Two Images
Students describe similarities and differences between two images.

**Example:**
"Compare these two landscape paintings:

**Image A:** Summer meadow with flowers, bright sunshine, blue sky
**Image B:** Same meadow in autumn, fallen leaves, gray clouds, rain

**Create a comparison table:**

| Element | Image A | Image B |
|---------|---------|---------|
| **Season** | ? | ? |
| **Colors** | ? | ? |
| **Lighting** | ? | ? |
| **Mood** | ? | ? |
| **Details** | ? | ? |

**Written comparison:**
*'Beide Bilder zeigen dieselbe Wiese, aber zu unterschiedlichen Jahreszeiten. Während Bild A..., zeigt Bild B...'*"
