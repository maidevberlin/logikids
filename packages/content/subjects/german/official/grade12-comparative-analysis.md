id: grade12-comparative-analysis
name: Comparative Literature Analysis
description: Comparing literary works across epochs and cultures with sophisticated analytical structure
grade: 12
focus: Cross-epoch and cross-cultural literary comparison
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Compare works from different literary epochs (e.g., Enlightenment vs. Romanticism)
  - Identify patterns of literary influence and development across time periods
  - Develop original comparative theses that illuminate both works through juxtaposition
  - Analyze how cultural contexts shape similar themes differently
  - Write comparative essays with sophisticated structure (alternating or block comparison)
  - Use comparison as a tool to deepen interpretation of individual works
  - Synthesize insights from multiple works into coherent arguments
problem_types:
  - Cross-epoch comparison
  - Theme comparison
  - Form and style
  - Cultural context
  - Literary influence
  - Character parallels
  - Motif analysis
  - Genre evolution
  - Author comparison
  - Aesthetic movements
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Compare two works from adjacent epochs with clear thematic similarities
    - Focus on obvious structural or thematic parallels
    - Use simple comparative structure (block comparison)
  medium:
    - Compare works from distant epochs or different cultural contexts
    - Analyze subtle connections and transformations of themes
    - Integrate historical and cultural context systematically
  hard:
    - Compare three or more works across multiple epochs or cultures
    - Develop original theses about literary development and influence
    - Use sophisticated comparative structure with integrated analysis
prerequisites:
  - grade11-literary-interpretation
  - grade10-text-comparison
  - grade11-enlightenment-literature
  - grade11-sturm-und-drang
  - grade11-classical-drama
real_world_context:
  - Analyzing how the theme of individual vs. society appears in Enlightenment drama versus Romanticism
  - Comparing nature imagery in Romantic poetry with Expressionist nature depictions
  - Examining how love and passion are portrayed in Sturm und Drang versus Realism
  - Tracing the development of the tragic hero from Classical drama to modern literature
  - Comparing coming-of-age narratives across different epochs and cultures
  - Analyzing how war is depicted in Baroque poetry versus post-WWI Expressionism
  - Examining the concept of the artist figure in Romanticism and modern literature
  - Comparing social criticism in Naturalist drama with contemporary engaged literature
  - Analyzing how women characters evolve from Enlightenment to contemporary literature
  - Tracing the motif of the double or Doppelg√§nger across literary epochs
  - Comparing utopian and dystopian visions from different time periods
  - Examining how the city is portrayed in Naturalism versus Expressionism
  - Analyzing religious doubt and faith across Baroque, Enlightenment, and modern texts
  - Comparing narrative techniques in Realist novels with postmodern fragmentation
  - Examining how death and mortality are treated in different literary movements
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid superficial similarities without analyzing deeper connections
  - Don't compare works without considering historical and cultural context
  - Avoid unbalanced treatment where one work dominates the analysis
  - Don't force comparisons between fundamentally incompatible works
  - Avoid listing similarities and differences without developing a thesis
