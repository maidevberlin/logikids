---
id: grade2-reading-simple-texts
name: Reading Simple Texts
description: Decoding words, reading simple sentences and short texts with basic comprehension
grade: 2
focus: Text-level reading comprehension
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Read age-appropriate short texts fluently
  - Understand main ideas in simple stories
  - Answer questions about what was read
  - Recognize common sight words automatically
  - Develop mental images while reading
  - Identify key information in informational texts
  - Distinguish between narrative and non-fiction texts
problem_types:
  - Main idea identification
  - Detail recall questions
  - Text-picture matching
  - Sequence ordering
  - Character identification
  - True/false comprehension
  - Information location
  - Story prediction
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Use texts of 3-5 sentences with simple vocabulary
    - Each sentence 4-6 words with familiar words
    - Questions ask for directly stated information
  medium:
    - Use texts of 5-8 sentences with varied vocabulary
    - Each sentence 6-8 words including descriptive words
    - Questions require basic inference from context
  hard:
    - Use texts of 8-12 sentences with expanded vocabulary
    - Each sentence 8-10 words with compound structures
    - Questions require connecting information across sentences
prerequisites:
  - grade1-reading-short-sentences
  - grade1-reading-simple-words
real_world_context:
  - Reading short stories and picture books independently
  - Understanding simple instructions for games and activities
  - Reading informational texts about animals, nature, or familiar topics
  - Following multi-step written directions for crafts or tasks
  - Reading dialogue in comics and graphic stories
  - A young adventurer reading treasure maps with written clues to find hidden gold
  - Reading signs at the zoo to learn interesting facts about different animals
  - Following a recipe with simple written steps to bake a birthday cake
  - A detective reading witness statements to solve a mystery case
  - Understanding safety instructions posted at the swimming pool or playground
  - Reading letters or postcards from pen pals or relatives living far away
  - A space explorer reading mission briefings about new planets to visit
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid texts longer than 12 sentences for Grade 2 students
  - Don't use vocabulary beyond everyday experiences of 7-8 year olds
  - Avoid complex sentence structures like relative clauses or passive voice
  - Don't ask questions requiring cultural or factual knowledge beyond text
  - Avoid texts with ambiguous meanings or requiring adult perspective
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
