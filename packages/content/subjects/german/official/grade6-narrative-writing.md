---
id: grade6-narrative-writing
name: Narrative Writing
description: Story composition, plot structure, character development, narrative perspective
grade: 6
focus: Creative storytelling and structured narrative composition
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Master narrative writing as one of three foundational forms
  - Organize ideas through brainstorming, sketching, clustering, mind mapping
  - Arrange events in logical sequence using outlines
  - Write narratives from clear perspectives with structured plot development
  - Create believable characters through description and internal monologue
  - Adapt or imitate literary works (myths, book excerpts)
  - Use vivid language, dialogue, and sensory details
problem_types:
  - Plot structure organization
  - Character development
  - Perspective consistency
  - Dialogue formatting
  - Opening hooks
  - Climax and resolution
  - Sensory detail selection
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Simple linear plots with clear beginning, middle, and end
    - Single narrative perspective throughout
    - Guided structure with provided story elements
  medium:
    - Subplots that enhance the main storyline
    - Character growth and development over the narrative arc
    - Varied sentence structures and descriptive language
  hard:
    - Complex narrative techniques such as flashbacks or foreshadowing
    - Multiple perspectives or first-person unreliable narration
    - Style imitation of specific literary works or authors
prerequisites:
  - grade4-text-planning
  - grade3-composition-planning
real_world_context:
  - Writing creative stories for school publications or competitions
  - Adapting myths or legends into modern retellings for class projects
  - Creating character-driven narratives based on personal experiences
  - Composing diary entries or journal narratives from fictional perspectives
  - Developing short stories that explore different cultures and time periods
  - Writing adventure stories set in fantasy worlds with dragons, wizards, and quests
  - Creating mystery stories where characters solve puzzles and uncover clues
  - Composing historical fiction narratives from the perspective of children in different eras
  - Writing sequels or prequels to favorite books, maintaining original character voices
  - Creating science fiction stories about space exploration or future technologies
  - Developing nature-based narratives from animal perspectives in wilderness settings
  - Writing collaborative stories with classmates, taking turns continuing the plot
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid jumbled chronology without clear transitions or flashback markers
  - Don't mix first-person and third-person perspectives within the same scene
  - Avoid dialogue without proper formatting (quotation marks and speaker tags)
  - Don't introduce too many characters in a short narrative (limit to 3-4 main)
  - Avoid abstract descriptions; use concrete sensory details instead
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
