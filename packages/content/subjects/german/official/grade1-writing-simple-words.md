---
id: grade1-writing-simple-words
name: Writing Simple Words
description: Learn to write words by listening to sounds, spell phonetically regular words, and write common sight words correctly
grade: 1
focus: Sound-spelling correspondence and basic word writing
difficulty: easy
learning_objectives:
  - Write words by listening to and identifying individual sounds
  - Apply letter-sound knowledge to spell phonetically regular words
  - Use syllable structure to help spell longer words
  - Write common sight words correctly from memory
  - Recognize when a written word looks incorrect and attempt self-correction
  - Encode simple compound words by combining known word parts
problem_types:
  - Sound-based spelling
  - Word completion
  - Picture-to-word writing
  - Sight word spelling
  - Error detection
  - Syllable-based encoding
  - Missing letter insertion
  - Word building from sounds
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Phonetically regular 3-4 letter words (CVC patterns like Oma, Eis, Brot)
    - Single-syllable words with clear sound-letter correspondence
    - Common sight words encountered frequently (der, die, das, ist, und)
  medium:
    - Phonetically regular 5-6 letter words with common patterns
    - Two-syllable words that can be segmented clearly
    - Words with basic consonant blends (bl, gr, tr)
  hard:
    - Words with umlauts (ä, ö, ü) requiring vowel modification
    - Words with letter combinations (ch, sch, ei, au, eu)
    - Less common sight words with irregular spellings
prerequisites:
  - grade1-letter-sounds
real_world_context:
  - Writing shopping lists with parents, spelling items like Milch, Brot, Käse
  - Labeling items in your room or classroom with their names
  - Writing simple messages in a personal diary or journal
  - Creating greeting cards for family members with handwritten words
  - Writing your own name and family members' names correctly
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid words with silent letters or highly irregular spellings
  - Don't use vocabulary unfamiliar to 6-7 year olds
  - Avoid words requiring advanced spelling rules not yet taught
  - Don't expect perfect spelling; accept phonetically plausible attempts
  - Avoid words with more than 3 syllables at easy/medium levels
version: 1
version_notes: Initial concept for German Grade 1 basic word writing and encoding
---
