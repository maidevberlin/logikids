---
id: grade11-language-manipulation
name: Language and Manipulation
description: Analyzing propaganda and persuasive language techniques including framing, spin, and the relationship between language and power
grade: 11
focus: Critical language awareness and manipulation
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Recognize propaganda techniques and their psychological mechanisms
  - Analyze how framing and perspective shape perception of events and issues
  - Identify spin and euphemistic language in political and media discourse
  - Understand the relationship between language and power in society
  - Evaluate the ethical implications of manipulative language use
  - Develop critical language awareness to resist manipulation
problem_types:
  - Propaganda analysis
  - Framing identification
  - Euphemism detection
  - Power dynamics
  - Media manipulation
  - Historical examples
  - Ethical evaluation
  - Counter-narrative
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify obvious propaganda techniques in historical or contemporary texts
    - Recognize clear examples of euphemistic language and framing
    - Describe basic power relationships reflected in language choices
  medium:
    - Analyze how multiple manipulation techniques work together in complex texts
    - Compare how different sources frame the same event or issue differently
    - Evaluate the effectiveness and ethical dimensions of manipulative language
  hard:
    - Analyze subtle manipulation in sophisticated political or media discourse
    - Trace how language constructs and reinforces power structures over time
    - Develop counter-narratives that expose and resist manipulative framing
real_world_context:
  - A government reframes military action using sanitized language like "collateral damage"
  - Political parties use different terms for the same policy to influence public opinion
  - Advertising campaigns use psychological triggers to manipulate consumer behavior
  - A news outlet's word choices reveal ideological bias in reporting
  - Historical propaganda posters from Nazi Germany or the GDR are analyzed
  - A corporation uses greenwashing to appear environmentally responsible
  - Social media algorithms create filter bubbles that reinforce specific worldviews
  - A politician uses dog-whistle language to signal to certain voter groups
  - Tabloid headlines sensationalize events to generate clicks and emotional reactions
  - A dictator's regime controls language to maintain power and suppress dissent
  - Marketing teams rebrand problematic products with positive-sounding names
  - Climate change deniers use framing to cast doubt on scientific consensus
  - A public relations firm spins a corporate scandal to minimize reputational damage
  - Wartime propaganda demonizes the enemy while glorifying one's own side
  - A movement reclaims previously derogatory language to empower marginalized groups
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid politically partisan examples that favor one contemporary ideology
  - Do not oversimplify complex political or historical situations
  - Avoid examples requiring specialized knowledge beyond grade 11 level
  - Do not present all persuasive language as inherently unethical
  - Avoid culturally obscure references unfamiliar to 16-year-olds
prerequisites:
  - grade10-speech-analysis
  - grade9-rhetorical-devices
version: 1
version_notes: Initial creation of grade 11 language manipulation concept
