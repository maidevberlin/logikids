Generate a task where {{childName}} learns and practices good conversation manners and listening skills.

**Focus areas to choose from:**
- Recognizing polite conversation behavior
- Understanding turn-taking in conversations
- Practicing active listening
- Staying on topic during discussions
- Using polite interruption phrases

## Task Variations

### Variation 1: Picture Scenario Analysis
Show {{num1}} pictures of conversation situations. For each picture, the student identifies who is following the rules and who isn't.

Example scenarios:
| Picture | Good Behavior | Bad Behavior |
|---------|---------------|--------------|
| Classroom discussion | Child raising hand | Child shouting out |
| Two friends talking | Child waiting turn | Child interrupting |
| Story time | Children listening quietly | Child talking during story |

### Variation 2: Rule Matching
Present {{num1}} conversation situations. The student must match each situation with the correct conversation rule to follow.

Example situations and rules:
- Situation: *Du möchtest etwas sagen.* → Rule: *Hand heben* (Raise hand)
- Situation: *Jemand spricht gerade.* → Rule: *Zuhören und warten* (Listen and wait)
- Situation: *Du verstehst etwas nicht.* → Rule: *Hand heben und fragen* (Raise hand and ask)

### Variation 3: What Should You Do?
Describe {{num1}} social scenarios. The student must choose or explain the correct behavior.

Example scenarios:
1. *Lisa erzählt von ihrem Wochenende. Du möchtest auch etwas sagen. Was machst du?*
   - ❌ Einfach losreden
   - ✅ Warten bis Lisa fertig ist
   - ❌ Lisa unterbrechen

2. *Der Lehrer fragt eine Frage. Du kennst die Antwort. Was machst du?*
   - ❌ Laut rufen
   - ✅ Hand heben
   - ❌ Einfach reden

### Variation 4: Role-Play Preparation
Present {{num1}} conversation situations. The student must explain what they would say or do to be polite.

Example situations:
- *You want to join your friends' conversation*: *"Darf ich mitsprechen?"*
- *Someone is talking and you need to interrupt*: *"Entschuldigung, darf ich etwas fragen?"*
- *You didn't hear what was said*: *"Kannst du das bitte wiederholen?"*

### Variation 5: Conversation Detective
Show a short dialogue between {{num1}} characters. The student identifies which conversation rules are being followed or broken.

Example dialogue:
*Lisa: "Gestern war ich im Zoo..."*
*Max: (unterbricht) "Ich war auch schon im Zoo!"*
*Lena: (hebt die Hand) "Darf ich auch etwas erzählen?"*

Questions:
- Who interrupts? (Max)
- Who is polite? (Lena)
- What should Max have done? (Wait until Lisa finishes)

### Variation 6: Sort Good and Bad Behaviors
Present {{num1}} conversation behaviors. The student sorts them into **Gut** (good) and **Nicht gut** (not good).

Behaviors to sort:
- Hand heben ✅
- Andere unterbrechen ❌
- Zuhören ✅
- Herumrufen ❌
- Warten bis man dran ist ✅
- Nicht aufpassen ❌

### Variation 7: Complete the Conversation
Show an incomplete conversation with {{num1}} gaps. The student fills in what should be said or done to follow conversation rules.

Example:
*Lehrer: "Wer weiß die Antwort?"*
*Tim: [___] (hebt die Hand)*
*Lehrer: "Ja, Tim?"*
*Tim: [___] (gibt die Antwort)*

### Variation 8: Active Listening Check
Tell or read a short story ({{num1}} sentences). Afterward, ask {{num1}} comprehension questions to check if the student was listening well.

Example:
*Story: "Emma ging in den Park. Dort spielte sie auf der Schaukel. Dann aß sie ein Eis."*

Questions:
- Wohin ging Emma? (In den Park)
- Was machte sie dort? (Spielte auf der Schaukel)
- Was aß sie? (Ein Eis)

This teaches the importance of listening carefully.
