---
id: grade10-material-based-writing
name: Material-Based Writing
description: Synthesizing multiple sources into coherent texts while maintaining objectivity
grade: 10
focus: Multi-source synthesis and objective writing
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Synthesize information from multiple sources into coherent arguments
  - Maintain objectivity while presenting different perspectives
  - Structure complex information logically and accessibly
  - Cite and attribute sources appropriately in synthesized texts
  - Create cohesive texts that integrate diverse materials effectively
  - Identify connections and contradictions across source materials
  - Adapt writing style to match required text type and audience
problem_types:
  - Information synthesis
  - Source comparison
  - Position development
  - Structural organization
  - Perspective integration
  - Transition creation
  - Coherence building
  - Attribution practice
  - Audience adaptation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Synthesize 2-3 sources with aligned perspectives
    - Create informative text of 400-600 words
    - Basic structural organization with clear topic sentences
  medium:
    - Integrate 4-5 sources with varying perspectives
    - Produce argumentative or informative text of 600-800 words
    - Sophisticated organization balancing multiple viewpoints
  hard:
    - Synthesize 6+ sources with conflicting information
    - Write nuanced text of 800-1000 words addressing complexity
    - Advanced structural techniques managing contradictions
real_world_context:
  - Writing a school newspaper article about digital learning using teacher interviews, student surveys, and research studies
  - Creating an information brochure about climate action using scientific reports, policy documents, and NGO statements
  - Composing a speech about healthy eating for a school assembly based on nutritional guidelines, medical studies, and youth perspectives
  - Writing a blog post about social media's effects combining psychological research, user testimonials, and platform statistics
  - Developing a presentation script about renewable energy using scientific articles, industry reports, and environmental assessments
  - Creating an essay about urban transportation from city planning documents, traffic studies, and resident feedback
  - Writing a magazine article about gaming culture using player interviews, academic research, and industry data
  - Composing a letter to local government about youth facilities based on community surveys, budget reports, and expert recommendations
  - Developing an informative guide about study strategies from educational research, teacher advice, and student success stories
  - Writing a feature article about local history combining archive documents, oral histories, and historical photographs
  - Creating a report on school exchange programs using participant testimonials, program statistics, and cultural studies
  - Composing an opinion piece about environmental activism from activist statements, scientific findings, and policy critiques
  - Writing a guide to careers in technology based on industry trends, professional interviews, and educational requirements
  - Developing a speech about volunteering using charity reports, volunteer experiences, and community impact data
  - Creating an article about music's role in society from cultural studies, artist perspectives, and audience research
anti_patterns:
  - Don't create tasks requiring specialized knowledge not in provided sources
  - Avoid asking for personal opinion when objectivity is required
  - Don't provide sources that completely contradict without resolution path
  - Avoid requiring citation formats beyond basic attribution
  - Don't create synthesis tasks with sources on unrelated topics
prerequisites:
  - grade9-research-writing
  - grade9-textgebundene-eroerterung
version: 1
version_notes: Initial version based on KMK Bildungsstandards for Grade 10 MSA level
---

Material-based writing requires students to work with multiple source texts to create new, coherent texts. Students must extract relevant information, identify relationships between sources, and synthesize content while maintaining appropriate objectivity and structure.

The focus is on creating unified texts from diverse materials, not simply summarizing each source separately. Students must make decisions about organization, emphasis, and integration to produce texts that serve specific communicative purposes.
