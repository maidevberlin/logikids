---
id: grade12-language-change
name: Language Change
description: Analyzing contemporary language developments including Anglicisms, internet language, and gender-inclusive language
grade: 12
focus: Contemporary language developments and linguistic evolution
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Analyze contemporary language developments and their social contexts
  - Understand language contact and borrowing processes (especially English influence)
  - Discuss language norms, prescription, and the tension between tradition and change
  - Analyze digital communication and its impact on German language structure
  - Evaluate arguments about language change from linguistic and social perspectives
  - Examine gender-inclusive language reforms and their linguistic implications
  - Apply sociolinguistic concepts to contemporary language debates
problem_types:
  - Anglicism analysis
  - Internet language examination
  - Gender-inclusive language evaluation
  - Language norm discussion
  - Digital communication impact
  - Generational language variation
  - Code-switching patterns
  - Language policy critique
  - Prescriptivism vs. descriptivism
  - Youth language evolution
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify obvious Anglicisms and categorize them by type (direct borrowing, loan translation)
    - Recognize basic internet language features like abbreviations and emoticons
    - Name common gender-inclusive forms (Binnen-I, Gender-Sternchen, Doppelnennung)
  medium:
    - Analyze why certain Anglicisms enter German while others don't (language prestige, lexical gaps)
    - Examine how digital communication creates new grammatical and orthographic patterns
    - Evaluate competing arguments about gender-inclusive language from linguistic and social angles
  hard:
    - Develop original positions on language change integrating linguistic theory and empirical evidence
    - Analyze the relationship between language ideology, social identity, and linguistic innovation
    - Compare contemporary language change with historical developments to identify patterns and differences
prerequisites:
  - grade10-language-history
  - grade9-language-variation
  - grade11-language-philosophy
real_world_context:
  - Analyzing whether English loanwords like "downloaden" or "leaken" enrich or threaten German vocabulary
  - Examining how TikTok and Instagram influence youth language through hashtags and viral phrases
  - Debating university style guides that require or prohibit gender-inclusive language forms
  - Analyzing news articles that use Gender-Sternchen versus traditional generic masculine forms
  - Examining how customer service chatbots adapt language to seem natural or accidentally create new patterns
  - Comparing German and French language policies (Académie française vs. more permissive German approach)
  - Analyzing political speeches to identify how politicians use or avoid Anglicisms strategically
  - Examining public broadcasting guidelines on gender-inclusive language and accessibility
  - Analyzing how gaming communities develop specialized vocabulary mixing German and English
  - Examining generational differences in attitudes toward "Kiezdeutsch" or "Denglisch"
  - Analyzing corporate language in German companies (meeting, deadline, teamwork) and its implications
  - Examining how spell-checkers and autocorrect shape written language norms
  - Analyzing social media activism around language (e.g., debates about "Zigeuner" vs. "Sinti und Roma")
  - Examining linguistic landscape changes (bilingual signs, English in advertising) in German cities
  - Analyzing how streaming services' subtitle choices influence language exposure and borrowing
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid treating language change as decline or corruption of German
  - Do not ignore social and political dimensions of language debates
  - Avoid presenting only prescriptivist views without evidence
  - Don't treat gender language purely as political without linguistic analysis
  - Avoid assuming all change is conscious (most is organic and unconscious)
