# Informational Texts

Create a task about informational texts for {{age}}-year-old {{gender}} at difficulty level {{difficulty}}.

## Task Variations

Choose ONE of these variations:

1. **Main Idea and Details**
   - Present a short informational text (5-7 sentences)
   - Students identify the main idea (one sentence)
   - Find 3-4 supporting details
   - Table: **Main Idea** | **Detail 1** | **Detail 2** | **Detail 3**

2. **Text Features Identification**
   - Show an informational text with features
   - Features: headings, captions, diagrams, bold words
   - Students identify each feature and explain its purpose
   - Example: "The heading tells us...", "Bold words are important because..."

3. **Question Answering**
   - Provide a factual text (about animals, places, or science)
   - Students answer 5-6 comprehension questions
   - Question types: literal (find in text), inferential (think about), vocabulary
   - All answers must be found in or based on the text

4. **Table of Contents Practice**
   - Show a table of contents from a reference book
   - Ask: "Where would you look for information about...?"
   - Students identify chapter and page number
   - Practice 5-6 different information searches

5. **Fact vs. Opinion**
   - Present 8-10 sentences from informational texts
   - Students sort into two categories: **Facts** | **Opinions**
   - Facts can be proven; opinions are beliefs
   - Examples: "Birds have feathers." (fact) vs. "Birds are beautiful." (opinion)

6. **Information Organization**
   - Read a text about a topic (e.g., dolphins)
   - Organize information into categories
   - Categories: **Appearance**, **Habitat**, **Food**, **Behavior**
   - Create a fact sheet with organized information

7. **Comparing Sources**
   - Present two short texts about the same topic
   - Compare: Do they give the same information?
   - What does Source 1 say that Source 2 doesn't?
   - Which source is more detailed? Which is more useful?

8. **Note-Taking Practice**
   - Read an informational text
   - Take notes using bullet points
   - Focus on main ideas and important facts
   - Use keywords, not full sentences
   - Practice condensing information
