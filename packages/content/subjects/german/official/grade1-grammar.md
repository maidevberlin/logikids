---
id: grade1-grammar
name: Grammar & Parts of Speech
description: Identify and use basic parts of speech including nouns, verbs, and adjectives, and understand simple word formation and sentence structure
grade: 1
focus: Grammatical structure and word categories
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Identify nouns using article test and recognize noun capitalization
  - Recognize verbs as action words and use them in correct forms
  - Use adjectives to describe people, animals, and objects
  - Create compound words by combining two words
  - Identify word components including prefixes, suffixes, and word stems
  - Recognize sentences as complete meaning units with correct structure
  - Distinguish between vowels and consonants in written words
problem_types:
  - Noun identification
  - Verb recognition
  - Adjective usage
  - Compound word formation
  - Word component analysis
  - Sentence structure
  - Part of speech classification
  - Vowel-consonant distinction
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Common concrete nouns with clear articles (der/die/das)
    - Simple present tense verb forms only
    - Everyday adjectives describing visible attributes (big, small, red)
  medium:
    - Abstract nouns and nouns requiring article test strategy
    - Multiple verb tenses with familiar verbs
    - Compound words from two familiar base words
  hard:
    - Identifying word stems and affixes in complex words
    - Analyzing sentence structure with multiple parts
    - Distinguishing similar word categories in context
prerequisites:
  - grade1-phonological-awareness
  - grade1-spelling
real_world_context:
  - Using the article test to know when to write words with capital letters
  - Describing what people and animals do using action words
  - Making sentences sound more interesting by adding descriptive words
  - Understanding how new words are created by combining familiar words
  - Recognizing that sentences express complete thoughts with proper structure
  - Naming people, pets, and places with capital letters in birthday invitations
  - Playing word detective to find verbs in storybooks and fairy tales
  - Describing favorite toys using colorful adjectives for show-and-tell
  - Creating funny compound words like "Regenbogenhaus" or "Schokoladenberg"
  - Helping younger siblings understand why "die Katze l√§uft" makes sense
  - Finding noun patterns when labeling classroom objects and furniture
  - Discovering how changing word parts creates related words in nature topics
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid complex grammatical terminology beyond grade level
  - Don't use irregular verb forms that haven't been introduced yet
  - Avoid abstract nouns not accessible to 6-7 year olds
  - Don't require knowledge of grammatical cases or declensions
  - Avoid sentences with subordinate clauses or complex structure
version: 2
