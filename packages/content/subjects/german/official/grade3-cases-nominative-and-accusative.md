# Cases - Nominative and Accusative

Create a task where {{name}} learns German cases appropriate for {{age}}-year-olds at {{difficulty}} level in {{language}}.

## Variation 1: Introduction to Cases with Questions
Learn to identify cases using question words:

**Nominative (1. Fall/Wer-Fall):**
- Question: **Wer?** (Who?) or **Was?** (What?)
- Function: The **subject** - who/what does the action
- Example: *Der Hund bellt.* → **Wer** bellt? → Der Hund

**Accusative (4. Fall/Wen-Fall):**
- Question: **Wen?** (Whom?) or **Was?** (What?)
- Function: The **direct object** - who/what receives the action
- Example: *Ich sehe den Hund.* → **Wen** sehe ich? → den Hund

Practice: Ask Wer? or Wen? for each sentence and identify the case.

## Variation 2: Article Declension Table
Learn how articles change in different cases:

**Definite Articles (der, die, das):**

| Gender | Nominative (Wer/Was?) | Accusative (Wen/Was?) |
|--------|----------------------|----------------------|
| Masculine | **der** Hund | **den** Hund |
| Feminine | **die** Katze | **die** Katze |
| Neuter | **das** Haus | **das** Haus |
| Plural | **die** Hunde | **die** Hunde |

**Key rule:** Only **masculine** articles change from *der* to *den*!

**Indefinite Articles (ein, eine):**

| Gender | Nominative | Accusative |
|--------|-----------|-----------|
| Masculine | **ein** Hund | **einen** Hund |
| Feminine | **eine** Katze | **eine** Katze |
| Neuter | **ein** Haus | **ein** Haus |

Practice: Fill in the correct article forms in blank tables.

## Variation 3: Subject vs Direct Object
Identify and label subjects and direct objects:

**Example sentences:**

1. *Der Junge wirft den Ball.*
   - **Subject (Nominative):** Der Junge (who throws?)
   - **Direct Object (Accusative):** den Ball (what is thrown?)

2. *Die Mutter ruft das Kind.*
   - **Subject:** Die Mutter
   - **Direct Object:** das Kind

3. *Ich sehe einen Vogel.*
   - **Subject:** Ich
   - **Direct Object:** einen Vogel

**Task:** Underline subjects once, underline direct objects twice.

## Variation 4: Choosing the Correct Article
Complete sentences with the correct article form:

**Examples:**
1. ___ Katze jagt ___ Maus. (Die/die or Die/den?)
   - Answer: **Die** Katze jagt **die** Maus.
   - (Nominative feminine / Accusative feminine - no change)

2. Ich sehe ___ Hund. (der or den?)
   - Answer: Ich sehe **den** Hund.
   - (Accusative masculine - changes to den!)

3. ___ Kind malt ___ Bild. (Das/das or Das/den?)
   - Answer: **Das** Kind malt **das** Bild.
   - (Nominative neuter / Accusative neuter - no change)

Practice with 10-15 fill-in-the-blank sentences.

## Variation 5: Finding the Action and its Direction
Understand which noun does the action (nominative) and which receives it (accusative):

**Pattern: Subject → Verb → Direct Object**

| Who/What acts? (Nom.) | Verb | Who/What is affected? (Acc.) |
|----------------------|------|------------------------------|
| Der Lehrer | erklärt | die Aufgabe |
| Das Mädchen | liest | ein Buch |
| Die Kinder | bauen | einen Turm |

Create your own sentences following this pattern.

## Variation 6: Common Accusative Verbs
Learn verbs that typically take accusative objects:

**Verbs requiring accusative:**
- *sehen* (to see): Ich sehe **den Baum**.
- *haben* (to have): Sie hat **eine Katze**.
- *lesen* (to read): Er liest **das Buch**.
- *essen* (to eat): Wir essen **einen Apfel**.
- *trinken* (to drink): Du trinkst **die Milch**.
- *malen* (to paint): Sie malt **ein Bild**.
- *kaufen* (to buy): Ich kaufe **den Ball**.

Practice: Use each verb in a sentence with the correct accusative article.

## Variation 7: Sentence Transformation
Change sentences and observe how cases change:

**Original:** *Der Hund jagt die Katze.*
- Nominative: der Hund
- Accusative: die Katze

**Reversed:** *Die Katze jagt den Hund.*
- Nominative: die Katze
- Accusative: **den** Hund (changed!)

Notice: When the masculine noun becomes the object, *der* changes to *den*.

Practice reversing 5 sentences and adjusting articles correctly.

## Variation 8: Case Detective Game
Analyze sentences and identify all cases:

**Sample analysis:**

*Der Vater kauft einen Ball für den Sohn.*

1. **Der Vater** - Nominative (Who buys? = Der Vater)
2. **einen Ball** - Accusative (What does he buy? = einen Ball)
3. Note: *für den Sohn* uses a preposition (beyond this lesson)

**Your task:**
For each sentence:
- Find the nominative noun (subject)
- Find the accusative noun (direct object)
- Check if masculine articles changed from *der* to *den* or *ein* to *einen*

**Practice sentences:**
1. *Die Lehrerin erklärt den Schülern die Aufgabe.*
2. *Ich male ein Bild.*
3. *Der Hund frisst das Futter.*
4. *Wir besuchen die Oma.*
5. *Das Kind schreibt einen Brief.*

**Remember - Case Rules:**
- Nominative = subject (who/what does the action)
- Accusative = direct object (who/what receives the action)
- Ask **Wer/Was?** for nominative
- Ask **Wen/Was?** for accusative
- Only **masculine** articles change in accusative: der→den, ein→einen
- Feminine, neuter, and plural stay the same
