---
id: grade3-dialogue-roleplay
name: Dialogue and Role Play
description: Navigate communication situations routinely, follow conversation rules, provide feedback, and assume roles in dramatic play with character perspective
grade: 3
focus: Dialogue management and dramatic interpretation
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Navigate standard communication situations routinely (apologies, congratulations, requests)
  - Follow established conversation rules and allow others to speak without interrupting
  - Provide constructive feedback that is specific and actionable
  - Manage time effectively during group discussions and presentations
  - Assume roles from literature or imagination with distinct character perspective
  - Express feelings through coordinated voice, posture, gesture, and language choices
  - Distinguish between personal self and character viewpoint during role play
problem_types:
  - Apology dialogue
  - Congratulation exchange
  - Feedback conversation
  - Time-managed discussion
  - Literary character roleplay
  - Improvised scenario
  - Perspective-taking dialogue
  - Multi-character interaction
  - Gesture-voice coordination
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single communication situation with clear prompt (simple apology or thanks)
    - One or two character perspectives in role play
    - Basic emotions expressed through voice and gesture (happy, sad, angry)
  medium:
    - Multiple-turn dialogue with topic consistency across 3-4 exchanges
    - Character interpretation requiring inference from text description
    - Combined expression using voice tone, posture, and specific gestures together
  hard:
    - Complex communication requiring navigation of social norms (formal vs. informal)
    - Multi-character scenes with distinct perspectives and conflicting goals
    - Subtle emotional expression through nuanced vocal and physical choices
prerequisites:
  - grade1-speaking-conversation
  - grade1-dramatic-expression
real_world_context:
  - Apologizing sincerely after accidentally bumping into someone or breaking something
  - Congratulating a friend who won a competition or achieved a personal goal
  - Giving specific feedback on a classmate's presentation or creative work
  - Taking on character roles during literature discussions or school plays
  - Managing turn-taking and time limits during group project planning sessions
  - Acting out a scene from a fairy tale where different characters have conflicting wishes
  - Practicing polite requests when asking the librarian for help finding a book
  - Role-playing a shopkeeper and customer during a math lesson about money
  - Taking on the perspective of an animal in a nature story and expressing its feelings
  - Performing a historical dialogue between a knight and a princess for history class
  - Giving constructive feedback to a partner about their poster presentation on planets
  - Role-playing a detective and witness to practice asking and answering questions
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid scenarios requiring cultural knowledge beyond 8-9 year experience
  - Don't expect adult-level formal register or business communication
  - Avoid multi-character interactions with more than 4 distinct roles
  - Don't require memorization of scripted lines - focus on improvisation
  - Avoid feedback tasks without clear criteria or sentence frames for support
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
