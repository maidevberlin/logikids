grade: 13
focus: Advanced oral defense and argumentation under pressure
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Defend academic positions under sustained questioning with confidence and precision
  - Respond constructively to challenges and counterarguments in real time
  - Demonstrate comprehensive depth of knowledge across topic boundaries
  - Think critically on one's feet without preparation time
  - Maintain academic composure and professionalism under examination pressure
  - Articulate connections between different aspects of research or literary analysis
  - Clarify and refine positions in response to probing questions
problem_types:
  - Position defense
  - Counterargument response
  - Knowledge demonstration
  - Spontaneous analysis
  - Clarification technique
  - Connection articulation
  - Challenge navigation
  - Assumption questioning
  - Evidence justification
  - Alternative consideration
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Defend straightforward thesis with prepared evidence and anticipated questions
    - Respond to direct questions about methodology and sources with clear explanations
    - Connect defense to well-rehearsed main arguments and supporting materials
  medium:
    - Defend nuanced interpretations against theoretical challenges and alternative readings
    - Respond to unexpected questions by drawing on broader knowledge framework
    - Navigate multi-part questions that test understanding across different dimensions
  hard:
    - Defend original or controversial positions under rigorous scholarly interrogation
    - Respond to challenges that expose assumptions or logical gaps in argumentation
    - Synthesize spontaneous connections between disparate topics under time pressure

  - grade12-academic-presentation
  - grade12-text-interpretation-abitur
  - grade11-seminar-discussion
  - grade11-rhetoric-oratory

  - Defending Facharbeit thesis on narrative unreliability to examiners questioning interpretive methodology
  - Responding to challenges about Expressionist aesthetics during oral Abitur examination under time limits
  - Defending literary epoch comparison while examiners test boundaries of knowledge and argumentation
  - Articulating connections between language philosophy and literary analysis when questioned by committee
  - Defending interpretations of ambiguous poetic imagery under theoretical cross-examination
  - Responding to examiner questions that challenge assumptions about authorial intention in modern literature
  - Defending thesis on drama theory while addressing counterexamples from different theatrical traditions
  - Navigating questions about methodology when defending comparative analysis of Romantic and Realist works
  - Defending position on literature's social function while responding to philosophical objections
  - Articulating spontaneous analysis of unseen text excerpt during oral examination defense phase
  - Responding to examiner challenges about historical context accuracy in epoch-spanning arguments
  - Defending creative writing choices and literary techniques when questioned about stylistic decisions
  - Navigating multi-part questions connecting language change research to broader sociolinguistic theory
  - Defending argumentative positions on contemporary literature while addressing canon and value questions
  - Responding to questions that test depth of author study knowledge beyond prepared presentation
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid hostile or unfair examiner behavior beyond realistic challenge
  - Don't require specialized knowledge beyond Abitur curriculum scope
  - Avoid trick questions designed to trap rather than test understanding
  - Don't model defensive or emotional responses as appropriate reactions
  - Avoid answers that are obscure trivia rather than demonstrable understanding
