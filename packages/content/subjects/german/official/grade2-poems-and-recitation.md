# Poems and Recitation

Create a task where {{name}} practices poetry and recitation skills appropriate for {{age}}-year-olds at {{difficulty}} level in {{language}}.

## Variation 1: Rhyme Scheme Recognition
Present a simple poem and have students identify the rhyme pattern:

**Example:**
*Meine Katze ist so klein,* (A)
*sie sitzt gern im Sonnenschein.* (A)
*Sie schnurrt und schläft den ganzen Tag,* (B)
*weil sie Ruhe gerne mag.* (B)

Mark the rhyme scheme: AABB

Find all rhyming word pairs:
- klein / Sonnenschein
- Tag / mag

## Variation 2: Memory and Recitation
Help the student learn a short poem (8-12 lines) by heart:

**Learning strategy:**
1. Read the poem aloud several times
2. Learn it line by line
3. Recite each stanza separately
4. Put the whole poem together
5. Practice without looking

**Recitation tips:**
- Speak clearly and loud enough
- Use expression in your voice
- Make pauses at line breaks
- Look at your audience
- Don't rush - enjoy the words

## Variation 3: Expressive Recitation
Take one poem and practice reciting it in different ways:

- **Happy**: Light, cheerful voice
- **Sad**: Slow, quiet voice
- **Excited**: Fast, energetic voice
- **Mysterious**: Quiet, dramatic voice
- **Angry**: Strong, forceful voice

How does the tone change the meaning of the poem?

## Variation 4: Creating Your Own Rhymes
Guide the student to write a simple 4-line poem with AABB rhyme scheme:

**Line 1:** Choose a topic (animal, season, toy)
**Line 2:** Write a line that rhymes with line 1
**Line 3:** Start a new thought
**Line 4:** Rhyme with line 3

**Example topic - dog:**
*Mein Hund ist groß und braun,*
*ich kann ihm wirklich traun.*
*Er spielt mit mir im Park,*
*sein Bellen ist sehr stark.*

## Variation 5: Rhythm and Beat
Explore the rhythm in poems by clapping or tapping:

- Read the poem aloud
- Clap on stressed syllables
- Feel the beat and pattern
- Notice how rhythm makes poems musical

Try with different poems:
- Nursery rhymes (steady beat)
- Modern poems (varied rhythm)

## Variation 6: Poem Illustration
After learning a poem, create illustrations:
- Draw a picture for each stanza
- Show what the poem describes
- Use colors that match the mood
- Present poem with pictures
- Recite while showing your artwork

## Variation 7: Rhyme Pattern Practice
Work with different rhyme schemes:

**AABB (couplets):**
*Der Mond scheint hell und klar,* (A)
*die Nacht ist wunderbar.* (A)
*Die Sterne funkeln sehr,* (B)
*am ganzen Himmelsmeer.* (B)

**ABAB (alternating):**
*Der Vogel singt im Baum,* (A)
*die Blumen sind so bunt.* (B)
*Ich höre ihm im Raum,* (A)
*sein Lied geht mir im Mund.* (B)

## Variation 8: Performance Poetry
Prepare a poem for performance:

**Preparation checklist:**
- ✓ Memorized completely
- ✓ Practiced pronunciation
- ✓ Planned gestures or movements
- ✓ Chosen appropriate volume
- ✓ Decided on tempo (fast/slow)
- ✓ Marked where to pause
- ✓ Ready for questions

**Performance elements:**
- Stand up straight
- Make eye contact
- Use hand gestures
- Vary your voice
- Show confidence
