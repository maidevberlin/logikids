---
id: grade9-debate-skills
name: Debate Skills
description: Participating in formal debates with structured arguments and responding to opponents
grade: 9
focus: Speaking and listening in argumentative contexts
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Prepare structured debate arguments with clear claims and evidence
  - Respond to opposing arguments effectively using logic and evidence
  - Maintain composure and respect during disagreement
  - Use evidence and reasoning under time pressure
  - Evaluate debate performance using criteria
  - Identify logical fallacies in opponents' arguments
  - Adapt arguments based on audience and format
problem_types:
  - Opening statement
  - Rebuttal speech
  - Cross-examination
  - Closing argument
  - Point of order
  - Point of information
  - Constructive argument
  - Impact weighing
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Arguments use simple claim-evidence structure
    - Rebuttals address one or two opponent points
    - Debates on familiar, low-complexity topics
  medium:
    - Arguments use claim-warrant-impact structure
    - Rebuttals address multiple points with prioritization
    - Debates require analyzing trade-offs between values
  hard:
    - Arguments anticipate and preempt counterarguments
    - Rebuttals rebuild after conceding weak points
    - Debates on abstract or multi-stakeholder issues
real_world_context:
  - A school council debates whether to extend lunch breaks by 15 minutes
  - Student representatives argue for or against school uniform policies
  - A youth parliament debates lowering the voting age to 16
  - Environmental club members debate banning single-use plastics in the cafeteria
  - Sports team captains debate tournament rules with the athletics director
  - Debate club practices for a regional competition on digital privacy
  - History class debates a historical decision from multiple perspectives
  - Student government discusses budget allocation for different school programs
  - Mock UN delegates debate climate policy from different nations' viewpoints
  - Class debates whether social media does more harm than good for teenagers
  - Literature students debate the motivations of a controversial character
  - Science class debates ethical implications of a new technology
  - Future career panel discusses work-life balance versus career advancement
  - Community forum where students debate local infrastructure improvements
  - Philosophy club discusses whether happiness or meaning matters more in life
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid debates where both sides have obviously unequal merit
  - Don't use topics requiring specialized knowledge beyond grade level
  - Avoid personal attacks or ad hominem examples in arguments
  - Don't create rebuttals that ignore the opponent's actual position
  - Avoid overly emotional topics inappropriate for 14-15 year olds
version: 1
