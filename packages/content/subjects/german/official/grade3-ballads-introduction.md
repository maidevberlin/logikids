# Ballads (Introduction)

Create a task about ballads for {{age}}-year-old {{gender}} at difficulty level {{difficulty}}.

## Task Variations

Choose ONE of these variations:

1. **Story Elements in Ballads**
   - Read a simple ballad (e.g., "Der Erlkönig" excerpt)
   - Identify story elements: **Characters** | **Setting** | **Conflict** | **Resolution**
   - Explain: How does this poem tell a story?
   - Compare with a prose story

2. **Ballad vs. Poem Comparison**
   - Create a comparison table
   - Categories: **Tells a Story?**, **Rhyme?**, **Length**, **Characters**, **Action**
   - Read 3 examples and classify as ballad or poem
   - Explain the key difference: ballads narrate, poems describe/express

3. **Dramatic Moments**
   - Read a ballad with a climax
   - Identify the most exciting part
   - Mark where tension builds and where it releases
   - Practice reading that stanza with dramatic expression

4. **Character Voices**
   - Read a ballad with dialogue (e.g., "Der Erlkönig")
   - Identify different speakers: narrator, father, child, Erlking
   - Practice reading each part with appropriate voice
   - Table: **Speaker** | **Lines** | **How to Read It**

5. **Ballad Structure**
   - Examine a ballad's structure
   - Count stanzas and lines per stanza
   - Identify rhyme scheme (ABAB, AABB)
   - Notice repetition or refrain
   - Create a visual diagram of the structure

6. **Retelling as Prose**
   - Read a short ballad
   - Retell the story in prose (3-5 sentences)
   - Compare: What's different? What's lost/gained?
   - Discuss: Why tell a story as a poem?

7. **Musical Elements**
   - Ballads were originally sung
   - Read a ballad and find its rhythm
   - Tap or clap the beat
   - Identify words that create rhythm (stressed/unstressed)
   - Try reading it like a song

8. **Creating Simple Ballad Stanzas**
   - Write one stanza (4 lines) telling a mini-story
   - Use simple AABB rhyme scheme
   - Include: character, action, what happens
   - Example start: "Ein Ritter ritt durch dunklen Wald..."
