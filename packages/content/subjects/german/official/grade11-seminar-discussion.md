---
id: grade11-seminar-discussion
name: Seminar Discussion
description: Leading and contributing to academic discourse, preparing discussion questions
grade: 11
focus: Advanced academic speaking and listening
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Lead academic discussions with clear structure and purpose
  - Prepare open-ended discussion questions that stimulate critical thinking
  - Synthesize contributions from multiple participants into coherent summaries
  - Navigate intellectual disagreement constructively and productively
  - Document and summarize discussion outcomes systematically
  - Build substantively on others' contributions rather than restating
  - Facilitate participation from all discussants while maintaining focus
problem_types:
  - Discussion question design
  - Opening moderation
  - Synthesizing viewpoints
  - Conflict mediation
  - Closing summary
  - Follow-up questioning
  - Documenting outcomes
  - Redirecting discussion
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Discussion on single text with clear interpretive questions
    - Moderation involves basic turn-taking and time management
    - Summary captures main points chronologically
  medium:
    - Discussion integrates multiple texts or complex theoretical concepts
    - Moderation requires balancing competing interpretations
    - Summary identifies patterns and unresolved tensions
  hard:
    - Discussion addresses abstract interdisciplinary questions
    - Moderation navigates philosophical disagreements productively
    - Summary synthesizes insights into new scholarly framework
real_world_context:
  - A literature seminar debates whether Faust's redemption is earned or arbitrary
  - Philosophy students discuss the relationship between language and reality
  - A reading group examines how different authors portray the individual versus society
  - Students moderate discussion on the ethics of literary censorship
  - A seminar explores how Enlightenment ideals influenced Romantic rebellion
  - Discussion leaders help classmates compare Sturm und Drang with modern youth movements
  - Students facilitate debate on whether art should serve political or aesthetic purposes
  - A book club discusses how unreliable narrators challenge reader trust
  - Seminar participants explore connections between historical trauma and literary form
  - Students lead discussion on how film adaptations reinterpret classic texts
  - A philosophy circle examines whether literature reveals or obscures truth
  - Discussion moderators help peers analyze propaganda techniques in political speeches
  - Students synthesize classmates' views on the purpose of studying literary epochs
  - A seminar explores how language shapes thought in Sprachkritik theory
  - Discussion leaders guide analysis of intertextual references across centuries
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid yes/no questions that shut down rather than open discussion
  - Don't create scenarios where one interpretation is obviously correct
  - Avoid topics requiring specialized knowledge not covered in curriculum
  - Don't model moderators silencing valid disagreement inappropriately
  - Avoid discussion questions too broad to address meaningfully in context
version: 1
