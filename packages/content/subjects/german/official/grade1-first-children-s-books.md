Generate a task where {{childName}} practices listening to stories and talking about characters, plot, and events from picture books.

**Focus areas to choose from:**
- Identifying main characters
- Understanding story sequence (beginning, middle, end)
- Retelling simple plots
- Describing favorite parts
- Making connections to the story

## Task Variations

### Variation 1: Character Identification
Present a short story or book description with {{num1}} characters. Ask the student to identify and name the main characters.

Example story excerpt:
*"Der kleine Bär wollte Honig finden. Seine Freundin, die Biene Maja, half ihm dabei. Zusammen suchten sie im Wald."*

Questions:
- Who are the main characters? (*Der kleine Bär, die Biene Maja*)
- Who helped the bear? (*Die Biene Maja*)

### Variation 2: Story Sequence
Show {{num1}} pictures from a story in mixed order. The student must arrange them in the correct sequence (beginning, middle, end).

Example story: *"The Very Hungry Caterpillar"* or *"Der Grüffelo"*
- Picture A: The caterpillar hatches
- Picture B: The caterpillar eats lots of food
- Picture C: The caterpillar becomes a butterfly

Correct order: A → B → C

### Variation 3: Simple Plot Retelling
Read or describe a very short story (3-4 sentences). The student must retell it in their own words.

Example story:
*"Ein kleiner Hase verlor seinen Ball. Er suchte überall. Seine Freunde halfen ihm. Am Ende fanden sie den Ball im Garten."*

Student retells: *"Der Hase hatte seinen Ball verloren. Er und seine Freunde haben gesucht. Sie fanden ihn wieder."*

### Variation 4: Beginning-Middle-End Analysis
Present a familiar children's story. Ask the student to identify what happened at the beginning, middle, and end.

Example using *"Rotkäppchen"* (Little Red Riding Hood):
| Part | What Happened |
|------|---------------|
| Beginning | Rotkäppchen geht zur Großmutter |
| Middle | Sie trifft den Wolf |
| End | Der Jäger rettet sie |

### Variation 5: Favorite Part Discussion
After reading a short story, ask {{num1}} questions about the student's personal response:

Questions:
- *Was war dein Lieblingsteil?* (What was your favorite part?)
- *Welches Bild hat dir am besten gefallen?* (Which picture did you like best?)
- *War die Geschichte lustig oder spannend?* (Was the story funny or exciting?)
- *Welche Figur magst du am liebsten?* (Which character do you like most?)

### Variation 6: Picture-Text Matching
Show {{num1}} pictures from a story and {{num1}} sentences. The student matches each sentence to the correct picture.

Example (from a bear story):
- Picture 1: Bear sleeping in cave → *"Der Bär schläft in seiner Höhle."*
- Picture 2: Bear finding honey → *"Der Bär findet Honig."*
- Picture 3: Bear eating → *"Der Bär isst den Honig."*

### Variation 7: Story Comprehension Questions
Read a short passage from a children's book. Ask {{num1}} comprehension questions.

Example passage:
*"Pipi Langstrumpf wohnt in der Villa Kunterbunt. Sie ist sehr stark und kann ein Pferd hochheben. Ihre besten Freunde sind Tommy und Annika."*

Questions:
- Wo wohnt Pipi? (In der Villa Kunterbunt)
- Was kann sie tun? (Ein Pferd hochheben)
- Wie heißen ihre Freunde? (Tommy und Annika)

### Variation 8: Predict What Happens Next
Read the beginning of a story, then stop. Ask the student to predict what might happen next.

Example:
*"Der kleine Drache fand ein glänzendes Ei. Es war warm. Plötzlich bewegte sich das Ei..."*

Question: *Was passiert jetzt wohl?* (What do you think happens now?)

Possible answers: *Das Ei bricht auf*, *Ein Baby-Drache kommt raus*, etc.
