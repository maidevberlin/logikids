learning_objectives:
  - Master advanced rhetorical techniques from classical rhetoric tradition
  - Deliver persuasive speeches adapted to different audiences and contexts
  - Analyze classical and modern oratory for rhetorical strategies and effectiveness
  - Apply the five canons of rhetoric in speech preparation and delivery
  - Use rhetoric effectively in formal debate and academic argumentation
  - Evaluate ethical dimensions of persuasive communication
problem_types:
  - Classical rhetoric analysis
  - Speech composition
  - Oratory performance
  - Audience adaptation
  - Debate argumentation
  - Rhetorical comparison
  - Ethical evaluation
  - Dispositio structuring
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify classical rhetorical techniques in famous speeches
    - Compose short persuasive speeches with clear structure
    - Analyze how speakers adapt rhetoric to specific audiences
  medium:
    - Apply the five canons of rhetoric to extended speech preparation
    - Compare rhetorical strategies across different orators and time periods
    - Deliver speeches using advanced techniques like appeals to ethos, pathos, logos
  hard:
    - Evaluate sophisticated rhetorical strategies in complex political discourse
    - Create original speeches using classical and modern rhetorical theory
    - Analyze and critique the ethical implications of persuasive techniques in oratory
