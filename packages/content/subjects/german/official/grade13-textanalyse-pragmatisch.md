---
id: grade13-textanalyse-pragmatisch
name: Pragmatic Text Analysis
description: Analyzing non-fictional texts at highest level - essays, speeches, philosophical texts
grade: 13
focus: Advanced analysis of non-fictional discourse and argumentation
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Identify and analyze complex argumentative structures in non-fictional texts
  - Evaluate rhetorical strategies and their effectiveness in context
  - Analyze the relationship between form, content, and persuasive intent
  - Apply pragmatic concepts to understand context-dependent meaning
  - Write comprehensive analyses meeting Abitur evaluation criteria
  - Synthesize linguistic, rhetorical, and content analysis
problem_types:
  - Argumentative structure
  - Rhetorical strategy
  - Thesis identification
  - Evidence evaluation
  - Audience analysis
  - Pragmatic analysis
  - Stylistic function
  - Logical validity
  - Persuasive technique
  - Authorial intent
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify main thesis and explicit arguments
    - Recognize common rhetorical devices
    - Analyze straightforward text structures
  medium:
    - Evaluate implicit argumentation and assumptions
    - Analyze complex rhetorical strategies and their interplay
    - Connect form to persuasive function
  hard:
    - Evaluate logical validity and hidden premises
    - Analyze sophisticated pragmatic strategies and context
    - Synthesize multiple analytical perspectives into coherent interpretation
real_world_context:
  - Students analyze a philosophical essay on artificial intelligence and human consciousness
  - A class examines a political speech addressing climate policy and economic transformation
  - Readers evaluate an opinion piece debating the future of European integration
  - Students analyze a scientific essay explaining the ethics of gene editing
  - A seminar discusses a cultural critique examining social media and democracy
  - Readers examine a journalist's investigation into global supply chains and ethics
  - Students analyze a philosopher's argument about free will and moral responsibility
  - A class evaluates a historian's essay on memory, trauma, and collective identity
  - Readers examine an economist's argument for universal basic income
  - Students analyze a speech from a human rights activist on migration and borders
  - A seminar discusses a literary critic's theory about the novel in the digital age
  - Readers evaluate a scientist's public lecture on climate tipping points
  - Students analyze a sociologist's essay on inequality and social mobility
  - A class examines a legal scholar's argument about privacy in the surveillance age
  - Readers discuss an educator's manifesto on reforming the school system
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid texts requiring specialized technical or scientific knowledge
  - Don't ask for plot summaries or pure content reproduction
  - Avoid questions with single correct interpretations
  - Don't conflate pragmatic text analysis with literary interpretation
  - Avoid texts shorter than 400 words or exceeding exam-appropriate length
