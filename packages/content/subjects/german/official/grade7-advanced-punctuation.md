id: grade7-advanced-punctuation
name: Advanced Punctuation
description: Commas with infinitive phrases and appositives, quotation marks for titles and speech, and punctuation in compound-complex sentences
grade: 7
focus: Advanced punctuation rules for complex sentence structures
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Use commas correctly with infinitive phrases including extended infinitives with zu
  - Apply comma rules for appositives and inserted explanatory phrases
  - Use quotation marks for direct speech, titles of works, and special terminology
  - Punctuate compound-complex sentences with multiple subordinate clauses correctly
  - Distinguish between restrictive and non-restrictive relative clauses in comma placement
  - Apply correction strategies to identify and fix punctuation errors in own writing
  - Recognize how punctuation affects sentence meaning and clarity in complex structures
problem_types:
  - Infinitive phrase punctuation
  - Appositive comma placement
  - Quotation mark usage
  - Compound-complex punctuation
  - Restrictive vs non-restrictive clauses
  - Title punctuation
  - Error identification
  - Punctuation revision
  - Comma omission recognition
  - Multiple clause punctuation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single infinitive phrases with clear zu-infinitive requiring comma
    - Simple appositives with straightforward explanatory content
    - Direct speech with standard speaker tag placement
  medium:
    - Compound-complex sentences with two subordinate clauses requiring comma decisions
    - Multiple infinitive constructions in one sentence requiring judgment
    - Quotation marks combined with other punctuation (commas, periods, question marks)
  hard:
    - Optional comma cases with infinitive phrases requiring meaning analysis
    - Restrictive versus non-restrictive relative clause distinction affecting comma placement
    - Complex nested structures with multiple punctuation rules applying simultaneously
prerequisites:
  - grade6-punctuation
  - grade6-grammar-syntax
real_world_context:
  - Using commas with infinitive phrases and appositives clarifies meaning in formal letters to authorities or businesses
  - Quotation marks distinguish direct speech from narration in creative writing and when citing books or articles in research papers
  - Complex sentence punctuation helps organize multi-clause arguments in persuasive essays and literary analysis
  - Distinguishing restrictive and non-restrictive clauses with commas prevents ambiguity in technical instructions and journalism
  - Correction strategies help identify and fix punctuation errors before submitting written work in all subjects
  - Punctuating dialogue correctly in short stories for school literary magazines or creative writing competitions
  - Using quotation marks for song titles and movie names when writing music or film reviews for student newspapers
  - Applying comma rules when writing detailed explanations in science lab reports with multiple dependent clauses
  - Formatting interview transcripts with proper quotation marks and speaker attributions for journalism projects
  - Punctuating complex arguments in debate preparation materials and formal speech outlines
  - Using appositives with commas to add biographical details about historical figures in history essays
  - Applying punctuation conventions when writing subtitles or captions for video projects and presentations
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid sentences with more than four subordinate clauses
  - Do not include rare or archaic punctuation conventions
  - Avoid infinitive phrases longer than eight words
  - Do not create ambiguous cases with multiple valid interpretations
  - Avoid technical terms beyond standard grade 7 curriculum
