# Sentence Parts

Create a task about sentence parts for {{age}}-year-old {{gender}} at difficulty level {{difficulty}}.

## Task Variations

Choose ONE of these variations:

1. **Subject and Predicate Identification**
   - Present 6-8 sentences
   - Students identify **subject** (who/what acts) and **predicate** (what happens)
   - Mark subjects and predicates with different colors or underlines
   - Examples: "Der Hund bellt.", "Maria spielt Fußball."

2. **Who-Does-What Table**
   - Analyze sentences in a table
   - Columns: **Wer/Was?** (Subject) | **Was tut er/sie/es?** (Predicate)
   - Example: "Die Katze schläft." → Who? Die Katze | Does what? schläft
   - Practice with 6-8 sentences

3. **Finding Objects**
   - Present sentences with objects
   - Identify object by asking "Wen?" (whom) or "Was?" (what)
   - Examples: "Ich lese ein Buch." → Was? ein Buch
   - Table: **Sentence** | **Subject** | **Predicate** | **Object**

4. **Sentence Building**
   - Provide separated sentence parts
   - Students combine them into complete sentences
   - Parts: subjects (der Junge, die Lehrerin), predicates (schreibt, erklärt), objects (einen Brief, die Aufgabe)
   - Create 5-6 logical sentences

5. **Expanding Sentences**
   - Start with simple subject + predicate
   - Add objects and other information
   - Example: "Der Vogel singt." → "Der Vogel singt ein Lied." → "Der Vogel singt ein schönes Lied."
   - Practice expanding 4-5 sentences

6. **Question Method**
   - Find sentence parts by asking questions
   - **Subject**: Wer oder was? (Who or what?)
   - **Predicate**: Was tut das Subjekt? (What does the subject do?)
   - **Object**: Wen oder was? (Whom or what?)
   - Practice with 5-7 sentences

7. **Sentence Part Sorting**
   - Provide mixed sentence parts (10-12 words/phrases)
   - Sort into categories: **Subjects** | **Predicates** | **Objects**
   - Examples: der Mann, läuft, das Auto, kauft, die Blume
   - Create sentences using parts from each category

8. **Error Correction**
   - Present sentences with missing or wrong sentence parts
   - Students identify what's missing
   - Add the missing part to complete the sentence
   - Examples: "Die Katze." (missing predicate), "Liest das Buch." (missing subject)
