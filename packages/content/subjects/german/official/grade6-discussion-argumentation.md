---
id: grade6-discussion-argumentation
name: Discussion and Argumentation
description: Master conversation skills in various contexts, state opinions with reasoned support, apply conflict resolution strategies, respond constructively to opposing viewpoints, and build on others' contributions
grade: 6
focus: Advanced discussion skills and structured argumentation
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Identify and adapt to different conversation contexts such as classroom discussions and formal group debates
  - Apply discussion rules including listening attentively, letting others finish, and asking clarifying questions
  - State personal opinions with reasoned support and evidence from relevant sources
  - Use conflict resolution strategies to address disagreements constructively
  - Respond constructively to opposing viewpoints by acknowledging valid points and refining arguments
  - Build on others' contributions in discussions by extending ideas or offering alternative perspectives
  - Distinguish between productive and unproductive discussion behaviors in various contexts
problem_types:
  - Discussion rule application
  - Argument construction
  - Counter-argument response
  - Conflict scenario resolution
  - Opinion justification
  - Turn-taking etiquette
  - Contribution building
  - Context adaptation
  - Productive behavior identification
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Clear-cut situations with explicit discussion rules and simple opinion statements
    - Single-perspective conflicts with obvious resolution strategies
    - Basic turn-taking scenarios with 2-3 participants
  medium:
    - Multiple perspectives requiring comparison and synthesis of different viewpoints
    - Building structured arguments with 2-3 supporting reasons or evidence pieces
    - Context-specific discussion adaptations between informal and formal settings
  hard:
    - Complex conflicts involving multiple valid competing arguments requiring weighing tradeoffs
    - Extended multi-turn discussions requiring active listening and synthesizing contributions
    - Distinguishing subtle differences between productive critique and unproductive behavior
prerequisites:
  - grade4-discussion-skills
real_world_context:
  - Participating in classroom debates about ethical dilemmas in literature or historical events
  - Mediating peer conflicts by helping friends understand multiple perspectives and find common ground
  - Contributing constructively to group project planning by building on teammates' ideas
  - Defending a research position with evidence from multiple sources in a presentation
  - Adapting discussion style when moving from casual peer conversations to formal class discussions
  - Discussing environmental issues in science class, weighing different conservation strategies with supporting data
  - Negotiating team roles and responsibilities when organizing a school event or sports team activity
  - Participating in family discussions about vacation plans, presenting preferences with logical reasoning
  - Engaging in book club conversations where members interpret characters' actions differently
  - Contributing to student council meetings about proposed school policy changes
  - Discussing historical decisions in a time-travel adventure, evaluating choices different historical figures made
  - Debating fantasy world governance systems in role-playing games, considering fairness and consequences
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid topics requiring specialized knowledge beyond 11-12 year experience
  - Don't expect formal debate tournament structures or advanced rhetoric
  - Avoid conflicts requiring sophisticated emotional regulation or moral philosophy
  - Don't include more than 6 distinct participant voices in discussion scenarios
  - Avoid opinion topics with objectively verifiable correct answers
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
