---
id: grade4-reading-comprehension
name: Advanced Reading Comprehension
description: Analyzing texts, finding central ideas, supporting claims with evidence, comparing texts
grade: 4
focus: Text analysis and comprehension strategies
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Use pre-reading strategies to orient and predict text content
  - Search for and locate specific information within texts
  - Retell texts accurately using own words
  - Identify central ideas and main messages in texts
  - Support claims with specific textual evidence
  - Find similarities and differences when comparing multiple texts
  - Make inferences beyond literal text content
problem_types:
  - Main idea identification
  - Evidence location
  - Text summary
  - Information search
  - Text comparison
  - Inference making
  - Prediction verification
  - Author's purpose
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Texts of 200-400 words with clear structure
    - Explicitly stated main ideas and information
    - Single straightforward inference required
  medium:
    - Texts of 400-700 words with moderate complexity
    - Main ideas require synthesis of multiple paragraphs
    - Multiple inferences or comparisons needed
  hard:
    - Texts of 700-1000 words with complex structure
    - Implicit main ideas requiring deep analysis
    - Multiple text comparison or complex inference chains
prerequisites:
  - grade3-reading-strategies
  - grade3-text-analysis
real_world_context:
  - Reading news articles to understand current events and distinguish facts from opinions
  - Following instructions in hobby guides, game manuals, or craft projects
  - Researching topics of personal interest using library books and websites
  - Understanding letters, emails, and messages from friends and family
  - Comparing product descriptions when making purchasing decisions
  - Reading recipe instructions carefully to successfully bake cookies or prepare meals with family
  - Analyzing movie or book reviews to decide what to watch or read during leisure time
  - Understanding safety instructions for sports equipment, theme park rides, or science experiments
  - Reading informational plaques at museums or zoos to learn about exhibits and animals
  - Following multi-step assembly instructions for toys, furniture, or craft kits
  - Researching animals or historical topics for school projects using multiple sources
  - Reading travel brochures or guidebooks when planning family trips or vacations
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid texts with vocabulary significantly above grade 4 level
  - Don't ask questions answerable without reading the text
  - Avoid ambiguous questions with multiple valid interpretations
  - Don't require knowledge of topics unfamiliar to 9-10 year olds
  - Avoid texts requiring specialized cultural or historical knowledge
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
---
