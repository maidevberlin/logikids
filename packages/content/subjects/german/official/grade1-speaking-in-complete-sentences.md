Generate a task where {{childName}} practices speaking in complete sentences to describe experiences, pictures, or express wishes.

**Focus areas to choose from:**
- Completing sentence starters
- Describing pictures in sentences
- Answering questions in complete sentences
- Building sentences from word prompts
- Telling about experiences

## Task Variations

### Variation 1: Sentence Completion
Provide {{num1}} sentence starters. The student must complete each one with their own words to make a full sentence.

Example starters:
- *Ich mag...*
- *Mein Lieblingstier ist...*
- *Am Wochenende...*
- *Ich kann...*
- *In der Schule...*

### Variation 2: Picture Description
Show {{num1}} pictures. For each picture, the student must describe what they see using at least one complete sentence.

Example pictures and possible sentences:
- Picture of a red ball: *Der Ball ist rot.*
- Picture of a child playing: *Das Kind spielt im Garten.*
- Picture of the sun: *Die Sonne scheint hell.*

### Variation 3: Question and Answer Practice
Ask {{num1}} simple questions. The student must answer each question in a complete sentence (not just one word).

Example questions:
- *Was ist deine Lieblingsfarbe?* → *Meine Lieblingsfarbe ist blau.* (not just "blau")
- *Was machst du gerne?* → *Ich spiele gerne Fußball.*
- *Wo wohnst du?* → *Ich wohne in [Stadt].*

### Variation 4: Build a Sentence
Provide {{num1}} sets of scrambled words. The student must arrange them into a complete, meaningful sentence.

Example word sets:
- *Ball, ist, rot, Der* → *Der Ball ist rot.*
- *spiele, Ich, gerne, Fußball* → *Ich spiele gerne Fußball.*
- *Schule, gehe, zur, Ich* → *Ich gehe zur Schule.*

### Variation 5: Tell Me About...
Give {{num1}} simple prompts. The student must speak 2-3 complete sentences about each topic.

Example prompts:
- *Erzähle mir von deinem Morgen.* (Tell me about your morning)
- *Was hast du gestern gemacht?* (What did you do yesterday?)
- *Was ist dein Lieblingsspiel?* (What is your favorite game?)

### Variation 6: Expand the Sentence
Provide {{num1}} very short sentences (2 words). The student must expand each one into a longer, more detailed sentence.

Examples:
- *Ball rollt.* → *Der rote Ball rollt über den Boden.*
- *Sonne scheint.* → *Die Sonne scheint hell am Himmel.*
- *Kind lacht.* → *Das Kind lacht fröhlich.*

### Variation 7: Wish and Need Expressions
Present {{num1}} scenarios. The student must express their wish or need in a complete sentence.

Example scenarios:
- You want to play outside → *Ich möchte draußen spielen.*
- You need help with homework → *Ich brauche Hilfe bei den Hausaufgaben.*
- You want a drink → *Ich hätte gerne etwas zu trinken.*
- You feel tired → *Ich bin müde.*

### Variation 8: Story Sentences
Show a sequence of {{num1}} pictures that tell a simple story. The student must describe each picture with a complete sentence to create a short story.

Example sequence (3 pictures):
1. Picture 1: *Das Kind geht zur Schule.*
2. Picture 2: *Es lernt Mathematik.*
3. Picture 3: *Danach spielt es mit Freunden.*
