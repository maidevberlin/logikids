**Create a task where {{student_name}} learns and practices effective internet research skills.**

Your task should:
- Focus on one of these variations:
  1. **Search Strategies**: Learn effective keyword selection and search techniques (specific terms, quotation marks, Boolean operators)
  2. **Source Evaluation**: Assess website credibility using criteria (author credentials, publication date, domain type .org/.edu/.com, purpose)
  3. **Information Reliability**: Distinguish between reliable sources (academic, official) and unreliable ones (personal blogs, unverified sites)
  4. **Citation Basics**: Practice citing online sources with required information (author, title, website, URL, access date)
  5. **Plagiarism Awareness**: Understand what plagiarism is and how to avoid it (paraphrasing, quoting, citing)
  6. **Research Planning**: Plan research by formulating questions, identifying needed information, selecting appropriate sources
  7. **Cross-Verification**: Learn to verify information by checking multiple independent sources
  8. **Safe Research**: Understand internet safety, privacy concerns, and recognizing inappropriate content

- Use age-appropriate research topics (school subjects, hobbies, current events, historical topics)
- Provide examples of good and poor sources
- Show proper citation format
- Include search strategy examples
- Use tables to compare sources or organize evaluation criteria

**Important**:
- Ensure diverse research topics each time (science, history, literature, current events)
- Vary the skill focus (searching, evaluating, citing, planning)
- Change the specific examples and scenarios
- Provide fresh, engaging research tasks
