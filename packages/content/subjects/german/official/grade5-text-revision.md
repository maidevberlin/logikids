---
id: grade5-text-revision
name: Text Revision
description: Planning, reviewing and improving own and peer texts
grade: 5
focus: Text planning and revision strategies
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Use planning methods before writing including brainstorming, clustering, mind-mapping, and outlines
  - Evaluate own texts using criteria for content, structure, grammatical correctness, and style
  - Evaluate peer texts constructively using established revision criteria
  - Apply revision methods including text analysis checklists and writing conferences
  - Give constructive feedback to peers about their written work
  - Receive and implement feedback to improve own texts
  - Use word processing tools for composition and revision tasks
problem_types:
  - Planning method selection
  - Checklist-based revision
  - Peer feedback scenarios
  - Error identification
  - Revision prioritization
  - Conference preparation
  - Digital editing tasks
  - Feedback implementation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Simple texts with 3-5 obvious errors to identify
    - Basic planning methods with clear templates
    - Straightforward checklist items covering single criteria
  medium:
    - Multi-paragraph texts requiring comprehensive evaluation
    - Multiple revision criteria applied simultaneously
    - Peer feedback requiring balanced positive and constructive comments
  hard:
    - Complex texts with subtle structural and stylistic issues
    - Prioritizing multiple revision needs across different criteria
    - Advanced planning methods combined for challenging text types
real_world_context:
  - Students prepare presentations for class by planning content and revising drafts based on teacher feedback
  - Peer writing groups in school where students exchange work and provide constructive suggestions for improvement
  - Using computers at home or school to type and revise essays, with features like spell-check and editing tools
  - Planning a personal letter or email by organizing thoughts before writing and checking clarity before sending
  - Creating school newspaper articles that go through multiple drafts with editor feedback
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid vague feedback like just better or improve this without specifics
  - Do not present revision as punishment rather than improvement opportunity
  - Avoid checklists with more than 5 criteria to prevent overwhelm
  - Do not expect perfect texts after single revision round
  - Avoid peer feedback scenarios with only negative criticism
prerequisites:
  - grade4-text-revision
