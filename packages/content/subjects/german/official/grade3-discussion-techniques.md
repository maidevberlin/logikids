# Discussion Techniques

Create a task about discussion techniques for {{age}}-year-old {{gender}} at difficulty level {{difficulty}}.

## Task Variations

Choose ONE of these variations:

1. **Opinion with Reasons**
   - Present a discussion topic (e.g., "Should students wear uniforms?")
   - Students write their opinion and 3 reasons
   - Structure: **Meinung** (Opinion) | **Begründung 1** | **Begründung 2** | **Begründung 3**
   - Use phrases: *Ich denke, dass...*, *weil...*, *außerdem...*

2. **Argument Response Practice**
   - Provide statements from different viewpoints
   - Students practice agreeing and disagreeing with reasons
   - Phrases: *Ich stimme zu, weil...*, *Ich bin anderer Meinung, denn...*, *Das sehe ich auch so.*
   - Example topic: pets in school, longer breaks, homework amount

3. **Discussion Rules Poster**
   - Create a list of good discussion rules
   - Include: listen carefully, raise hand, stay on topic, respect others
   - Students write 5-6 rules in complete sentences
   - Add examples: *Gut: "Ich finde, dass..." Schlecht: "Das ist dumm!"*

4. **Topic vs. Off-Topic**
   - Present a discussion topic
   - Show 6-8 statements
   - Students identify which stay on topic and which don't
   - Topic example: "School lunch" - On topic: "Das Essen könnte gesünder sein."

5. **Building on Others' Ideas**
   - Show a chain of discussion contributions
   - Students practice connecting to previous speakers
   - Phrases: *Wie Max schon gesagt hat...*, *Ich stimme Anna zu und möchte ergänzen...*
   - Write 3-4 connected statements on one topic

6. **Disagreeing Politely**
   - Present rude vs. polite disagreement
   - Students rewrite rude statements politely
   - Rude: "Das ist falsch!" → Polite: "Ich sehe das anders, weil..."
   - Practice 5-6 transformations

7. **Discussion Preparation**
   - Given a topic, students prepare for both sides
   - Table: **Pro-Argumente** | **Contra-Argumente**
   - Example: "Should there be more sports in school?"
   - Write 3 arguments for each side

8. **Active Listening Responses**
   - Present someone's opinion
   - Students write responses showing they listened
   - Phrases: *Du meinst also, dass...*, *Wenn ich dich richtig verstehe...*, *Kannst du erklären, warum...?*
   - Include follow-up questions
