---
id: grade6-text-revision
name: Text Revision and Editing
description: Peer feedback, revision strategies, editing with established criteria
grade: 6
focus: Writing process - collaborative revision and strategic editing
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Evaluate and revise own and peer writing using established criteria
  - Employ revision methods including feedback forms and collaborative editing
  - Use word processing software for composition and revision
  - Check for coherence, clarity, and audience appropriateness
  - Apply revision criteria including verstandlichkeit, Adressatenbezug, Textsorte, and sprachliche Richtigkeit
  - Conduct writing conferences (Schreibkonferenz) effectively
  - Distinguish between content revision and surface editing
problem_types:
  - Error identification
  - Feedback formulation
  - Revision priority setting
  - Coherence improvement
  - Clarity enhancement
  - Peer feedback response
  - Self-editing checklist
  - Writing conference simulation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Short texts with obvious errors, guided revision criteria, single focus area
    - Simple feedback forms with 3-5 checkable criteria
    - Clear coherence issues (missing transitions, repeated information)
  medium:
    - Multi-paragraph texts requiring multiple revision aspects simultaneously
    - Constructive peer feedback writing with specific improvement suggestions
    - Balancing clarity, audience appropriateness, and text type conventions
  hard:
    - Extended texts needing subtle style refinement and nuanced improvements
    - Complex writing conferences addressing multiple competing revision priorities
    - Advanced audience adaptation and genre-specific revision strategies
prerequisites:
  - grade4-text-revision
  - grade6-narrative-writing
  - grade6-informative-writing
real_world_context:
  - Students conduct peer review sessions before submitting essays or reports for grading
  - Young writers participate in writing workshops where they give and receive constructive feedback
  - Students revise and polish texts for school publications, blogs, or competitions
  - Adolescents edit their written work using digital tools and collaborative platforms
  - Students prepare presentations and written materials by iteratively improving clarity and audience fit
  - Revising creative stories after teacher feedback to strengthen character development and plot coherence
  - Using writing conference protocols to help classmates improve their science lab reports
  - Editing draft emails to teachers or administrators to ensure appropriate tone and clarity
  - Revising persuasive essays by checking that arguments align with intended audience values
  - Improving procedural writing by testing instructions with a partner and clarifying confusing steps
  - Using word processing spell-check and grammar tools while maintaining critical judgment about suggestions
  - Revising historical narratives to ensure factual accuracy while maintaining engaging storytelling
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid focusing only on surface errors - include content and structure revision
  - Don't provide complete corrections - guide students to identify and fix issues
  - Avoid unrealistic peer feedback scenarios with hostile or unhelpful responses
  - Don't mix too many unrelated revision criteria in a single task
  - Avoid texts where revision priorities are unclear or contradictory
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
