# Active Listening

Create a task about active listening for {{age}}-year-old {{gender}} at difficulty level {{difficulty}}.

## Task Variations

Choose ONE of these variations:

1. **Summarizing Practice**
   - Present a short text or story (5-7 sentences)
   - Students summarize in 2-3 sentences
   - Focus on main points, not details
   - Check: Did they capture the essential information?

2. **Follow-Up Questions**
   - Provide a statement or short presentation
   - Students write 4-5 follow-up questions
   - Questions should show understanding and interest
   - Examples: "Warum hast du...?", "Kannst du mehr über... erzählen?", "Was meinst du mit...?"

3. **Feedback Formula**
   - Practice giving constructive feedback
   - Structure: **2 Positive Dinge** + **1 Verbesserungsvorschlag** + **1 Frage**
   - Example: "Mir gefiel... Gut war auch... Vielleicht könntest du... Hast du...?"
   - Apply to a sample presentation or story

4. **Listening Checklist**
   - Create a good listener checklist
   - Items: eyes on speaker, not interrupting, nodding, asking questions
   - Students write 6-8 good listening behaviors
   - Compare good vs. poor listening examples

5. **Detail Recall**
   - Read a short text (4-6 sentences)
   - Students answer specific questions about details
   - Questions: Who? What? When? Where? Why?
   - Tests careful listening for facts

6. **Paraphrasing Exercise**
   - Present 4-5 statements
   - Students rephrase in their own words
   - Original: "Ich mag den Sommer, weil es warm ist und ich schwimmen kann."
   - Paraphrase: "Du findest Sommer toll wegen der Wärme und dem Schwimmen."

7. **Non-Verbal Listening Signals**
   - Identify good and bad listening body language
   - Table: **Good Signals** | **Bad Signals**
   - Examples: nodding vs. looking away, eye contact vs. fidgeting
   - Explain why each matters

8. **Understanding Emotions**
   - Present statements with emotional content
   - Students identify the speaker's feeling
   - Write an appropriate response showing understanding
   - Example: "Das macht mich traurig." → "Ich verstehe, dass du traurig bist. Möchtest du darüber sprechen?"
