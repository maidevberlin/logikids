# Sentence Structure

Create a task where {{name}} practices understanding sentence structure appropriate for {{age}}-year-olds at {{difficulty}} level in {{language}}.

## Variation 1: Subject and Predicate Identification
Explain that every sentence has two main parts:
- **Subject (Subjekt)**: Who or what the sentence is about
- **Predicate (Prädikat)**: What the subject does or what happens

Give sentences and have students identify both parts:

| Sentence | Subject (Who/What?) | Predicate (What happens?) |
|----------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| *Der Hund bellt laut.* | Der Hund | bellt laut |
| *Die Kinder spielen.* | Die Kinder | spielen |
| *Die Sonne scheint.* | Die Sonne | scheint |

## Variation 2: Sentence Building Blocks
Provide separate subject and predicate cards for students to combine:

**Subject cards:**
- *Der Vogel*
- *Meine Schwester*
- *Das Auto*
- *Die Blume*

**Predicate cards:**
- *singt schön*
- *fährt schnell*
- *liest ein Buch*
- *blüht im Garten*

Mix and match to create different sentences. Check that they make sense!

## Variation 3: Complete or Incomplete?
Present word groups and have students decide if they are complete sentences:

✓ Complete: *Die Katze schläft.* (has subject and predicate)
✗ Incomplete: *Die kleine Katze* (only subject, no predicate)
✗ Incomplete: *Schläft auf dem Sofa* (only predicate, no subject)
✓ Complete: *Ich spiele gerne.* (has subject and predicate)

## Variation 4: Sentence Boundaries
Give a text without punctuation and have students mark where sentences begin and end:

*der hund läuft durch den park die kinder spielen ball die sonne scheint hell*

Rewrite with correct capitalization and punctuation:
*Der Hund läuft durch den Park. Die Kinder spielen Ball. Die Sonne scheint hell.*

## Variation 5: Expanding Sentences
Start with a basic sentence and expand it step by step:

**Basic:** *Der Hund bellt.*

**Add adjective:** *Der große Hund bellt.*

**Add time:** *Der große Hund bellt nachts.*

**Add place:** *Der große Hund bellt nachts im Garten.*

Show how sentences can grow while keeping subject and predicate.

## Variation 6: Question Sentence Structure
Compare statements and questions:

| Statement | Question |
|-----------|----------|
| *Du gehst nach Hause.* | *Gehst du nach Hause?* |
| *Sie liest ein Buch.* | *Liest sie ein Buch?* |
| *Er spielt Fußball.* | *Spielt er Fußball?* |

Notice how word order changes in questions. The verb moves to the front!

## Variation 7: Sentence Repair
Give broken or scrambled sentences and have students fix them:

**Scrambled:** *rennt der Hund schnell*
**Corrected:** *Der Hund rennt schnell.*

**Scrambled:** *die Blumen im Garten schön blühen*
**Corrected:** *Die schönen Blumen blühen im Garten.*

Check:
- Capital letter at start
- Correct word order (subject-verb)
- Period at end

## Variation 8: Subject-Verb Agreement
Teach that the verb must match the subject:

| Subject | Verb Form |
|---------|-----------|
| *Ich* | *lese* |
| *Du* | *liest* |
| *Er/Sie/Es* | *liest* |
| *Wir* | *lesen* |
| *Ihr* | *lest* |
| *Sie* | *lesen* |

Practice: Fill in the correct verb form:
- *Ich ___ (spielen)* → *Ich spiele*
- *Du ___ (malen)* → *Du malst*
- *Wir ___ (singen)* → *Wir singen*
