id: grade13-abitur-essay
name: Abitur Essay Writing
description: Mastering all Abitur essay formats under exam conditions
grade: 13
focus: Academic writing under exam pressure
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Master the structure and conventions of literary interpretation essays
  - Write text-based argumentative essays (textgebundene Erörterung) under time constraints
  - Produce material-based texts (materialgestütztes Schreiben) synthesizing multiple sources
  - Analyze pragmatic texts (speeches, essays, articles) with rhetorical awareness
  - Develop thesis-driven arguments balancing analysis with personal insight
  - Apply all formal requirements (introduction, body, conclusion, transitions)
  - Manage time effectively during 300-minute exam writing periods
problem_types:
  - Literary interpretation
  - Text-based argumentation
  - Material synthesis
  - Comparative analysis
  - Pragmatic text analysis
  - Argumentative essay
  - Opinion piece
  - Speech analysis
  - Poetry comparison
  - Structured position paper
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single-text analysis with clear structure
    - Straightforward thesis development
    - Basic integration of textual evidence
  medium:
    - Multi-text comparison requiring synthesis
    - Complex argumentative structures with counterarguments
    - Integration of historical and literary context
  hard:
    - Unseen texts requiring rapid analysis under exam conditions
    - Multiple source synthesis with original positioning
    - Sophisticated argumentation addressing nuanced literary questions
prerequisites:
  - grade12-text-interpretation-abitur
  - grade11-wissenschaftliches-schreiben
  - grade10-literary-essay
real_world_context:
  - Analyzing a contemporary dystopian novel's critique of surveillance society
  - Comparing Romantic nature poetry with modern environmental literature
  - Evaluating arguments about social media's impact on political discourse
  - Interpreting a Kafka text through the lens of alienation and bureaucracy
  - Synthesizing sources about language change and gender-inclusive German
  - Writing about the role of literature in processing historical trauma
  - Analyzing a political speech's rhetorical strategies and effectiveness
  - Comparing Expressionist war poetry with contemporary conflict literature
  - Developing a position on the value of studying classical vs. contemporary texts
  - Interpreting drama excerpts about generational conflict and authority
  - Evaluating essays about digital reading vs. print culture
  - Writing about migration narratives in German-language literature
  - Analyzing satire's effectiveness in critiquing social problems
  - Comparing different authors' treatments of identity and belonging
  - Synthesizing materials about artificial intelligence and creative writing
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid vague thesis statements that cannot be proven with textual evidence
  - Don't write plot summaries instead of analytical interpretation
  - Never ignore formal requirements (structure, length, academic register)
  - Avoid using only one source when multiple are provided
  - Don't skip time management practice for 300-minute exam conditions
