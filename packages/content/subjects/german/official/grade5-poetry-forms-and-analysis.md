**Create a task where {{student_name}} analyzes poetry forms and poetic techniques.**

Your task should:
- Focus on one of these variations:
  1. **Rhyme Scheme Identification**: Identify and name rhyme schemes (**Paarreim** aa bb, **Kreuzreim** ab ab, **umarmender Reim** ab ba)
  2. **Meter Analysis**: Identify the meter/rhythm, marking stressed (x́) and unstressed (x) syllables
  3. **Meter Types**: Distinguish **Jambus** (unstressed-stressed) from **Trochäus** (stressed-unstressed)
  4. **Stanza Forms**: Analyze stanza structure (number of lines, line length, pattern)
  5. **Lyrical I**: Explain the concept of the **lyrisches Ich** and how it differs from the poet
  6. **Poetic Devices**: Identify devices like **Metapher, Vergleich, Personifikation, Alliteration**
  7. **Sound Analysis**: Analyze how sound patterns (rhyme, rhythm, alliteration) affect meaning and mood
  8. **Interpretation**: Interpret a poem's meaning considering form, content, and poetic devices

- Provide age-appropriate poems (4-16 lines)
- Use clear examples of rhyme schemes and meter
- Include nature poetry, emotion poetry, or narrative poetry
- Use tables to organize analysis (rhyme scheme, meter pattern, devices)
- Mark stressed/unstressed syllables when analyzing meter

**Important**:
- Ensure diverse poem types each time (nature, emotions, seasons, experiences)
- Vary the formal focus (rhyme, meter, devices, interpretation)
- Change the specific poems and poetic techniques
- Provide fresh, engaging poetry examples
