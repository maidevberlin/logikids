---
id: grade7-argumentative-writing
name: Argumentative Writing
description: Writing formal argumentative texts including letters, organizing arguments by validity, and supporting claims with reasoning and evidence
grade: 7
focus: Formal argumentation and persuasive writing
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Support claims with reasoning and concrete examples from relevant sources
  - Arrange arguments strategically by validity from weakest to strongest
  - Construct linear argumentative texts following formal letter conventions
  - Distinguish between factual support and opinion-based claims in arguments
  - Use varied language techniques including precise word choice and subordinate clauses
  - Address counterarguments to strengthen overall position
  - Evaluate and revise own and peer writing using appropriate argumentation criteria
problem_types:
  - Claim formulation
  - Argument arrangement
  - Evidence selection
  - Counterargument integration
  - Formal letter structure
  - Thesis development
  - Register adaptation
  - Argument validity assessment
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single clear thesis with 2-3 supporting arguments, provided structural framework
    - Familiar everyday topics with straightforward positions and obvious supporting evidence
    - Formal letter format with template guidance for salutation, body, and closing
  medium:
    - Complex thesis requiring 4-5 supporting arguments arranged strategically
    - Topics requiring distinction between strong and weak evidence from multiple sources
    - Independent formal letter composition with appropriate register and persuasive language
  hard:
    - Nuanced positions requiring counterargument integration and rebuttal
    - Abstract topics demanding precise terminology and sophisticated logical connections
    - Multiple argument strategies requiring subordinate clauses and rhetorical variation
prerequisites:
  - grade6-informative-writing
  - grade6-discussion-argumentation
  - grade6-text-revision
real_world_context:
  - Writing a formal letter to the school principal arguing for extended library hours with supporting evidence
  - Drafting a letter to a local government official advocating for a new bike lane with logical reasoning
  - Creating a formal complaint letter to a company about a defective product with structured claims
  - Composing an argumentative piece for the school newspaper about homework policies supported by research
  - Writing a persuasive text about social media usage policies, addressing counterarguments from opposing viewpoints
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid topics requiring political sophistication beyond 12-13 year experience
  - Don't expect formal rhetoric terminology like ethos, pathos, logos
  - Avoid arguments that rely on statistics or data analysis beyond grade level
  - Don't mix informal conversational tone with formal letter conventions
  - Avoid counterarguments so complex they require philosophical reasoning
version: 1
