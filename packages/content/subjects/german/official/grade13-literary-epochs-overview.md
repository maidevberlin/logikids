learning_objectives:
  - Synthesize knowledge of all major German literary epochs from Baroque to present
  - Identify continuities and ruptures across literary periods
  - Compare thematic, formal, and philosophical developments across epochs
  - Apply epochal knowledge flexibly to analyze unfamiliar texts
  - Trace the evolution of literary concepts across time periods
  - Situate individual works within broader historical-literary trajectories
  - Answer comparative epoch questions under exam conditions
problem_types:
  - Epoch comparison
  - Thematic tracing
  - Formal evolution
  - Philosophical development
  - Period identification
  - Contextual placement
  - Historical trajectory
  - Movement influence
  - Aesthetic continuity
  - Literary revolution analysis
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify epoch based on clear formal or thematic markers
    - Compare two adjacent epochs with explicit guidance
    - Recognize basic continuities between periods
  medium:
    - Compare non-adjacent epochs identifying indirect influences
    - Trace thematic development across three or more periods
    - Analyze how historical events shaped multiple literary movements
  hard:
    - Synthesize insights from all major epochs to address complex questions
    - Identify subtle influences and transformations across centuries
    - Defend nuanced positions about literary-historical relationships
prerequisites:
  - grade12-romanticism
  - grade12-realism-naturalism
  - grade12-expressionism
  - grade11-classical-drama
  - grade11-enlightenment-literature
  - grade11-sturm-und-drang
real_world_context:
  - Tracing nature imagery from Baroque vanitas motifs through Romantic Sehnsucht to modern ecological consciousness
  - Comparing heroic ideals in Classical drama with anti-heroes in Naturalism and absurdist theater
  - Following the evolution of individual vs. society conflict from Sturm und Drang to Expressionism to contemporary literature
  - Analyzing how war is represented across Baroque, Napoleonic-era, WWI Expressionist, and post-WWII texts
  - Comparing Enlightenment rationalism with Romantic feeling with modernist fragmentation
  - Tracing urban alienation from Naturalist social drama through Expressionist city poetry to contemporary prose
  - Comparing language experimentation in Baroque, Romanticism, Expressionism, and postmodern texts
  - Following representations of gender roles from Enlightenment ideals through Realism to contemporary discussions
  - Analyzing religious crisis across epochs from Baroque piety to Enlightenment skepticism to modern existentialism
  - 'Comparing poetic forms: Baroque sonnet, Classical verse drama, Romantic fragment, Expressionist free verse'
  - Tracing concepts of genius from Sturm und Drang Geniekult to Romantic artist figures to modernist doubt
  - Following the development of narrative perspective from Classical omniscience to modernist stream-of-consciousness
  - Comparing social criticism in Enlightenment satire, Naturalist drama, and contemporary engaged literature
  - Analyzing how technological change appears in Realist railways, Expressionist machinery, and digital-age texts
  - Tracing the artist's relationship to society from Classical idealism through Romantic isolation to political engagement
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid treating epochs as isolated units without connections or influences
  - Don't reduce epochs to simplistic formulas or single characteristics
  - Never ignore historical context when comparing literary periods
  - Avoid anachronistic judgments applying modern values to historical texts
  - Don't confuse chronological proximity with artistic similarity
