**Create a task where {{student_name}} analyzes or works creatively with traditional fairy tales or fables.**

Your task should:
- Focus on one of these variations:
  1. **Structural Analysis**: Examine the typical structure of a fairy tale (opening formula, magical elements, happy ending, etc.) using a specific example
  2. **Version Comparison**: Compare two different versions of the same fairy tale (traditional vs. modern, different cultural versions, or different authors)
  3. **Fable Interpretation**: Analyze a classic fable, identifying characters, conflict, and the moral lesson
  4. **Modern Transfer**: Rewrite a fairy tale or fable in a contemporary setting while maintaining the core message
  5. **Character Analysis**: Examine typical fairy tale or fable characters (heroes, villains, tricksters) and their symbolic roles
  6. **Moral Discussion**: Discuss whether a fable's moral is still relevant today and provide examples
  7. **Cross-Cultural Comparison**: Compare similar fairy tales or fables from different cultures
  8. **Creative Extension**: Write an alternative ending or sequel to a fairy tale that changes or deepens its meaning

- Use age-appropriate fairy tales and fables (Grimm, Aesop, La Fontaine, Lessing)
- Include specific text examples when analyzing structure or content
- Encourage critical thinking about moral messages
- Connect traditional stories to modern life
- Use tables to organize comparisons or structural elements

**Important**:
- Ensure fresh, engaging examples each time
- Vary the specific fairy tales and fables used
- Change the analytical focus (structure, character, moral, cultural context)
- Provide diverse creative writing prompts
