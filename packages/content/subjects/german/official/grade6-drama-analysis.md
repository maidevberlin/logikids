**Create a task where {{student_name}} analyzes dramatic texts and theatrical elements.**

Your task should:
- Focus on one of these variations:
  1. **Drama Structure**: Analyze the structure of a drama (acts/**Akte**, scenes/**Szenen**, exposition, climax, resolution)
  2. **Conflict Analysis**: Identify and trace the central conflict and how it develops through the drama
  3. **Dialogue Analysis**: Analyze how characters are revealed through dialogue and speech patterns
  4. **Stage Directions**: Understand the role of **Regieanweisungen** (stage directions) for setting, action, and tone
  5. **Character Conflict**: Analyze conflicts between characters and their causes
  6. **Dramatic Techniques**: Identify techniques like **Monolog, Dialog, Beiseitesprechen** (aside)
  7. **Text vs. Performance**: Compare how a written scene could be interpreted differently in performance
  8. **Scene Analysis**: Analyze a key scene for turning points, revelations, or conflicts

- Provide drama excerpts (150-300 words) with dialogue and stage directions
- Use age-appropriate youth drama or classic scenes
- Include clear examples of dramatic elements
- Show dialogue formatting and stage directions
- Use tables to organize analysis (characters, conflicts, structure)

**Important**:
- Ensure diverse drama types each time (realistic, historical, comic, serious)
- Vary the analytical focus (structure, conflict, dialogue, performance)
- Change the specific scenes and situations
- Provide fresh, engaging drama examples
