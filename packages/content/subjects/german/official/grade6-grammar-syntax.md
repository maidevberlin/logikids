---
id: grade6-grammar-syntax
name: Grammar and Syntax
description: Parts of speech, active and passive voice, sentence components, and clause types
grade: 6
focus: Grammatical terminology and sentence structure analysis
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Use correct grammatical terminology in discussion
  - Identify all parts of speech and apply them functionally
  - Distinguish active from passive voice and employ correctly for rhetorical effect
  - Use verb tenses correctly (present, past, perfect, future)
  - Recognize sentence components (predicatives, objects, adverbials)
  - Identify clause types (temporal, causal, purposive, modal, relative)
  - Differentiate simple and compound sentences from complex sentences
problem_types:
  - Part-of-speech identification
  - Active-passive transformation
  - Sentence component labeling
  - Clause type identification
  - Tense usage selection
  - Sentence combining
  - Grammatical error correction
  - Subject-object clause recognition
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Clear examples with common structures and single grammatical focus
    - Familiar vocabulary in straightforward sentences
    - One grammatical concept per question
  medium:
    - Mixed structures requiring identification of multiple components
    - Transformation tasks between grammatical forms
    - Context-dependent grammatical choices
  hard:
    - Ambiguous cases requiring careful grammatical analysis
    - Stylistic choices involving multiple valid grammatical options
    - Complex sentences combining multiple clause types
real_world_context:
  - Understanding and writing formal emails or letters with appropriate grammatical structures
  - Analyzing how authors use active versus passive voice to create different effects in texts
  - Recognizing how sentence structure affects clarity and emphasis in news articles
  - Using grammatical terminology to discuss and improve writing in peer revision sessions
  - Identifying how different clause types help organize complex information in textbooks
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid overly technical grammar terms beyond grade 6 curriculum
  - Don't use archaic or literary German forms unfamiliar to 11-12 year olds
  - Avoid sentences longer than 25 words in complexity assessment
  - Don't combine more than three clause types in a single sentence
  - Avoid ambiguous sentences with multiple valid grammatical interpretations
prerequisites:
  - grade4-parts-of-speech
  - grade4-sentence-structure
  - grade3-grammar-structures
version: 1
version_notes: Initial creation based on KMK Bildungsstandards Deutsch Sekundarstufe I (2022)
