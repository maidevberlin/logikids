---
id: grade11-sturm-und-drang
name: Sturm und Drang
description: Understanding the Sturm und Drang movement's rebellion against rationalism through the Genie concept, emotion, and nature
grade: 11
focus: Literary movement analysis and emotional expression
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Understand Sturm und Drang as a rebellion against Enlightenment rationalism
  - Analyze the Genie concept and its expression in literature
  - Identify and interpret typical themes including nature, emotion, and individual rebellion
  - Analyze innovative language and stylistic features characteristic of the movement
  - Compare Sturm und Drang with both Enlightenment and Weimarer Klassik
  - Trace the influence of Sturm und Drang on later literary movements
  - Interpret key works by major Sturm und Drang authors
problem_types:
  - Thematic analysis
  - Movement comparison
  - Language innovation study
  - Character motivation analysis
  - Genie concept interpretation
  - Nature symbolism analysis
  - Emotional expression analysis
  - Historical context integration
  - Stylistic feature identification
  - Author intention evaluation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify basic themes like emotion, nature, and rebellion in excerpts
    - Recognize obvious differences between Enlightenment and Sturm und Drang texts
    - Define the Genie concept in simple terms
  medium:
    - Analyze how language and style create emotional intensity
    - Compare specific works from Enlightenment and Sturm und Drang periods
    - Interpret the social and generational rebellion in major works
  hard:
    - Evaluate how Sturm und Drang challenged Enlightenment ideals philosophically
    - Trace the evolution from Sturm und Drang to Klassik in individual authors
    - Develop original interpretations of how the Genie concept manifests in texts
real_world_context:
  - Students analyze Goethe's Werther to understand how the novel sparked a cultural phenomenon
  - A theater company performs Schiller's Die Räuber with emphasis on youthful rebellion
  - Comparing the restrained language of Lessing with the passionate outbursts in Sturm und Drang poetry
  - Analyzing how young Goethe rejected his father's rational worldview through literature
  - A documentary explores the generational conflict between Enlightenment thinkers and Sturm und Drang writers
  - Students trace how nature imagery in Goethe's early poetry expresses freedom and emotion
  - Examining the Genie figure in different Sturm und Drang works as a challenge to social norms
  - A museum exhibit displays manuscripts showing Goethe's stylistic evolution from Sturm und Drang to Klassik
  - Analyzing how Klinger's play gave the movement its name and captured its spirit
  - Students debate whether modern youth movements share characteristics with Sturm und Drang
  - Comparing the rational hero of Enlightenment drama with the passionate protagonist of Die Räuber
  - A literature seminar explores how Herder's folk song collections influenced Sturm und Drang aesthetics
  - Analyzing the tension between individual genius and social expectations in Sturm und Drang texts
  - Students examine how the movement's emphasis on feeling influenced Romantic literature
  - Tracing biographical elements in young Schiller's and Goethe's early rebellious works
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid treating Sturm und Drang as purely biographical without literary analysis
  - Do not oversimplify the movement as just "emotion versus reason"
  - Avoid anachronistic interpretations using modern psychological concepts
  - Do not ignore the historical context of absolutism and social constraints
  - Avoid conflating Sturm und Drang with Romanticism (distinct movements)
prerequisites:
  - grade11-enlightenment-literature
