name: Creative Writing
description: Producing creative texts by adapting literary forms
grade: 5
focus: Literary text variation, imitation, and creative adaptation
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Write original fables (Fabeln) with animals as characters representing human traits and a clear moral lesson
  - Adapt or continue fairy tales (MÃ¤rchen) by applying genre conventions like magic elements, good vs. evil, and typical formulas
  - Create simple poems using basic poetic elements like rhyme, rhythm, and imagery
  - Write formal and informal letters with appropriate structure and tone
  - Apply perspective changes to retell familiar stories from different character viewpoints
  - Use creative methods to vary and imitate literary texts
  - Apply learned genre conventions when creating original texts
problem_types:
  - Original fable writing
  - Fairy tale adaptation
  - Fairy tale continuation
  - Simple poem creation
  - Letter writing
  - Perspective retelling
  - Genre imitation
  - Story variation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Simple fable with one clear moral and obvious animal characteristics
    - Fairy tale continuation with provided starting point and clear instructions
    - Letter with structured template and familiar context
  medium:
    - Original fable requiring moral lesson integration and character symbolism
    - Fairy tale adaptation changing key elements while maintaining genre conventions
    - Poem using specific rhyme scheme and imagery requirements
  hard:
    - Complex perspective change requiring deep character understanding
    - Multiple genre conventions applied simultaneously in creative text
    - Sophisticated variation of literary text with intentional stylistic changes
real_world_context:
  - Writing fables to teach younger children moral lessons through entertaining animal stories
  - Creating adapted fairy tales for school performances, class books, or creative projects
  - Composing poems for greeting cards, special occasions, or school poetry contests
  - Writing letters to pen pals, relatives, or as characters from stories to explore perspectives
  - Retelling familiar stories from villain or secondary character viewpoints for creative discussions
  - Inventing fantasy adventure stories featuring heroes, magical creatures, and enchanted forests
  - Writing continuation stories for cliffhanger endings in favorite book series
  - Creating fables about environmental themes like teamwork in ant colonies or forest conservation
  - Composing humorous poems about school life, pets, or family situations for class magazines
  - Writing diary entries from historical figures' perspectives after learning about different time periods
  - Adapting classic tales to modern settings like cities, space stations, or underwater kingdoms
  - Creating illustrated story books as gifts for younger siblings or charity book drives
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid obscure fable morals or complex philosophical lessons
  - Do not ask for advanced poetic forms beyond simple rhyme and rhythm
  - Avoid fairy tale adaptations with culturally unfamiliar references
  - Do not expect complex metaphorical thinking beyond concrete imagery
  - Avoid prompts mixing multiple literary forms in confusing ways
