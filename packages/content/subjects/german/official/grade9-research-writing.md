---
id: grade9-research-writing
name: Research Writing
description: Integrating sources, proper citation, and synthesizing research into coherent arguments
grade: 9
focus: Academic research and source integration
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Develop focused research questions and clear thesis statements
  - Locate and evaluate academic sources for credibility and relevance
  - Integrate quotations and paraphrases effectively into written text
  - Apply citation conventions consistently and accurately
  - Synthesize information from multiple sources into coherent arguments
problem_types:
  - Source evaluation
  - Citation formatting
  - Quotation integration
  - Paraphrasing practice
  - Research question development
  - Thesis formulation
  - Source synthesis
  - Bibliography creation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Use 2-3 clearly reliable sources
    - Apply basic citation format for simple sources
    - Distinguish between direct quotes and paraphrases
  medium:
    - Evaluate 4-5 sources for credibility and bias
    - Cite diverse source types correctly (books, articles, websites)
    - Integrate multiple sources to support a single point
  hard:
    - Assess conflicting information from 6+ sources
    - Apply advanced citation rules for complex sources
    - Synthesize diverse perspectives into original arguments
real_world_context:
  - Writing a research report on renewable energy for science class
  - Investigating local history by examining historical documents and interviews
  - Creating a presentation on social media's impact using academic studies
  - Researching animal migration patterns for a biology project
  - Preparing a debate argument using statistical data and expert opinions
  - Writing about a historical figure using biographies and primary sources
  - Investigating climate change effects in your region with scientific articles
  - Researching teenage health trends using medical journals and surveys
  - Creating a documentary script about a cultural tradition using multiple sources
  - Writing about technological innovations by comparing expert analyses
  - Investigating urban planning issues using city reports and interviews
  - Researching the history of music genres using recordings and critiques
  - Exploring space exploration milestones through NASA documents and news archives
  - Writing about endangered species using conservation organization reports
  - Investigating sports history through newspaper archives and athlete memoirs
anti_patterns:
  - Don't accept sources without checking author credentials and publication date
  - Avoid copying sentences without quotation marks even if cited
  - Don't mix different citation styles within the same paper
  - Avoid relying on a single source for complex claims
  - Don't include sources in bibliography that weren't actually cited in text
