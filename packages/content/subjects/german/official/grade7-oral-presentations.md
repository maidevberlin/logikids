---
id: grade7-oral-presentations
name: Structured Oral Presentations
description: Delivering presentations with visualization aids, rhetorical devices, and audience engagement techniques
grade: 7
focus: Advanced presentation skills with rhetorical techniques
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Deliver structured, audience-appropriate speeches with clear introduction, body, and conclusion
  - Present work results through reports with effective visualization aids
  - Employ basic rhetorical devices with vocal and non-verbal support
  - Listen attentively to presentations and formulate content-based questions
  - Adapt presentation style and language register to different audiences
  - Respond to audience questions with relevant, well-structured answers
  - Use pauses, emphasis, and pacing strategically to enhance message delivery
problem_types:
  - Rhetorical device application
  - Audience analysis
  - Visual aid effectiveness
  - Question response strategy
  - Presentation structure critique
  - Vocal technique integration
  - Persuasive element identification
  - Transition crafting
  - Opening hook design
  - Closing impact strategy
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - 5-7 minute presentations on familiar topics with simple rhetorical devices
    - Basic visualization aids like slides or posters with clear information
    - Respond to straightforward audience questions with prepared material
  medium:
    - 7-10 minute presentations requiring synthesis of multiple sources
    - Integrated rhetorical devices (repetition, rhetorical questions, triadic structures)
    - Handle unexpected questions with relevant examples and explanations
  hard:
    - 10-15 minute presentations on complex topics requiring critical analysis
    - Sophisticated visualization combining data, images, and structural elements
    - Navigate challenging questions and integrate impromptu supporting evidence
prerequisites:
  - grade6-oral-presentation
  - grade5-oral-presentations
real_world_context:
  - Presenting research findings on environmental topics to classmates with data visualizations and graphs
  - Delivering persuasive speeches about school policy changes to student government representatives
  - Explaining scientific experiment results using diagrams, charts, and demonstration videos
  - Presenting literary analysis of novels with quotations, character maps, and thematic connections
  - Delivering historical event presentations with timeline visuals, maps, and primary source excerpts
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid topics requiring university-level analysis or professional expertise
  - Don't expect presentations longer than 15 minutes for 12-13 year olds
  - Avoid advanced rhetorical devices like chiasmus or synecdoche
  - Don't require perfect memorization - notes and prompts are appropriate
  - Avoid high-pressure scenarios like formal conferences or adult audiences
version: 1
version_notes: Initial creation for Grade 7 German curriculum
