# Giving Presentations

Create a task where {{name}} practices presentation skills appropriate for {{age}}-year-olds at {{difficulty}} level in {{language}}.

## Variation 1: Show and Tell Presentation
Guide the student to prepare a short presentation about a special object:
- Choose an object (toy, book, souvenir, collection item)
- Prepare what to say:
  - *What* is it?
  - *Where* did it come from?
  - *Why* is it special?
  - Interesting facts or stories about it
- Practice speaking clearly
- Show the object while talking

## Variation 2: My Hobby Presentation
Have the student present a hobby or favorite activity:

**Presentation structure:**
1. **Introduction**: "My hobby is..."
2. **When started**: "I started... ago"
3. **What I do**: Describe the activity
4. **Why I like it**: Personal reasons
5. **Materials needed**: Show or list items
6. **Invitation**: "Would you like to try it?"

Use pictures, drawings, or objects as visual support.

## Variation 3: Animal Expert Presentation
Student researches and presents about a favorite animal:
- Prepare 5-7 facts about the animal
- Bring pictures, drawings, or a stuffed animal
- Talk about:
  - What it looks like
  - Where it lives
  - What it eats
  - Interesting behaviors
  - Why you chose this animal

## Variation 4: Book Recommendation Presentation
Present a favorite book to classmates:
- Show the book cover
- Tell what the book is about (no spoilers!)
- Show favorite illustration
- Read a short, exciting passage
- Explain why others should read it
- Answer questions from audience

## Variation 5: Family Tradition Presentation
Share about a family tradition or celebration:
- Describe the tradition
- When does it happen?
- Who participates?
- What do you do?
- Why is it special to your family?
- Bring photos or objects related to the tradition

## Variation 6: How-To Presentation
Teach the class how to do something simple:
- Choose a skill (making a sandwich, tying shoes, simple craft)
- Break it into steps:
  1. *First...*
  2. *Then...*
  3. *Next...*
  4. *Finally...*
- Demonstrate while explaining
- Let audience try if possible

## Variation 7: Picture Poster Presentation
Create a poster with pictures and present it:
- Choose a topic (favorite season, dream vacation, best day ever)
- Arrange 4-6 pictures on a poster
- For each picture, explain:
  - What is shown
  - Why you chose it
  - What it means to you
- Point to pictures while talking

## Variation 8: Expert Interview Presentation
Present information learned from interviewing someone:
- Interview a family member about their job, hobby, or childhood
- Prepare to share:
  - Who you interviewed
  - What questions you asked
  - Most interesting answer
  - Something surprising you learned
- Bring a photo of the person if possible

**Presentation tips for all variations:**
- Speak loud enough for everyone to hear
- Look at your audience
- Hold supporting materials so everyone can see
- Take your time
- Be ready to answer questions
