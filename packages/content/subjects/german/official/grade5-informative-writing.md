---
id: grade5-informative-writing
name: Informative Writing
description: Writing factual texts with logical organization
grade: 5
focus: Informative text composition with objective tone and clear structure
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Write reports (Bericht) using W-Fragen structure to convey factual information
  - Create systematic descriptions (Beschreibung) of objects, persons, and processes
  - Organize information with logical progression and appropriate connectors
  - Maintain objective tone appropriate for informational writing
  - Distinguish informative text type from narrative and argumentative forms
  - Structure texts with clear introduction, body, and conclusion
  - Use appropriate temporal and logical connectors (dann, danach, schließlich, außerdem)
problem_types:
  - Event report
  - Person description
  - Object description
  - Process instruction
  - Procedure explanation
  - Animal or plant description
  - Place description
  - Sequential documentation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Simple W-Fragen structure (Wer? Was? Wann? Wo?)
    - Single-topic descriptions with 3-5 main points
    - Basic connectors (und, dann, danach)
  medium:
    - Complete W-Fragen coverage including Wie? and Warum?
    - Multi-aspect descriptions with logical grouping
    - Varied connectors for sequence and addition (außerdem, schließlich, zunächst)
  hard:
    - Complex factual accounts requiring source integration
    - Detailed systematic descriptions with hierarchical organization
    - Sophisticated connector usage including causal relationships
real_world_context:
  - Writing accident or event reports for school documentation or insurance purposes
  - Creating step-by-step instructions for games, crafts, or recipes to share with classmates
  - Describing animals or natural phenomena for science projects and presentations
  - Writing person descriptions for creative writing or lost-and-found notices
  - Documenting procedures like experiments, field trips, or class activities for portfolios
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid mixing narrative storytelling elements into factual reports
  - Do not use first-person perspective or subjective opinions
  - Avoid vague time references instead of precise temporal information
  - Do not require specialized vocabulary beyond Grade 5 comprehension
  - Avoid topics requiring expert knowledge or technical terminology
prerequisites:
  - grade4-text-planning
version: 1
version_notes: Initial concept for German Grade 5 informative writing aligned with Berlin-Brandenburg Rahmenlehrplan
