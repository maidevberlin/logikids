id: grade13-materialgestuetztes-schreiben
name: Material-Based Writing (Abitur)
description: Synthesizing diverse sources for argumentative texts at Abitur level - integrating multiple perspectives while developing original positions
grade: 13
focus: Advanced source synthesis and argumentation
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Synthesize diverse source materials into coherent argumentative or informative texts
  - Develop original positions while integrating multiple source perspectives
  - Evaluate source credibility and recognize bias across materials
  - Maintain genre-specific conventions for different text types (speech, commentary, article)
  - Work efficiently with materials under exam time constraints
  - Balance source integration with independent argumentation
  - Adapt register and style to match audience and communicative purpose
problem_types:
  - Argumentative synthesis
  - Informative synthesis
  - Source evaluation
  - Position development
  - Perspective integration
  - Genre adaptation
  - Rhetorical strategy
  - Counter-argument handling
  - Evidence selection
  - Audience targeting
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Synthesize 3-4 aligned sources into informative text
    - Write 600-800 words with clear structure
    - Basic genre conventions for familiar text types
  medium:
    - Integrate 5-6 sources with mixed perspectives into argumentative text
    - Produce 800-1000 words balancing synthesis and argumentation
    - Sophisticated genre adaptation with rhetorical awareness
  hard:
    - Synthesize 7+ sources with conflicting positions into nuanced argument
    - Write 1000-1200 words demonstrating critical evaluation
    - Advanced rhetorical strategies and counter-argument integration
real_world_context:
  - Writing a newspaper commentary on artificial intelligence regulation using tech industry statements, ethical guidelines, and legal analyses
  - Composing a speech for a public forum on climate policy based on scientific reports, economic studies, and activist perspectives
  - Creating an article about education reform for a magazine using curriculum documents, teacher unions' positions, and learning research
  - Developing a position paper on social media responsibility from platform policies, psychological studies, and regulatory proposals
  - Writing a commentary on sustainable consumption using environmental data, consumer behavior research, and industry responses
  - Composing a speech about democratic participation for youth parliament using political theory, voter statistics, and civic initiatives
  - Creating an essay on work-life balance for a professional journal from labor studies, employer surveys, and health research
  - Writing an op-ed about cultural diversity in public institutions using sociological research, policy documents, and community voices
  - Developing a speech on digital privacy rights from legal frameworks, tech company practices, and civil liberties advocacy
  - Composing an article about mental health awareness using medical research, personal testimonials, and healthcare policy
  - Writing a commentary on urban development from city planning studies, resident feedback, and environmental assessments
  - Creating a speech about migration policy for a debate using demographic data, integration studies, and humanitarian perspectives
  - Developing an article on gender equality in STEM fields from statistical reports, industry initiatives, and academic research
  - Writing a position paper on renewable energy transition using technological assessments, economic analyses, and environmental impact studies
  - Composing a commentary on media literacy education from communication research, curriculum proposals, and digital platform analyses
anti_patterns:
  - Don't require specialized knowledge beyond provided sources
  - Avoid sources so contradictory that no coherent position is possible
  - Don't demand specific citation formats beyond naming source/author
  - Avoid genre requirements unfamiliar at this curriculum level
  - Don't create tasks solvable by summarizing sources without synthesis

Unlike Grade 10 material-based writing which focuses on objective synthesis, this concept emphasizes critical evaluation and argumentative positioning.

Students must work efficiently with diverse materials, identify key arguments and evidence, evaluate source credibility, and integrate perspectives into genre-appropriate texts. The focus is on creating original arguments informed by sources, not merely compiling information. {{#if (eq difficulty "hard")}}Tasks should require students to navigate contradictory sources and develop nuanced positions that acknowledge complexity.{{/if}}
