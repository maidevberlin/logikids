---
id: grade7-indirect-speech
name: Indirect Speech and Paraphrasing
description: Converting direct speech to indirect speech, paraphrasing sources accurately, and properly marking borrowed content
grade: 7
focus: Reporting speech and integrating source material
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Convert direct speech to indirect speech using appropriate pronoun and tense shifts
  - Paraphrase source material without copying the original wording
  - Clearly mark and attribute borrowed content from texts
  - Summarize literary content and plot developments accurately in indirect form
  - Recognize when indirect speech is appropriate versus direct quotation
  - Apply proper citation markers when incorporating material from sources
  - Integrate quoted and paraphrased material smoothly into written compositions
problem_types:
  - Direct to indirect conversion
  - Paraphrasing practice
  - Attribution identification
  - Pronoun shift application
  - Tense adjustment
  - Source citation
  - Quote integration
  - Summary writing
  - Borrowed content marking
  - Plagiarism detection
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Simple direct speech with clear reporting verbs (er sagte, sie meinte)
    - Single-sentence conversions with straightforward pronoun changes
    - Identifying clearly marked quotations versus unmarked borrowed content
  medium:
    - Multiple sentences requiring consistent pronoun and tense shifts
    - Paraphrasing complex sentences while preserving original meaning
    - Selecting appropriate reporting verbs for different contexts (behaupten, erkl√§ren, fragen)
  hard:
    - Converting dialogue with multiple speakers requiring careful attribution
    - Paraphrasing technical or abstract content without losing nuance
    - Integrating multiple sources with proper attribution in a single paragraph
real_world_context:
  - Writing research reports for school that incorporate information from books and websites with proper attribution
  - Retelling conversations to parents or friends using indirect speech to relay what someone said
  - Reading news articles that report statements from interviews using indirect speech
  - Creating book reports that summarize plot events without copying the author's exact words
  - Writing essays that reference multiple sources while avoiding plagiarism
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid mixing direct and indirect speech without clear transitions
  - Don't introduce subjunctive mood forms not yet covered in Grade 7
  - Avoid overly complex attribution chains (more than two reporting levels)
  - Don't use quotation scenarios unfamiliar to 12-13 year olds
  - Avoid technical citation formats (APA, MLA) beyond simple attribution

  - grade7-subjunctive-mood
  - grade6-grammar-syntax
  - grade5-sentence-elements
