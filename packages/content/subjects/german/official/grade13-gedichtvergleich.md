name: Poetry Comparison (Gedichtvergleich)
description: Comparative analysis of poems - Abitur format with balanced treatment
grade: 13
focus: Systematic comparative poetry analysis for Abitur examination
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Compare poems systematically using meaningful criteria
  - Identify and analyze similarities and differences in form, content, and style
  - Balance treatment of both poems equally throughout the analysis
  - Use comparison to deepen interpretation of individual poems
  - Write comparative analyses under exam time constraints
  - Develop comparative thesis statements that illuminate both texts
  - Integrate knowledge of epochs, motifs, and poetic techniques
problem_types:
  - Thematic comparison
  - Formal analysis
  - Epoch-based comparison
  - Motif comparison
  - Symbolic interpretation
  - Tone and atmosphere
  - Structural comparison
  - Language and imagery
  - Historical context
  - Speaker perspective
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Poems with clear thematic parallels or contrasts
    - Similar forms or obvious structural differences
    - Straightforward comparative criteria (theme, form, epoch)
  medium:
    - Poems requiring nuanced comparative analysis
    - Multiple valid comparison angles requiring selection
    - Integration of form-content relationship across both poems
  hard:
    - Subtle connections requiring sophisticated interpretation
    - Complex synthesis of multiple comparison criteria
    - Original comparative thesis with layered argumentation

anti_patterns:
  - Avoid treating one poem as primary and the other as secondary
  - Don't list observations without connecting them comparatively
  - Avoid comparing only form or only content without integrating both
  - Don't require memorized biographical details over textual analysis
  - Avoid poems so obscure that comparison requires extensive external knowledge
