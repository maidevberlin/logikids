---
id: grade2-retelling-stories
name: Retelling Stories
description: Listen to or read simple stories and retell them in correct sequence, maintaining key story elements
grade: 2
focus: Narrative comprehension and oral retelling skills
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Retell a story in the correct chronological sequence
  - Identify and include main story elements (characters, setting, events)
  - Use temporal connectors (zuerst, dann, danach, zuletzt) to structure narration
  - Distinguish important plot points from minor details
  - Maintain beginning, middle, and end structure when retelling
  - Recall and retell stories after listening or reading independently
problem_types:
  - Story sequencing
  - Event ordering
  - Character identification
  - Missing story part
  - Beginning-middle-end structure
  - Timeline reconstruction
  - Key detail selection
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - 3-4 event stories with clear linear progression
    - Familiar everyday scenarios (morning routine, playing, going to school)
    - Simple temporal sequence with obvious order
  medium:
    - 5-6 event stories with minor subplots
    - Stories with cause-and-effect relationships between events
    - Mix of explicit and slightly implicit event sequences
  hard:
    - 7-8 event stories with parallel storylines
    - Stories requiring inference about event order from context
    - Multiple characters with overlapping actions
prerequisites:
  - grade1-listening-comprehension
  - grade1-reading-short-sentences
real_world_context:
  - Telling parents what happened at school during the day
  - Explaining the plot of a favorite book or movie to a friend
  - Retelling a story heard during story time in the library
  - Describing the sequence of events in a game played at recess
  - Sharing what happened during a family outing or trip
  - A young reporter retelling news events for the class newspaper in order
  - Describing a birthday party celebration from arrival to going home
  - A knight retelling a quest adventure to the king using first, then, finally
  - Explaining how a caterpillar becomes a butterfly in the correct sequence
  - Retelling a fairy tale to younger siblings using story structure
  - A detective recounting clues discovered in chronological order to solve a case
  - Describing a soccer game from kickoff to final whistle with main events
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid stories with more than 8 distinct events at this level
  - Don't use flashbacks or non-chronological story structures
  - Avoid abstract themes beyond 7-8 year old understanding
  - Don't include multiple similar characters that cause confusion
  - Avoid stories requiring cultural knowledge beyond typical Grade 2 experience
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
