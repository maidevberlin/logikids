**Create a task where {{student_name}} learns about newspapers and journalistic writing.**

Your task should:
- Focus on one of these variations:
  1. **Newspaper Structure**: Identify and explain different sections of a newspaper (politics, sports, culture, local news, etc.)
  2. **Text Type Distinction**: Compare and contrast **Nachricht** (news brief), **Bericht** (report), and **Kommentar** (commentary)
  3. **News Writing**: Write a news article using the inverted pyramid structure (most important information first)
  4. **W-Questions**: Practice answering all W-questions (Wer? Was? Wann? Wo? Warum? Wie?) in a news article
  5. **Headlines**: Analyze effective headlines and write your own attention-grabbing headlines
  6. **Objectivity vs. Opinion**: Identify objective facts vs. subjective opinions in articles
  7. **Journalistic Principles**: Understand principles like accuracy, fairness, objectivity, and source citation
  8. **Interview Technique**: Plan and write interview questions for a news story

- Use age-appropriate news topics (school events, local community, sports, youth interests, environment)
- Provide example articles (150-250 words)
- Show clear differences between factual reporting and opinion pieces
- Use tables to compare text types or organize information
- Include elements like headlines, lead paragraphs, and photo captions

**Important**:
- Ensure diverse news topics each time (school, sports, technology, environment, community)
- Vary the journalistic focus (writing, analyzing, comparing, evaluating)
- Change the specific events and situations
- Provide fresh, engaging news contexts
