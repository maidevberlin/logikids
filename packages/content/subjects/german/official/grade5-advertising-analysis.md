# Advertising Analysis

Create {{count}} varied tasks about **analyzing advertising and persuasive techniques** for a {{age}}-year-old student. Focus on {{focus_area}}.

## Task Variations

### Variation 1: Target Group Identification
Students analyze advertisements and identify the intended target group.

**Example:**
"Look at these three advertising slogans:
1. *'Mehr Power für echte Gamer!'* (More power for real gamers!)
2. *'Für die schönsten Momente mit Ihrer Familie'* (For the most beautiful moments with your family)
3. *'Damit du in der Schule glänzt!'* (So you shine at school!)

**Tasks:**
- Who is the target group for each slogan?
- Which words give you clues about the target audience?
- How would you change slogan 3 to target parents instead of children?"

### Variation 2: Persuasive Language Analysis
Students identify and categorize persuasive words and phrases.

**Example:**
"Find the persuasive words in this advertisement text:
*'Das **neue** SuperPhone ist **endlich** da! **Nur** diese Woche zum **unglaublichen** Preis! **Alle** deine Freunde haben es **schon**. Sei **nicht** der Letzte! **Jetzt** bestellen und ein **kostenloses** Geschenk erhalten!'*

**Categorize the persuasive techniques:**
- Time pressure words: _______
- Peer pressure words: _______
- Superlatives/exaggerations: _______
- Free bonus tricks: _______"

### Variation 3: Fact vs. Opinion
Students distinguish between factual information and persuasive opinions in ads.

**Example:**
"Read this snack advertisement. Mark each statement as **Fact (F)** or **Opinion (O)**:
1. ___ 'Dieser Riegel enthält 30% weniger Zucker als andere Marken.'
2. ___ 'Der leckerste Snack für zwischendurch!'
3. ___ 'Hergestellt mit echten Nüssen.'
4. ___ 'Perfekt für aktive Kids!'
5. ___ 'In drei Geschmacksrichtungen erhältlich.'

*Which statements could you verify? Which are just marketing claims?*"

### Variation 4: Visual Tricks
Students analyze visual elements and their persuasive effects.

**Example:**
"A toy advertisement shows:
- Bright, neon colors
- The toy looking much larger than a child's hand
- Children smiling and jumping
- The word '**NEU**' in a star burst
- Small text at the bottom: '*Batteries not included. Some assembly required.*'

**Analyze:**
1. How do the colors affect your feelings?
2. Why is the toy shown so large?
3. What emotion do the smiling children create?
4. Why is the warning text so small?
5. What might customers be disappointed about after buying?"

### Variation 5: Comparing Advertisements
Students compare two ads for similar products.

**Example:**
"Compare these two cereal advertisements:

**Ad A:** *'ChocoCrunch - So lecker, dass du nicht aufhören kannst! Mit extra viel Schokolade!'*
**Ad B:** *'HealthyStart - Vollkorngetreide mit Vitaminen. Enthält 50% des Tagesbedarfs an Vitamin D.'*

**Questions:**
1. Which ad uses emotional appeal? Which uses facts?
2. Which target group does each ad address?
3. Which ad focuses on taste? Which on health?
4. Which ad would convince a parent? Which would convince a child?
5. Can you rewrite Ad A to include more facts?"

### Variation 6: Celebrity Endorsement
Students analyze the use of celebrities or experts in advertising.

**Example:**
"Three toothpaste advertisements:
1. *'Von Zahnärzten empfohlen!'* (Recommended by dentists!)
2. *'Star-Fußballer Max Müller nutzt es täglich!'* (Star footballer Max Müller uses it daily!)
3. *'9 von 10 Kindern bevorzugen den Geschmack!'* (9 out of 10 children prefer the taste!)

**Analyze:**
- Which statement uses **expert opinion**?
- Which uses **celebrity endorsement**?
- Which uses **statistics**?
- Which is most trustworthy? Why?
- Could statement 3 be misleading? How?"

### Variation 7: Hidden Messages
Students identify implicit messages and assumptions in advertising.

**Example:**
"What hidden messages are in these slogans?
1. *'Für Siegertypen!'* (For winner types!) → Hidden message: If you don't buy this, you're a _______?
2. *'Weil du es verdienst!'* (Because you deserve it!) → Hidden message: You should reward yourself by _______?
3. *'Nicht für Langweiler!'* (Not for boring people!) → Hidden message: If you don't buy this, you're _______?

*How do these slogans make you feel? What emotions do they target?*"

### Variation 8: Creating Counter-Advertising
Students create their own critical response to manipulative advertising.

**Example:**
"An ad says: *'Das coolste Spiel des Jahres! Deine Freunde spielen es alle! Nur 59,99€ – ein Schnäppchen!'*

**Your task:**
Create a **warning label** for this ad that reveals the persuasive tricks:
- Point out the peer pressure
- Question the price claim
- Mention what information is missing
- Suggest questions buyers should ask

*Example start: 'Achtung! Diese Werbung benutzt...'*"
