---
id: grade5-informational-texts
name: Informational Text Comprehension
description: Comprehending non-fiction texts and non-linear information formats
grade: 5
focus: Non-fiction comprehension and information extraction
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Comprehend age-appropriate informational texts from children's magazines and encyclopedias
  - Interpret simple non-linear texts including bar charts and tables
  - Distinguish facts from opinions in informational texts
  - Identify main ideas and supporting details in non-fiction
  - Gather and organize information from multiple sources for writing
  - Extract specific information from clearly structured reference materials
  - Recognize text structure and organizational patterns in informational writing
problem_types:
  - Main idea identification
  - Fact vs opinion distinction
  - Chart and table interpretation
  - Information synthesis
  - Supporting detail recognition
  - Text structure analysis
  - Multi-source comparison
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single-paragraph texts with clear topic sentences
    - Simple bar charts with 3-5 data points and clear labels
    - Explicit facts with obvious opinion markers
  medium:
    - Multi-paragraph texts with headings and subheadings
    - Tables or charts requiring comparison across 2-3 categories
    - Mixed fact-opinion statements requiring analysis
  hard:
    - Complex informational texts with multiple sections and implicit connections
    - Multi-variable charts requiring synthesis of multiple data points
    - Subtle opinion elements embedded in factual reporting
prerequisites:
  - grade4-reading-comprehension
real_world_context:
  - Reading encyclopedia entries to research school projects about animals or countries
  - Understanding weather charts in newspapers to plan outdoor activities
  - Reading children's magazines to learn about science topics or current events
  - Interpreting nutritional tables on food packaging to make healthy choices
  - Gathering information from multiple library books to write reports
  - Reading museum exhibit labels to learn about artifacts, artworks, or natural history specimens
  - Understanding sports statistics charts showing player performance or team rankings
  - Reading instruction manuals for assembling toys, models, or craft projects
  - Interpreting bus or train timetables to plan trips with family or class excursions
  - Reading informational brochures about national parks, zoos, or tourist attractions
  - Understanding graphs in science textbooks showing plant growth or temperature changes
  - Reading fact sheets about endangered animals or environmental conservation efforts
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid texts with vocabulary far beyond grade 5 level
  - Do not use charts with more than 5 categories or complex scales
  - Avoid opinion pieces requiring adult political or social knowledge
  - Do not expect interpretation of statistical concepts beyond simple comparison
  - Avoid texts longer than 300 words for comprehension tasks
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
