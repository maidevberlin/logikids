Generate a task where {{childName}} practices using word derivation and word families to determine correct spelling.

**Focus areas to choose from:**
- Finding related words to check spelling
- Identifying word stems and roots
- Using plural forms to verify spelling
- Recognizing word families
- Deriving spelling from conjugations or declensions

## Task Variations

### Variation 1: Singular-Plural Derivation
Present {{num1}} words in singular form where the spelling is unclear. The student uses the plural form to determine the correct spelling.

Example words:
| Singular (unclear) | Plural Form | Correct Spelling |
|--------------------|-------------|------------------|
| Rad or Rat? | Räder | **Rad** (with 'd') |
| Wald or Walt? | Wälder | **Wald** (with 'd') |
| Tag or Tack? | Tage | **Tag** (with 'g') |
| Hund or Hunt? | Hunde | **Hund** (with 'd') |

### Variation 2: Word Family Discovery
Provide {{num1}} base words. The student must find {{num2}} related words from the same word family.

Example:
- Base word: *spiel* → Related: *spielen, Spieler, Spielzeug, verspielt*
- Base word: *fahr* → Related: *fahren, Fahrer, Fahrzeug, verfahren*
- Base word: *les* → Related: *lesen, Leser, lesbar, vorlesen*

### Variation 3: Spelling Detective
Show {{num1}} words with uncertain spelling. The student must find a related word that makes the correct spelling clear.

Examples:
- *Lan_* (Land or Lant?) → Related word: *Länder* → Answer: **Land**
- *Ber_* (Berg or Berk?) → Related word: *Berge* → Answer: **Berg**
- *Kin_* (Kind or Kint?) → Related word: *Kinder* → Answer: **Kind**

### Variation 4: Verb Conjugation Check
Present {{num1}} verb stems where a letter might be unclear. Use conjugation to verify the correct spelling.

Example:
| Uncertain Spelling | Conjugated Form | Correct Stem |
|--------------------|-----------------|--------------|
| leb or lep? | **leben** (we live) | **leb** |
| spiel or spil? | **spielen** | **spiel** |
| schwimm or schwim? | **schwimmen** | **schwimm** |

### Variation 5: Root Word Identification
Show {{num1}} longer words. The student must identify the root/stem word.

Examples:
- *Spielzeug* → Root: **spiel**
- *Lesezeit* → Root: **lese** (from lesen)
- *Fahrrad* → Root: **fahr** (from fahren)
- *Baustein* → Root: **bau** (from bauen)

### Variation 6: Adjective-Adverb Derivation
Present {{num1}} adjectives. The student derives related forms to understand the spelling.

Example:
| Adjective | Comparative | Confirms Spelling |
|-----------|-------------|-------------------|
| *groß* | größer | ß stays |
| *kalt* | kälter | 'alt' stem |
| *jung* | jünger | 'g' confirmed |

### Variation 7: Compound Word Analysis
Show {{num1}} compound words. The student breaks them into parts to understand and verify spelling.

Examples:
- *Hausaufgaben* = Haus + Aufgaben
  - Check: *Häuser* confirms 's' in Haus
- *Wochenende* = Woche + Ende
  - Check: *Wochen* confirms spelling
- *Regenschirm* = Regen + Schirm
  - Check: Both parts verified separately

### Variation 8: Word Family Sorting
Provide {{num1}} groups of words. The student sorts them into word families based on shared roots.

Example word list:
*fahren, Fahrer, lesen, Leser, Fahrzeug, Lesebuch, verfahren, vorlesen*

Sorted families:
- **fahr**: fahren, Fahrer, Fahrzeug, verfahren
- **les**: lesen, Leser, Lesebuch, vorlesen
