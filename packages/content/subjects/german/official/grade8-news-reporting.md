---
id: grade8-news-reporting
name: News and Reporting
description: Distinguishing news from opinion, analyzing journalistic text types
grade: 8
focus: Critical analysis of journalistic texts and news literacy
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Distinguish between news articles, reportage, and commentary
  - Identify bias and perspective in journalistic texts
  - Understand and apply the W-questions structure in news analysis
  - Analyze the function and impact of headlines and lead paragraphs
  - Evaluate source credibility and journalistic quality
  - Recognize different journalistic genres and their conventions
  - Analyze how language choices reveal stance and objectivity
problem_types:
  - Genre identification
  - Bias detection
  - W-questions analysis
  - Headline-text comparison
  - Source evaluation
  - Fact-opinion separation
  - Lead paragraph analysis
  - Journalistic language analysis
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify basic journalistic genres from clear text examples
    - Find explicit W-questions answers in straightforward news articles
    - Distinguish obvious facts from clearly marked opinion statements
  medium:
    - Detect subtle bias through language choices and framing
    - Compare headlines with article content for emphasis or distortion
    - Evaluate source credibility using multiple journalistic quality criteria
  hard:
    - Analyze how selection and omission of information reveals perspective
    - Compare coverage of the same event across multiple outlets to identify framing
    - Evaluate complex multi-source articles for balance and journalistic integrity
prerequisites:
  - grade7-media-literacy
  - grade6-informational-texts
real_world_context:
  - Reading local newspaper coverage of school events to identify reporting perspective
  - Comparing how different news outlets cover the same political announcement
  - Analyzing sports reporting to distinguish game summaries from commentary columns
  - Evaluating environmental news articles for balance between different stakeholder views
  - Examining celebrity news to identify gossip versus factual reporting
  - Comparing international news coverage of major events across different countries' media
  - Analyzing science news articles to check if sources are properly cited and expert
  - Reading restaurant reviews to distinguish factual description from personal opinion
  - Comparing online news articles with traditional newspaper reporting styles
  - Evaluating breaking news coverage to see how initial reports change as facts emerge
  - Analyzing disaster or crisis reporting for sensationalism versus responsible journalism
  - Examining product launch coverage to identify promotional content versus critical analysis
  - Reading historical news archives to understand how reporting styles have evolved
  - Comparing citizen journalism on social media with professional news reporting
  - Analyzing election coverage to identify balanced reporting versus partisan commentary
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid requiring knowledge of specific current events or political figures
  - Don't assume access to specific news outlets or subscription services
  - Avoid politically divisive topics requiring mature political judgment
  - Don't conflate all online content with journalism (distinguish news from blogs)
  - Avoid technical journalism jargon beyond grade-appropriate terminology
version: 1
version_notes: Initial creation for Grade 8 German curriculum
