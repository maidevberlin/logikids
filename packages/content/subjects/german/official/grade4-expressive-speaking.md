---
id: grade4-expressive-speaking
name: Expressive Speaking and Presentation
description: Adapting speech to context (narrating, informing, arguing), using standard German, presentation skills
grade: 4
focus: Context-adaptive oral communication and expressive speech
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Use standard German (Hochdeutsch) with clear articulation and pronunciation
  - Adapt speaking style to different contexts (narrating, informing, arguing, and appealing)
  - Plan and structure remarks and conversations before speaking
  - Understand how different speaking styles create different effects on listeners
  - Present information clearly and engagingly to an audience
  - Use appropriate volume, pace, and intonation for different purposes
  - Apply rhetorical techniques to enhance message effectiveness
problem_types:
  - Narrating experiences
  - Informing audience
  - Presenting arguments
  - Appealing to listeners
  - Style adaptation
  - Speech planning
  - Effect analysis
  - Rhetorical delivery
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Simple 2-3 minute presentations with one clear purpose (narrate OR inform)
    - Basic planning with key points written down
    - Standard German with clear articulation on familiar topics
  medium:
    - 3-5 minute presentations combining purposes (narrate AND inform)
    - Structured planning considering audience and desired effect
    - Conscious style adaptation with varied pace and emphasis
  hard:
    - 5-7 minute presentations with sophisticated purpose (argue OR appeal)
    - Detailed planning including rhetorical strategies and audience analysis
    - Fluent style-switching with intentional use of pauses, emphasis, and gesture
prerequisites:
  - grade3-speaking-presentations
  - grade3-dialogue-roleplay
real_world_context:
  - Presenting a persuasive argument to convince classmates to support a class project
  - Narrating a personal experience with expressive detail to engage listeners emotionally
  - Informing parents about a school event using clear, structured explanations
  - Appealing to the school community to participate in a charity initiative
  - Adapting speech register when speaking to teachers versus peers about the same topic
  - Telling an exciting adventure story to younger siblings using vivid language and varied voice tones
  - Presenting a science experiment to the class with careful explanations of each step and the results
  - Arguing for a family pet by presenting well-organized reasons and addressing potential concerns
  - Explaining game rules to new players using clear, sequential instructions with appropriate pacing
  - Delivering a book report that combines factual information with personal reactions and recommendations
  - Welcoming visiting students to school with a friendly, informative presentation about daily routines and activities
  - Narrating a sports match or game experience with dramatic emphasis on exciting moments and outcomes
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid topics requiring rhetorical skills beyond 9-10 year old capacity
  - Don't expect formal debate-level argumentation for Grade 4
  - Avoid abstract or philosophical topics not relatable to children
  - Don't require dialects students may not speak at home
  - Avoid scenarios with emotionally charged or controversial subject matter
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
