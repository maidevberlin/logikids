name: Media Theory
description: Understanding media criticism theories
grade: 12
focus: Media theory and critical analysis
difficulty: hard
learning_objectives:
  - Understand key concepts from McLuhan's media theory (medium is the message, hot vs. cold media)
  - Analyze the impact of digital transformation on society and communication
  - Evaluate media's role in shaping public discourse and social reality
  - Apply critical media theory to contemporary media ecosystems
  - Compare different approaches to media criticism and theory
  - Analyze the relationship between media technology and social change
  - Critically assess how media structures influence content and reception
problem_types:
  - Theory application
  - Media ecosystem analysis
  - Digital transformation impact
  - McLuhan concept identification
  - Social role evaluation
  - Historical media comparison
  - Technology-society relationship
  - Critical theory synthesis
  - Medium-message analysis
  - Media structure critique
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Identify basic McLuhan concepts in straightforward examples
    - Recognize obvious impacts of digital transformation
    - Describe general social functions of media
  medium:
    - Apply media theory concepts to analyze contemporary phenomena
    - Evaluate complex relationships between media and society
    - Compare theoretical approaches and their implications
  hard:
    - Synthesize multiple theoretical perspectives on media issues
    - Critically assess limitations and strengths of media theories
    - Develop original theoretical insights about emerging media forms
prerequisites:
  - grade11-film-analysis
  - grade8-media-criticism
real_world_context:
  - Analyzing how social media platforms shape political discourse differently than newspapers
  - Applying McLuhan's hot/cold media distinction to TikTok vs. podcasts
  - Examining how streaming services change viewing behavior compared to television
  - Investigating the transformation of journalism from print to digital formats
  - Understanding filter bubbles and algorithmic curation through media theory
  - Analyzing how memes function as a new form of cultural communication
  - Examining the role of influencers in contemporary advertising ecosystems
  - Studying how video games create immersive narrative experiences differently than books
  - Investigating surveillance capitalism and data collection in digital media
  - Analyzing how messaging apps transform interpersonal communication patterns
  - Understanding the decline of traditional gatekeepers in media production
  - Examining how YouTube's recommendation algorithm shapes information consumption
  - Analyzing the transformation of photography from analog to smartphone culture
  - Investigating how virtual reality creates new forms of presence and experience
  - Understanding parasocial relationships between creators and audiences on platforms
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid requiring memorization of theorist names without understanding
  - Don't present media theory as purely negative (acknowledge benefits)
  - Avoid examples requiring technical knowledge of media production
  - Don't use jargon-heavy language without clear conceptual grounding
  - Avoid treating all media effects as deterministic or inevitable
