**Create a task where {{student_name}} creatively interprets and extends literary texts.**

Your task should:
- Focus on one of these variations:
  1. **Internal Monologue**: Write a character's inner thoughts (**innerer Monolog**) at a key moment in the story
  2. **Diary Entry**: Write a diary entry from a character's perspective about story events
  3. **Story Continuation**: Continue a story beyond its ending, maintaining style and character consistency
  4. **Missing Scene**: Write a scene that might have happened between two known events
  5. **Alternative Ending**: Rewrite the ending of a story while staying true to character development
  6. **Letter Writing**: Write a letter from one character to another
  7. **Perspective Shift**: Retell a scene from a different character's internal perspective
  8. **Parallel Scene**: Create a parallel scene showing what another character was doing at the same time

- Provide a story excerpt or summary (150-250 words) as context
- Use age-appropriate stories and situations
- Guide students to stay true to character traits and story logic
- Encourage use of appropriate language and style for the character
- Show examples of internal thoughts vs. external narration

**Important**:
- Ensure diverse story contexts each time (different genres, situations, characters)
- Vary the creative task type (monologue, diary, continuation, alternative)
- Change the specific characters and story moments
- Provide fresh, engaging creative interpretation prompts
