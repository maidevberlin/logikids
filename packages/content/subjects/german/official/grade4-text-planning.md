---
id: grade4-text-planning
name: Planning and Structuring Texts
description: Learn to clarify purpose and audience, gather vocabulary and phrases, and organize ideas systematically before writing
grade: 4
focus: Strategic text planning with audience awareness
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Clarify writing purpose, situation, and target audience before beginning
  - Gather relevant vocabulary, phrases, and text models for writing tasks
  - Organize ideas in logical sequence using planning techniques
  - Apply graphic organizers and outlines to structure complex texts
  - Consider reader's perspective and needs when planning content
  - Select appropriate planning strategies for different text types
  - Evaluate and adjust plans based on assignment requirements
problem_types:
  - Audience analysis
  - Purpose clarification
  - Vocabulary collection
  - Sequential organization
  - Graphic organizer application
  - Model text analysis
  - Planning strategy selection
  - Outline creation
  - Reader perspective-taking
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Simple plans for familiar audiences (classmates, family members)
    - Basic purpose identification (inform, entertain, persuade)
    - Vocabulary lists with 8-12 words organized by category
  medium:
    - Detailed plans adapting content for different audience needs
    - Multi-step outlines with main points and supporting details
    - Integration of text models to identify structural features
  hard:
    - Complex planning for unfamiliar or formal audiences
    - Strategic selection among multiple planning techniques
    - Plans that anticipate and address potential reader questions
prerequisites:
  - grade3-composition-planning
  - grade3-text-analysis
real_world_context:
  - Planning a formal letter to the mayor requesting a new playground, considering what information officials need
  - Creating a detailed outline for a book report, organizing plot summary, character analysis, and personal opinion
  - Gathering technical vocabulary before writing instructions for a science experiment classmates will follow
  - Analyzing model newspaper articles to identify features before writing a class newsletter report
  - Developing a persuasive plan with reasons and evidence to convince parents to allow a class pet
  - Brainstorming ideas and organizing thoughts before writing a fantasy adventure story for creative writing class
  - Planning birthday party invitations by deciding what information guests need and how to present it clearly
  - Creating a structured outline for a presentation about favorite hobbies or personal interests
  - Gathering facts and organizing information before writing an informational report about endangered animals
  - Planning a thank-you letter to grandparents by thinking about what to mention and how to sound grateful
  - Developing a step-by-step plan before writing how-to instructions for teaching others a game or skill
  - Analyzing example restaurant menus or advertisements before designing their own for a class project
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid requiring planning for text lengths exceeding 200-300 words
  - Don't expect audience analysis beyond concrete groups students know
  - Avoid abstract purposes not tied to real communicative situations
  - Don't require more than 4-5 main organizational sections in outlines
  - Avoid planning tasks without clear connection to actual writing goals
version: 2
version_notes: Expanded real_world_context from 5 to 12 items for better variety
