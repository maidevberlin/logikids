## Framework: Five Leitideen (Guiding Principles)

The KMK Bildungsstandards organize mathematics content through five Leitideen:
1. **L1: Zahl** (Number/Arithmetic)
2. **L2: Raum und Form** (Space and Form/Geometry)
3. **L3: Muster, Strukturen und funktionale Zusammenhänge** (Patterns, Structures and Functional Relationships)
4. **L4: Größen und Messen** (Quantities and Measurement)
5. **L5: Daten und Zufall** (Data and Chance/Statistics and Probability)

These Leitideen structure the content below and ensure comprehensive curriculum coverage.

## Concepts by Grade

### Grade 5

#### 1. natural-numbers-operations
- **Title (DE)**: Natürliche Zahlen und Grundrechenarten
- **Title (EN)**: Natural Numbers and Basic Operations
- **Scope**: Introduction to the natural number system, place value understanding up to millions, and mastery of the four basic arithmetic operations (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division) with increasingly large numbers. Includes working with parentheses, commutative and associative laws, and mental arithmetic strategies.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Read, write, compare, and order natural numbers up to one million using place value understanding
  2. Calculate with the four basic operations applying order of operations and arithmetic laws
  3. Solve multi-step arithmetic problems using mental math and written algorithms
  4. Apply calculation strategies including estimation and checking results for plausibility

#### 2. divisibility-prime-numbers
- **Title (DE)**: Teilbarkeit und Primzahlen
- **Title (EN)**: Divisibility and Prime Numbers
- **Scope**: Understanding divisibility rules (for 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, 10), distinguishing between prime and composite numbers, factorization into prime factors, and determination of greatest common divisor (ggT) and least common multiple (kgV). Connects arithmetic with algebraic thinking patterns.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Apply divisibility rules to determine whether numbers are divisible by 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 9, or 10
  2. Identify and generate prime numbers; factorize composite numbers into prime factors
  3. Determine the greatest common divisor and least common multiple of numbers
  4. Recognize and describe patterns in number sequences

#### 3. geometric-shapes-2d
- **Title (DE)**: Ebene geometrische Figuren
- **Title (EN)**: Two-Dimensional Geometric Shapes
- **Scope**: Properties and classification of basic 2D shapes (triangles, quadrilaterals, circles), angle concepts, symmetry (axis and point symmetry), parallel and perpendicular lines. Introduction to geometric constructions using compass and ruler.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Identify, describe, and classify geometric figures based on their properties
  2. Recognize and construct axis and point symmetry in figures
  3. Use geometric tools (compass, ruler, protractor) for basic constructions
  4. Measure, estimate, and draw angles up to 360°

#### 4. area-perimeter
- **Title (DE)**: Flächeninhalt und Umfang
- **Title (EN)**: Area and Perimeter
- **Scope**: Calculating perimeter and area of rectangles and squares, understanding the relationship between units, converting between area units (mm², cm², m², km²), solving practical problems involving area and perimeter. Introduction to area concepts through visual models.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Calculate perimeter and area of rectangles and squares using formulas
  2. Convert between different area units (mm², cm², m², km²)
  3. Solve real-world problems involving area and perimeter calculations
  4. Estimate areas of irregular shapes and verify calculations

#### 5. volume-capacity
- **Title (DE)**: Volumen und Rauminhalt
- **Title (EN)**: Volume and Capacity
- **Scope**: Introduction to 3D geometric solids (cube, cuboid), calculating volume of cuboids, understanding the relationship between volume and capacity units (mm³, cm³, m³, mL, L), solving practical problems involving volume. Introduction to surface area of cuboids.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Calculate volume of cubes and cuboids using the formula V = a × b × c
  2. Convert between volume units (mm³, cm³, m³) and capacity units (mL, L)
  3. Visualize and describe 3D solids using nets and perspective drawings
  4. Solve real-world problems involving volume and capacity

#### 6. measurement-units
- **Title (DE)**: Größen und Maßeinheiten
- **Title (EN)**: Quantities and Units of Measurement
- **Scope**: Working with different measurement systems including length (mm, cm, m, km), mass (g, kg, t), time (s, min, h, d), and money. Converting between units, estimating measurements, and solving practical problems involving multiple units and conversions.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Convert between units within each measurement system (length, mass, time, money)
  2. Estimate and measure quantities using appropriate tools and units
  3. Solve multi-step problems involving different measurement units
  4. Compare and order quantities expressed in different units

#### 7. coordinate-system-basics
- **Title (DE)**: Koordinatensystem und Lagebeziehungen
- **Title (EN)**: Coordinate System and Spatial Relationships
- **Scope**: Introduction to the Cartesian coordinate system in the first quadrant, plotting and reading points, describing positions and paths using coordinates, understanding spatial relationships (parallel, perpendicular, intersecting), distance calculations on grid systems.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Plot and read coordinates of points in the first quadrant of a coordinate system
  2. Describe positions and paths using coordinate notation
  3. Identify and describe spatial relationships between points and lines
  4. Calculate distances between points on a coordinate grid

#### 8. data-collection-charts
- **Title (DE)**: Datenerfassung und Diagramme
- **Title (EN)**: Data Collection and Charts
- **Scope**: Collecting, organizing, and representing data using tables, bar charts, pictographs, and line graphs. Reading and interpreting different types of charts, understanding scales, and making simple comparisons and conclusions from data displays.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Collect and organize data systematically using tables and lists
  2. Create and interpret bar charts, pictographs, and simple line graphs
  3. Read and compare data from different representations
  4. Draw simple conclusions from data displays and identify trends

#### 9. introduction-fractions
- **Title (DE)**: Einführung in Brüche
- **Title (EN)**: Introduction to Fractions
- **Scope**: Understanding fractions as parts of a whole and as numbers, representing fractions visually (area models, number line), comparing and ordering simple fractions, recognizing equivalent fractions through visual models, introduction to the concept of the unit fraction.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Understand and represent fractions as parts of a whole and as positions on a number line
  2. Compare and order simple fractions with like denominators
  3. Recognize equivalent fractions through visual models and simple patterns
  4. Use fractions to describe real-world situations and quantities

### Grade 6

#### 10. fraction-operations
- **Title (DE)**: Bruchrechnung
- **Title (EN)**: Fraction Operations
- **Scope**: Comprehensive work with fractions including expanding and reducing fractions, converting between improper fractions and mixed numbers, adding and subtracting fractions with unlike denominators, multiplying fractions, introduction to dividing fractions, solving problems with fractions.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Expand and reduce fractions to simplest form; convert between mixed numbers and improper fractions
  2. Add and subtract fractions with unlike denominators using least common multiples
  3. Multiply fractions and mixed numbers; understand division of fractions
  4. Solve multi-step problems involving fraction operations in real-world contexts

#### 11. decimal-numbers
- **Title (DE)**: Dezimalzahlen
- **Title (EN)**: Decimal Numbers
- **Scope**: Understanding decimal notation and place value, reading and writing decimal numbers, representing decimals on number lines, converting between fractions and decimals, ordering and comparing decimals, rounding decimals, operations with decimals (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division).
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Understand place value in decimal numbers; read, write, and compare decimals
  2. Convert between common fractions and decimal representations (e.g., 1/2 = 0.5, 1/4 = 0.25)
  3. Perform all four operations with decimal numbers
  4. Round decimal numbers appropriately and estimate results of decimal calculations

#### 12. percentages-basics
- **Title (DE)**: Einführung in Prozentrechnung
- **Title (EN)**: Introduction to Percentages
- **Scope**: Understanding percent as "per hundred," visual representations of percentages, converting between fractions, decimals, and percentages, calculating percentages of quantities, finding what percent one number is of another, solving basic percentage problems in everyday contexts (discounts, sales tax).
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Understand the concept of percent and convert between fractions, decimals, and percentages
  2. Calculate a given percentage of a quantity (finding the percentage value)
  3. Determine what percent one quantity is of another (finding the percentage rate)
  4. Solve real-world percentage problems including discounts, interest, and statistics

#### 13. ratios-proportions
- **Title (DE)**: Verhältnisse und Proportionen
- **Title (EN)**: Ratios and Proportions
- **Scope**: Understanding ratios as comparisons of quantities, writing and simplifying ratios, understanding proportions and proportional relationships, solving proportion problems using cross multiplication, applying ratios to scale drawings, mixtures, and recipes.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Express and simplify ratios; distinguish between part-to-part and part-to-whole ratios
  2. Recognize and describe proportional relationships
  3. Solve proportion problems using equivalent ratios and cross multiplication
  4. Apply ratios and proportions to real-world problems including scales, recipes, and mixtures

#### 14. triangles-quadrilaterals
- **Title (DE)**: Dreiecke und Vierecke
- **Title (EN)**: Triangles and Quadrilaterals
- **Scope**: Properties and classification of triangles (equilateral, isosceles, scalene; acute, right, obtuse) and quadrilaterals (parallelogram, rectangle, square, rhombus, trapezoid). Angle relationships, angle sums in polygons, congruence concepts, constructing triangles and quadrilaterals.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Classify and describe triangles and quadrilaterals based on sides and angles
  2. Apply the angle sum property: sum of angles in triangles (180°) and quadrilaterals (360°)
  3. Construct triangles using given measurements (SSS, SAS, ASA)
  4. Identify congruent figures and understand conditions for triangle congruence

#### 15. circle-geometry
- **Title (DE)**: Kreis und Kreisberechnungen
- **Title (EN)**: Circle Geometry
- **Scope**: Understanding circle terminology (center, radius, diameter, chord, arc, sector, segment), relationship between radius and diameter, calculating circumference and area of circles, recognizing and drawing circles with compass, positional relationships between circles and lines.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Identify and use circle terminology; understand relationship between radius and diameter
  2. Calculate circumference using C = π × d or C = 2 × π × r
  3. Calculate area of circles using A = π × r²
  4. Solve problems involving circles in real-world contexts (wheels, circular fields, etc.)

#### 16. area-complex-shapes
- **Title (DE)**: Flächenberechnung zusammengesetzter Figuren
- **Title (EN)**: Area of Composite Shapes
- **Scope**: Calculating areas of triangles using the formula A = (b × h) / 2, parallelograms, and trapezoids. Decomposing complex shapes into simpler components, calculating areas of composite figures, applying area formulas to solve real-world problems.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Apply area formulas for triangles, parallelograms, and trapezoids
  2. Decompose composite shapes into simpler shapes to calculate total area
  3. Solve multi-step problems involving areas of complex figures
  4. Verify area calculations using different decomposition strategies

#### 17. surface-area-volume
- **Title (DE)**: Oberfläche und Volumen von Körpern
- **Title (EN)**: Surface Area and Volume of Solids
- **Scope**: Calculating surface area and volume of prisms (especially cuboids), cylinders, introduction to nets of 3D solids, understanding the relationship between dimensions and volume/surface area, solving problems involving packaging, containers, and construction.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Calculate surface area of cuboids and prisms using nets and formulas
  2. Calculate volume of prisms using V = base area × height
  3. Calculate surface area and volume of cylinders
  4. Solve real-world problems involving surface area and volume

#### 18. probability-basics
- **Title (DE)**: Einführung in Wahrscheinlichkeit
- **Title (EN)**: Introduction to Probability
- **Scope**: Understanding probability as a measure of likelihood, probability scale from 0 to 1, experimental vs. theoretical probability, conducting simple probability experiments (coins, dice, spinners), recording outcomes, calculating simple probabilities for equally likely outcomes.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Understand probability as a number between 0 and 1 representing likelihood
  2. Conduct probability experiments and record outcomes systematically
  3. Calculate theoretical probabilities for simple events with equally likely outcomes
  4. Compare experimental and theoretical probabilities and explain differences

### Grade 7

#### 19. integers-negative-numbers
- **Title (DE)**: Ganze Zahlen und negative Zahlen
- **Title (EN)**: Integers and Negative Numbers
- **Scope**: Extending the number system to include negative numbers, representing integers on number lines, understanding absolute value and opposite numbers, comparing and ordering integers, operations with integers (addition, subtraction, multiplication, division), applying integers to real-world contexts (temperatures, elevations, bank accounts).
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Represent, compare, and order integers on a number line
  2. Understand absolute value as distance from zero and identify opposite numbers
  3. Perform all four operations with integers applying sign rules correctly
  4. Apply integers to solve real-world problems involving contexts with negative quantities

#### 20. rational-numbers
- **Title (DE)**: Rationale Zahlen
- **Title (EN)**: Rational Numbers
- **Scope**: Understanding the rational number system (ℚ) as including all integers, fractions, and finite/periodic decimals. Operating with positive and negative fractions and decimals, ordering rational numbers, representing rational numbers on number lines, solving problems with rational numbers.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Understand rational numbers as the set ℚ including integers, fractions, and decimals
  2. Perform operations with positive and negative fractions and decimals
  3. Convert flexibly between fraction, decimal, and percent representations of rational numbers
  4. Solve complex problems involving rational numbers in various forms

#### 21. terms-algebraic-expressions
- **Title (DE)**: Terme und algebraische Ausdrücke
- **Title (EN)**: Terms and Algebraic Expressions
- **Scope**: Introduction to algebraic thinking, using variables to represent unknown quantities, writing and interpreting algebraic terms, simplifying terms by combining like terms, distributive property, expanding and factoring simple expressions, evaluating algebraic expressions.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Understand and use variables to represent unknown or changing quantities
  2. Simplify algebraic expressions by combining like terms
  3. Apply the distributive property to expand and factor expressions
  4. Evaluate algebraic expressions by substituting given values for variables

#### 22. linear-equations
- **Title (DE)**: Lineare Gleichungen
- **Title (EN)**: Linear Equations
- **Scope**: Understanding equations as statements of equality, solving one-step and two-step linear equations, solving equations with variables on both sides, translating word problems into equations, checking solutions, understanding solution sets, applications of linear equations in real-world contexts.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Solve linear equations using inverse operations and equivalence transformations
  2. Translate word problems into algebraic equations
  3. Check solutions by substitution and identify equations with no solution or infinite solutions
  4. Apply equation-solving skills to real-world problems and contexts

#### 23. inequalities
- **Title (DE)**: Lineare Ungleichungen
- **Title (EN)**: Linear Inequalities
- **Scope**: Understanding inequalities and inequality notation (<, >, ≤, ≥), solving simple linear inequalities, representing solution sets on number lines, understanding that inequalities have infinitely many solutions, applying inequalities to real-world constraint problems.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Solve simple linear inequalities using inverse operations
  2. Represent solution sets of inequalities on number lines
  3. Understand the effect of multiplying or dividing by negative numbers on inequality direction
  4. Model real-world constraint situations using inequalities

#### 24. proportional-relationships
- **Title (DE)**: Proportionale und antiproportionale Zuordnungen
- **Title (EN)**: Proportional and Inversely Proportional Relationships
- **Scope**: Understanding direct proportionality (y = kx) and inverse proportionality (y = k/x), recognizing proportional relationships in tables, graphs, and equations, calculating the constant of proportionality, applying the rule of three (Dreisatz) for proportional and inversely proportional situations, solving real-world problems.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Recognize and describe proportional and inversely proportional relationships from tables, graphs, and contexts
  2. Determine the constant of proportionality and write equations for proportional relationships
  3. Apply the rule of three (Dreisatz) to solve proportional and inversely proportional problems
  4. Create and interpret graphs of proportional and inversely proportional relationships

#### 25. coordinate-system-advanced
- **Title (DE)**: Koordinatensystem und lineare Funktionen
- **Title (EN)**: Coordinate System and Linear Functions
- **Scope**: Extending the coordinate system to all four quadrants, plotting points with negative coordinates, graphing simple linear relationships, understanding slope and intercept informally, reading and creating graphs of real-world situations, distance calculations in the coordinate plane.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Plot and identify points in all four quadrants of the coordinate plane
  2. Graph linear relationships and interpret the meaning of slope and intercept in context
  3. Create graphs representing real-world linear relationships
  4. Calculate distances between points in the coordinate plane

#### 26. angle-relationships
- **Title (DE)**: Winkelbeziehungen
- **Title (EN)**: Angle Relationships
- **Scope**: Understanding angle types (acute, right, obtuse, straight, reflex), angle relationships (complementary, supplementary, vertical angles), angles formed by parallel lines and a transversal (corresponding, alternate interior, alternate exterior, co-interior angles), calculating unknown angles using angle relationships.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Identify and classify angles based on their measures
  2. Apply angle relationships (complementary, supplementary, vertical) to find unknown angles
  3. Recognize and use angle relationships formed by parallel lines cut by a transversal
  4. Solve multi-step problems involving multiple angle relationships

#### 27. congruence-similarity-intro
- **Title (DE)**: Kongruenz und Ähnlichkeit
- **Title (EN)**: Congruence and Similarity Introduction
- **Scope**: Understanding congruence as same size and shape, conditions for triangle congruence (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS), introduction to similarity as same shape but different size, scale factors, solving problems involving congruent and similar figures, applications to maps and scale drawings.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Understand and apply conditions for triangle congruence (SSS, SAS, ASA, AAS)
  2. Identify congruent and similar figures and explain the relationship
  3. Determine scale factors in similar figures and calculate unknown lengths
  4. Apply congruence and similarity to solve problems involving maps and scale drawings

#### 28. statistics-interpretation
- **Title (DE)**: Datenauswertung und Statistik
- **Title (EN)**: Data Analysis and Statistics
- **Scope**: Collecting and organizing larger data sets, calculating measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode), understanding range as a measure of spread, creating and interpreting various charts (histograms, stem-and-leaf plots, box plots introduction), comparing data sets, identifying misleading representations of data.
- **Learning Objectives**:
  1. Calculate and interpret measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode) and range
  2. Create and interpret various data displays including histograms and stem-and-leaf plots
  3. Compare data sets using statistical measures and graphical representations
  4. Identify and explain misleading uses of statistics and data visualization

## Notes on Curriculum Alignment

### KMK Leitideen Coverage

This concept list provides comprehensive coverage of all five KMK Leitideen:

- **L1: Zahl (Number)**: Concepts 1, 2, 9, 10, 11, 12, 13, 19, 20
- **L2: Raum und Form (Space and Form)**: Concepts 3, 14, 15, 16, 17, 26, 27
- **L3: Muster, Strukturen und funktionale Zusammenhänge (Patterns, Structures and Functional Relationships)**: Concepts 2, 21, 22, 23, 24, 25
- **L4: Größen und Messen (Quantities and Measurement)**: Concepts 4, 5, 6, 16, 17
- **L5: Daten und Zufall (Data and Chance)**: Concepts 8, 18, 28

### Berlin-Brandenburg Niveaustufen

The concepts align with Berlin-Brandenburg Rahmenlehrplan competency levels:
- **Grade 5**: Primarily Niveaustufe C-D
- **Grade 6**: Primarily Niveaustufe D
- **Grade 7**: Niveaustufe D-E

### Pedagogical Progression

The concept sequence follows standard German mathematics curriculum progression:
1. **Grade 5**: Foundation in natural numbers, basic geometry, introduction to measurement and data
2. **Grade 6**: Extension to fractions, decimals, percentages, and more complex geometry
3. **Grade 7**: Introduction to algebra, negative numbers, rational numbers, and functional relationships

### Granularity Decisions

To meet the 24-30 concept constraint, related topics were consolidated:
- Fractions and operations combined into single comprehensive concepts
- Measurement types grouped by similar cognitive demands
- Geometry topics organized by dimensional complexity (2D shapes, then 3D solids)
- Algebraic topics progressed from expressions to equations to functions

This ensures manageable scope while maintaining comprehensive curriculum coverage aligned with official KMK Bildungsstandards for Sekundarstufe I.
