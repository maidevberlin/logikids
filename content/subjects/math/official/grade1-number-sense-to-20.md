---
id: grade1-number-sense-to-20
name: Number Sense to 20
description: Counting, reading, writing, comparing, and ordering numbers from 0 to 20; understanding quantity and number relationships
grade: 1
focus: Number sense and place value
difficulty: easy
learning_objectives:
  - Count forward and backward within 20
  - Read and write numerals from 0 to 20
  - Compare numbers using greater than, less than, and equal to
  - Order a set of numbers from smallest to largest or largest to smallest
  - Understand the relationship between numbers and quantities
  - Recognize and identify numbers in different contexts
  - Identify the number that comes before, after, or between given numbers
problem_types:
  - Counting sequence
  - Number comparison
  - Number ordering
  - Number identification
  - Before/after/between
  - Quantity matching
  - Number line placement
  - Skip counting
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Numbers 0-10 only
    - Simple counting forward or comparing two adjacent numbers
    - Visual quantity support acceptable
  medium:
    - Full range 0-20
    - Counting backward or comparing non-adjacent numbers
    - Ordering 3-4 numbers
  hard:
    - Skip counting by 2s or 5s within 20
    - Complex ordering of 5+ numbers
    - Multiple comparison steps or number line reasoning
real_world_context:
  - Counting children in the classroom or family members at home
  - Reading house numbers, bus numbers, or page numbers in books
  - Comparing ages of friends or siblings
  - Organizing items by quantity, such as pencils in a case or toys on a shelf
  - Using number cards or dice in games
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid numbers larger than 20
  - Don't introduce place value terminology (tens/ones) yet
  - Avoid negative numbers or zero as a subtraction result
  - Don't use fraction or decimal notation
  - Avoid abstract contexts unfamiliar to 6-7 year olds
version: 1
version_notes: Initial version based on KMK Bildungsstandards for Grade 1
---
