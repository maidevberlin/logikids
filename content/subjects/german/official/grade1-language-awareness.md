---
id: grade1-language-awareness
name: Language Awareness & Multilingualism
description: Understanding communication purposes, recognizing dialects, comparing languages, and appreciating linguistic diversity
grade: 1
focus: Metacommunication and language comparison
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Identify different communication purposes from everyday examples
  - Recognize forms of successful and unsuccessful communication
  - Describe differences between everyday language and educational language
  - Examine dialectal variations and regional speech differences
  - Compare German with other languages and writing systems
  - Appreciate linguistic diversity in the classroom and community
  - Understand when language helps or hinders mutual understanding
problem_types:
  - Communication purpose identification
  - Dialect comparison
  - Language system comparison
  - Multilingual word recognition
  - Respectful vs. insulting expression
  - Register appropriateness
  - Writing system exploration
  - Language diversity appreciation
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single communication example with clear purpose (greeting, request, thanks)
    - Common everyday vs. school language distinctions
    - Recognizing familiar dialectal words or simple foreign language greetings
  medium:
    - Comparing two communication situations for effectiveness
    - Identifying when dialect or everyday language fits vs. school context
    - Matching simple words across 2-3 languages
  hard:
    - Evaluating communication success across multiple contexts
    - Explaining why certain language forms help or hinder understanding
    - Comparing writing systems or grammatical features across languages
real_world_context:
  - Noticing classmates who speak different languages at home
  - Hearing grandparents use dialectal words that differ from standard German
  - Understanding when to use polite vs. casual language with teachers and friends
  - Recognizing that "Hallo" in German is "Hello" in English and "Hola" in Spanish
  - Appreciating that some friends write their names in different alphabets
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid implying any language or dialect is "wrong" or inferior
  - Don't use complex linguistic terminology beyond "dialect" and "language"
  - Avoid examples requiring reading fluency in multiple writing systems
  - Don't expect metalinguistic analysis beyond simple comparisons
  - Avoid stereotyping languages or cultures
version: 1
version_notes: Initial version based on LehrplanPLUS Bayern Grade 1 language awareness standards (4.1-4.2)
