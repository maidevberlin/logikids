---
id: grade2-asking-questions
name: Asking Questions
description: Forming questions, using question words, and punctuating questions correctly
grade: 2
focus: Question formation and communication skills
difficulty: medium
learning_objectives:
  - Form complete questions using proper word order in German
  - Use question words (Wer, Was, Wann, Wo, Wie, Warum) appropriately
  - Recognize when a sentence is a question versus a statement
  - Punctuate questions correctly with question marks
  - Ask clarifying questions to understand information better
problem_types:
  - Question word selection
  - Question mark placement
  - Reordering words to form questions
  - Statement to question conversion
  - Question word matching
  - Question or statement identification
  - Complete the question
  - Question-answer matching
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Use simple present tense only
    - Limit to 4-6 words per question
    - Focus on common question words (Wer, Was, Wo)
  medium:
    - Include all six question words
    - Use 5-8 words per question
    - Mix different verb forms (ist, hat, macht)
  hard:
    - Combine multiple concepts (question words + verb conjugation)
    - Use 7-10 words per question
    - Include less common verbs and contexts
real_world_context:
  - Children ask questions to understand instructions in school
  - Students use questions to get information from teachers and classmates
  - Questions help children learn about new topics and clarify confusion
  - Asking questions is essential for conversations with friends and family
  - Children use questions to express curiosity about the world around them
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid complex subordinate clauses or compound questions
  - Don't use question words that require abstract thinking (Weshalb, Wieso)
  - Avoid situations unfamiliar to 7-8 year olds
  - Don't mix formal and informal address (Sie/du) in the same task
  - Avoid questions requiring cultural knowledge beyond German basics
prerequisites: []
version: 1
version_notes: Initial concept for German Grade 2 question formation
