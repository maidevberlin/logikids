learning_objectives:
  - Write phonetically regular words through syllabic articulation
  - Identify syllable cores using vowels to spell correctly
  - Recognize and spell unstressed endings accurately
  - Distinguish short and long vowel qualities for correct spelling
  - Apply morphological principles by identifying word stems for umlauts and consonant shifts
  - Use noun identification strategies for capitalization
  - Spell high-frequency words correctly from memory
problem_types:
  - Sound-based spelling
  - Syllable division
  - Long vowel spelling
  - Double consonant spelling
  - Noun capitalization
  - Word stem derivation
  - Unstressed ending
  - High-frequency word
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Phonetically regular CVC words (Mama, Papa, Hut)
    - Single-syllable words with clear vowel sounds
    - Common sight words (der, die, das, ist)
  medium:
    - Two-syllable words with clear syllable boundaries
    - Words with long vowel markers (ie, ah, oh, eh)
    - Simple compound words (Schulhof, Pausenbrot)
  hard:
    - Words requiring morphological derivation (Haus-HÃ¤user)
    - Three-syllable words with unstressed endings

  - Writing shopping lists or birthday cards for family members
  - Labeling personal belongings and classroom items
  - Creating signs and posters for school projects
  - Writing messages or notes to friends and teachers
  - Keeping a simple diary or learning journal

  - Avoid words with silent letters or irregular spellings
  - Don't use vocabulary beyond typical first-grade experience
