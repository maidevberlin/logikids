---
id: grade1-dramatic-expression
name: Dramatic Expression & Role Play
description: Express emotions and characters through gesture, movement, facial expressions, and role-playing activities
grade: 1
focus: Dramatic play and non-verbal communication
difficulty: easy
learning_objectives:
  - Identify and describe character traits from stories or scenarios
  - Express emotions through facial expressions and body language
  - Use gestures and movement to convey meaning without words
  - Engage in role-playing using puppets or personal performance
  - Observe and appreciate dramatic performances by peers
  - Respond to stories through physical and dramatic expression
problem_types:
  - Character portrayal
  - Emotion recognition
  - Gesture matching
  - Movement expression
  - Puppet dialogue
  - Role-play scenario
  - Physical storytelling
difficulty_guidelines:
  easy:
    - Single basic emotions (happy, sad, angry, surprised)
    - Familiar everyday characters (parent, teacher, animal)
    - Simple one-action movements (waving, jumping, sleeping)
  medium:
    - Combined emotions or emotion transitions (excited then tired)
    - Story characters with distinct personality traits
    - Multi-step movement sequences (walking then stopping then turning)
  hard:
    - Subtle emotional nuances (shy, proud, worried, relieved)
    - Abstract characters requiring interpretation (brave knight, wise owl)
    - Complex movement patterns expressing story progression
prerequisites: []
real_world_context:
  - Acting out characters while playing pretend games with friends
  - Using hand puppets or stuffed animals to tell stories at home
  - Showing emotions through face and body when words are difficult
  - Participating in class plays or performance activities at school
  - Imitating animals, people, or characters from favorite stories
anti_patterns:
  - Avoid requiring reading skills beyond simple character names
  - Don't use abstract emotions beyond 6-7 year old experience
  - Avoid scenarios requiring cultural knowledge unfamiliar to young children
  - Don't expect complex multi-character interactions at this level
  - Avoid movements requiring advanced motor coordination
version: 1
version_notes: Initial concept for German Grade 1 dramatic expression based on LehrplanPLUS Bayern
